# RQ-25-10-001: 120-Passenger Cabin Configuration Requirements

**Requirement ID:** RQ-25-10-001  
**Title:** Q100 120-Passenger Cabin Configuration Requirements  
**ATA Chapter:** 25-10-00 (Cabin Configuration)  
**Project:** AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E Q100 INTEGRA  
**Status:** Approved  
**Priority:** High  
**Date:** 2025-11-10

---

## 1. Functional Requirements

### FR-001: Passenger Capacity
**Requirement:** The cabin configuration shall accommodate 120 passengers in a single-class layout.

**Rationale:** Mid-sized BWB optimized for regional and short-haul operations.

**Verification Method:** Design review and cabin layout documentation.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied (18 rows: 12×7 + 6×6 = 120)

---

### FR-002: Seat Configuration
**Requirement:** The cabin shall utilize mixed seating configurations:
- Rows 1-12: 2-3-2 configuration (7 seats per row)
- Rows 13-18: 2-2-2 configuration (6 seats per row)

**Rationale:** Optimize cabin width utilization while maintaining passenger comfort.

**Verification Method:** Cabin layout drawings and 3D model review.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied

---

### FR-003: Seat Pitch
**Requirement:** All passenger seats shall have a minimum pitch of 780 mm (30.7 inches).

**Rationale:** Provide economy comfort standard exceeding minimum requirements.

**Verification Method:** Physical measurement and layout verification.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied

---

### FR-004: Seat Width
**Requirement:** All passenger seats shall have a minimum width of 450 mm (17.7 inches).

**Rationale:** Match wide-body comfort standards.

**Verification Method:** Seat manufacturer specifications and testing.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied (subject to final seat selection)

---

### FR-005: Aisle Configuration
**Requirement:** The cabin shall provide dual aisles with minimum width of 500 mm each.

**Rationale:** Enable efficient passenger flow and meet emergency egress requirements.

**Verification Method:** Layout measurements and evacuation analysis.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied  
**Recommendation:** Consider upgrade to 600 mm per aisle for industry best practice.

---

## 2. Galley Requirements

### FR-006: Forward Galley
**Requirement:** A forward galley shall be provided with dimensions 4,550 × 2,200 × 2,000 mm (W×L×H).

**Rationale:** Support full meal service capability for 120 passengers.

**Verification Method:** Equipment fit check and service workflow analysis.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied

---

### FR-007: Aft Galley
**Requirement:** An aft galley shall be provided with dimensions 4,550 × 1,800 × 2,000 mm (W×L×H).

**Rationale:** Enable split-cabin service operations and beverage service.

**Verification Method:** Equipment fit check and service workflow analysis.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied

---

## 3. Lavatory Requirements

### FR-008: Lavatory Quantity
**Requirement:** The cabin shall provide a minimum of 4 lavatories.

**Rationale:** Industry standard of 1 lavatory per 30 passengers.

**Verification Method:** Layout verification and operational analysis.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied (4 lavatories provided)

---

### FR-009: Lavatory Dimensions
**Requirement:** Standard lavatories shall have minimum footprint of 900 × 1,200 mm each.

**Rationale:** Meet minimum passenger comfort and operational requirements.

**Verification Method:** Layout drawings and physical mockup.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied

---

### FR-010: Accessible Lavatory
**Requirement:** At least one lavatory shall meet accessibility requirements per CS-25.803.

**Rationale:** Regulatory compliance for passengers with reduced mobility.

**Verification Method:** Certification compliance review.

**Status:** ⚠ TBD - Design in progress

---

## 4. Emergency Exit Requirements

### FR-011: Emergency Exit Count
**Requirement:** The cabin shall provide 8 emergency exits (4 port, 4 starboard).

**Rationale:** Meet CS-25.807 requirements with safety margin for 120 passengers.

**Verification Method:** Regulatory compliance analysis and evacuation testing.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied (50% safety margin)

---

### FR-012: Exit Dimensions
**Requirement:** Each emergency exit shall have minimum clear opening of 800 × 1,200 mm.

**Rationale:** Type A or Type I exit requirements for passenger capacity.

**Verification Method:** Physical measurement and certification testing.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied

---

### FR-013: Evacuation Capability
**Requirement:** The cabin configuration shall enable 90-second evacuation of all passengers.

**Rationale:** CS-25.803 regulatory requirement.

**Verification Method:** Evacuation demonstration test with flight attendants and volunteers.

**Status:** ⚠ TBD - Testing scheduled during certification phase

---

## 5. Stowage Requirements

### FR-014: Overhead Bin Depth
**Requirement:** Overhead bins shall provide minimum 500 mm depth.

**Rationale:** Accommodate standard roller bags and carry-on luggage.

**Verification Method:** Physical measurement and luggage fit testing.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied

---

### FR-015: Overhead Bin Volume
**Requirement:** Total overhead bin volume shall be 6,000-8,400 liters.

**Rationale:** Provide 50-70 liters per passenger stowage capacity.

**Verification Method:** Volume calculation and CAD model analysis.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied (~7,200 L achieved)

---

### FR-016: Bin Opening Height
**Requirement:** Overhead bin opening shall provide 350-380 mm height clearance.

**Rationale:** Enable easy loading/unloading of carry-on items.

**Verification Method:** Physical measurement and ergonomic analysis.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied

---

## 6. Dimensional Requirements

### FR-017: Cabin Length
**Requirement:** Total cabin length including galleys and vestibules shall not exceed 19,500 mm.

**Rationale:** Fit within BWB fuselage structural limits.

**Verification Method:** 3D model integration and structural analysis.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied (~19,240 mm estimated)

---

### FR-018: Cabin Width
**Requirement:** Central usable cabin width shall be approximately 4,550 mm in midsection.

**Rationale:** Optimize BWB width utilization for dual-aisle configuration.

**Verification Method:** Structural layout and systems integration analysis.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied

---

### FR-019: Cabin Height
**Requirement:** Minimum cabin height shall be 2,000 mm at centerline.

**Rationale:** Adequate headroom for passengers and crew movement.

**Verification Method:** Cross-section analysis and clearance verification.

**Status:** ✓ Satisfied (2,200 mm provided)

---

## 7. Regulatory Compliance Requirements

### FR-020: CS-25 Compliance
**Requirement:** All cabin furnishings shall comply with CS-25 certification specifications.

**Rationale:** Airworthiness certification requirement.

**Verification Method:** Compliance matrix and certification test program.

**Status:** ⚠ In Progress - Certification activities ongoing

**Specific Requirements:**
- CS-25.787: Stowage compartments
- CS-25.803: Emergency evacuation
- CS-25.807: Emergency exits
- CS-25.815: Width of aisle
- CS-25.853: Compartment interiors (flammability)
- CS-25.855: Cargo and baggage compartments

**Verification Method:** Material testing per applicable standards.

## 13. Open Issues and Risks

**Description:** Current design uses 500 mm aisles; industry best practice is 600 mm.  
**Impact:** Potential operational efficiency and passenger comfort concern.  
**Mitigation:** Evaluate feasibility of 600 mm aisles in next design iteration.  
**Owner:** Cabin Design Lead  
**Target Resolution:** Q1 2026

### Issue #2: Accessible Lavatory Design
**Description:** Accessible lavatory design not yet finalized.  
**Impact:** Regulatory certification requirement.  
**Mitigation:** Prioritize accessible lavatory design completion.  
**Owner:** Cabin Design Lead  
**Target Resolution:** Q4 2025

**Description:** Final seat manufacturer and model not selected.  
**Impact:** Weight, dimensions, and certification testing schedule.

**Owner:** Procurement Team  
**Target Resolution:** Q4 2025
