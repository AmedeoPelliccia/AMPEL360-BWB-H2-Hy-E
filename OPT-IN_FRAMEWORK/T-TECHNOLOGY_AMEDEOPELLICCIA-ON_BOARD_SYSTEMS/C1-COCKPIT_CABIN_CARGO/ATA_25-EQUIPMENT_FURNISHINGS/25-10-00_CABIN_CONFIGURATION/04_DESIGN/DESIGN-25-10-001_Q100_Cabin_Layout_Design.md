## 1. Design Overview

### 1.1 Purpose
This document provides detailed cabin layout design for the AMPEL360 Q100 120-passenger configuration, including dimensional analysis, area calculations, and integration considerations.

### 1.2 Design Philosophy
- Maximize passenger comfort within BWB constraints
- Optimize space utilization for 120-passenger capacity
- Enable efficient operations and maintenance
- Comply with all safety and certification requirements
- Leverage BWB unique advantages

## 5. Emergency Exit Design

### 5.1 Exit Configuration

**Forward Exits (Main Doors):**
- Quantity: 2 per side (4 total)
- Type: Type A passenger entry doors
- Clear opening: 1,070 × 1,830 mm (42" × 72")
- Location: Forward of Row 1
- Function: Primary boarding/deplaning and emergency egress

**Mid-Cabin Exits:**
- Quantity: 2 per side (4 total)
- Type: Type III or Type A over-wing exits
- Clear opening: 800 × 1,200 mm minimum
- Location: Between Rows 9-10 (seating section transition)
- Function: Emergency egress only

**Aft Exits:**
- Quantity: Integrated with main doors if configuration allows
- Alternative: Dedicated aft service doors with emergency capability

### 5.2 Evacuation Analysis

**Regulatory Requirement:** CS-25.807
- 120 passengers require adequate exits for 90-second evacuation
- Type A exit capacity: 110 passengers per pair (both sides)
- Type III exit capacity: 35 passengers per pair

**Configuration Analysis:**

```
Exit Capacity Calculation:
├─ Forward Type A doors: 2 × 110 = 220 passengers (both sides)
├─ Mid-cabin exits: 4 × 35 = 140 passengers (if Type III)
└─ Total theoretical capacity: 360 passengers

Actual Configuration:
├─ 120 passengers
├─ Primary exits: Forward Type A doors (220 pax capacity)
└─ Safety margin: (220 - 120) / 120 = +83% ✓

With mid-cabin exits:
└─ Total capacity: 360 passengers
└─ Safety margin: (360 - 120) / 120 = +200% ✓✓
```

**Conclusion:** Configuration significantly exceeds minimum requirements.

### 5.3 Exit Spacing

**Longitudinal Distribution:**
```
Forward Exits:    @ Station 0 (forward pressure bulkhead)
Mid-cabin Exits:  @ Station 9,500 mm (between rows 9-10)
Aft Exits:        @ Station 19,000 mm (aft pressure bulkhead)

Maximum distance between exits: ~9,500 mm
CS-25.807 requirement: Adequate spacing for evacuation ✓
```

## 6. Overhead Bin Design

### 6.1 Bin Configuration

**Forward/Mid Section (Rows 1-12):**
- Rows: 12
- Bin pairs: 24 (port and starboard)
- Length per bin: Variable (600-800 mm typical)
- Total bins: 24 units

**Aft Section (Rows 13-18):**
- Rows: 6
- Bin pairs: 12 (port and starboard)
- Length per bin: Variable (600-800 mm typical)
- Total bins: 12 units

**Total Configuration:** 36 overhead bins

### 6.2 Volume Calculations

**Per-Bin Specifications:**
- Depth: 500 mm
- Height (opening): 350-380 mm
- Internal height: 400-450 mm
- Width: Approximately 700 mm average

**Volume per bin:**
- V = 700 mm × 500 mm × 400 mm = 140,000 mm³ = 140 L

**Total System Volume:**
- Forward/Mid section: 24 bins × 140 L = 3,360 L
- Aft section: 12 bins × 140 L = 1,680 L
- Additional undercut bins: ~2,160 L (estimated)
- **Total: ~7,200 L**

**Per-Passenger Allocation:**
- 7,200 L ÷ 120 passengers = 60 L per passenger ✓

**Standard Bag Fit:**
- IATA cabin bag: 56 × 35 × 23 cm = 45 L
- Overhead bin capacity: 140 L per bin
- Bags per bin: ~3 bags (allowing for shape inefficiency)

### 6.3 Structural Considerations

**Load Requirements:**
- 16g ultimate load per CS-25.787
- Bag weight: ~10 kg average
- Load per bin: 30 kg (3 bags) × 16g = 480 kg ultimate
- Bin structure: Carbon fiber/aluminum composite
- Attachment: Multiple hard points to overhead structure

## 11. Integration Considerations

### 11.1 Structural Integration
- Floor beams aligned with seat tracks
- Overhead bin attachment to fuselage frames
- Galley equipment load paths to structural hard points
- Door opening reinforcements

### 11.2 Systems Integration
- ECS distribution along ceiling
- Electrical power routing under floor and overhead
- Plumbing for galleys and lavatories (grey/black water)
- Lighting and PSU wiring in overhead
- Hydrogen system separation from cabin pressure vessel

### 11.3 Manufacturing Considerations
- Modular galley design for installation flexibility
- Removable overhead bins for access
- Replaceable floor panels for maintenance
- Standardized seat track spacing (industry standard)
