## 2. CREW FACTORS

### 2.1 Cabin Crew Physical Requirements

| Requirement | Specification | Rationale |
|------------|---------------|-----------|
| Handle force | <50 lbf (222 N) | 5th percentile female |
| Handle height | 48-60 inches | Reach zone |
| Visual indication | >20/40 vision | Standard requirement |
| Reaction time | <2 seconds | Emergency response |
| Sustained effort | 3 minutes | Extended emergency |

### 2.2 Crew Mental Workload

#### Normal Evacuation
- **Task complexity:** Moderate
- **Time pressure:** High
- **Stress level:** High
- **Decision points:** 3-5
- **Concurrent tasks:** 2-3

#### Degraded Conditions
- **Task complexity:** High
- **Time pressure:** Extreme
- **Stress level:** Extreme
- **Decision points:** 5-10
- **Concurrent tasks:** 4-6

### 2.4 Crew Interface Design

#### Handle Design
```
Handle Specifications:
├─ Grip diameter: 1.5 inches (optimal)
├─ Pull direction: Straight out
├─ Pull distance: 6 inches
├─ Force required: 30-40 lbf
├─ Color: RED with white stripe
└─ Marking: "PULL TO OPEN"
```

#### Visual Feedback
- **Armed indicator:** Green light + placard
- **Locked indicator:** Red light
- **Power status:** Amber light
- **Fault warning:** Flashing red

#### Tactile Feedback
- Handle detent at start
- Resistance increase during pull
- Positive click at end
- Vibration from power assist

## 3. PASSENGER FACTORS

### 3.1 Passenger Demographics

| Category | Percentage | Considerations |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| Adults 18-60 | 60% | Standard capability |
| Elderly >60 | 15% | Reduced mobility/speed |
| Children 2-12 | 15% | Need supervision |
| Disabled | 5% | Special assistance |
| Infants <2 | 5% | Carried by adults |

### 3.2 Passenger Behavior Models

#### Cooperative Behavior (60%)
- Follow crew instructions
- Move in orderly fashion
- Help others when asked
- Wait turn at door

#### Competitive Behavior (25%)
- Push forward aggressively
- Ignore crew commands
- Block aisles
- Cut in line

#### Passive Behavior (15%)
- Freeze in place
- Require physical guidance
- Slow to react
- Need repeated commands

### 3.3 Panic Response

#### Early Stage (0-30 sec)
- Increased movement speed
- Reduced spatial awareness
- Tunnel vision
- Elevated voice levels

#### Peak Panic (30-60 sec)
- Irrational behavior
- Physical aggression
- Crushing at exits
- Disregard for safety

#### Resolution (>60 sec)
- Exhaustion sets in
- More compliant
- Accept crew control
- Follow instructions

### 3.4 Passenger Capabilities

| Slide speed | 8 ft/s | 5 ft/s | 12 ft/s |
| Decision time | 2 sec | 1 sec | 5 sec |
| Overhead reach | 80 in | 65 in | 90 in |

## 4. STRESS FACTORS

### 4.1 Environmental Stressors

| Stressor | Effect | Mitigation |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Smoke | Visibility reduced 90% | Floor lighting |
| Heat | Cognitive impairment | Rapid evacuation |
| Noise | Communication difficult | Visual cues |
| Darkness | Disorientation | Photoluminescent |
| Motion | Balance problems | Handholds |

### 4.2 Psychological Stressors

| Stressor | Impact | Mitigation |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Time pressure | Hasty decisions | Clear procedures |
| Uncertainty | Hesitation | Positive commands |
| Fear | Panic behavior | Crew confidence |
| Confined space | Claustrophobia | Wide aisles |
| Social pressure | Groupthink | Individual guidance |

## 5. AGE AND ABILITY CONSIDERATIONS

### 5.1 Elderly Passengers (>60 years)

**Characteristics:**
- Reduced mobility (30% slower)
- Vision/hearing impairment
- Balance issues
- Cognitive processing slower
- Medication effects

**Design Accommodations:**
- Lower handle force
- Clear visual marking
- Handholds at door
- Crew assistance priority
- Additional time allowance

### 5.2 Children

**Characteristics:**
- Height limitations
- Limited strength
- Fear and confusion
- Separation anxiety
- Impulsive behavior

**Design Accommodations:**
- Parent/crew carry infants
- Clear, simple markings
- Child-friendly lighting
- Crew guidance priority
- Slide safety features

### 5.3 Disabled Passengers

**Mobility Impaired:**
- Wheelchair users
- Crutch/cane users
- Limited strength

**Sensory Impaired:**
- Visual impairment
- Hearing impairment

**Cognitive Impaired:**
- Reduced comprehension
- Slower processing

**Accommodations:**
- Crew assistance plan
- Priority seating near exit
- Special briefing
- Extra time allowance
- Alternative egress if needed

## 12. RECOMMENDATIONS

### 12.1 Design Recommendations
1. Reduce handle force to 35 lbf max
2. Add tactile guide on handle
3. Enhance visual contrast
4. Simplify backup procedure
5. Improve slide entry guidance

### 12.2 Procedure Recommendations
1. Standardize crew commands
2. Add decision flowcharts
3. Enhance training scenarios
4. Include stress management
5. Practice degraded modes

### 12.3 Training Recommendations
1. Increase simulation time
2. Add stress inoculation
3. Practice elderly assistance
4. Multi-language scenarios
5. Annual full-scale drill

## 13. CONCLUSION

This Human Factors Analysis demonstrates that the L3 Aft Emergency Exit design addresses the physical and cognitive capabilities of both crew and passengers. Key success factors include intuitive operation, clear feedback, and robust training programs.

**Critical Items:**
1. Handle force within crew capability
2. Clear visual/tactile feedback
3. Comprehensive crew training
4. Passenger flow management
5. Stress-resistant procedures
