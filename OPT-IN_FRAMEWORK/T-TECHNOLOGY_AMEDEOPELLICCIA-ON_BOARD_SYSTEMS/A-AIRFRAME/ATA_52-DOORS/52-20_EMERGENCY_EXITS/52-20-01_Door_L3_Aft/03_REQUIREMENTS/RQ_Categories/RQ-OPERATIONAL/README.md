# RQ-OPERATIONAL - Operational Requirements

**Category:** RQ-OPERATIONAL  
**ID Range:** RQ-52-20-01-140 to RQ-52-20-01-149

**Priority:** High

## Overview

This category contains all operational requirements for the Door L3 Aft Emergency Exit, including crew procedures, training requirements, and ground handling operations.

## Operational Design Philosophy

Operations must be:
- Intuitive for crew with minimal training
- Standardized across aircraft fleet
- Safe in all operational scenarios
- Compatible with airline procedures
- Efficient for ground operations

## Standard Operating Procedures

### Pre-Flight Check
1. Visual inspection of door exterior
2. Check armed/disarmed indicator
3. Verify seal condition
4. Test emergency lighting
5. Check slide pack pressure gauge
6. Document check in aircraft log

### Arming for Flight
1. Ensure door fully closed and locked
2. Move arming lever to ARMED position
3. Verify armed indicator illuminates
4. Cross-check from adjacent door
5. Report to flight deck

### Disarming After Landing
1. Aircraft stopped and parking brake set
2. Move arming lever to DISARMED position
3. Verify disarmed indicator shows
4. Ensure slide will not deploy
5. Report to flight deck

### Normal Door Opening
1. Verify door is disarmed
2. Verify no pressure differential
3. Unlock door handle
4. Pull handle and swing door open
5. Secure door in open position

### Emergency Door Opening
1. Assess situation (fire, smoke, water)
2. Command passengers to move back
3. Pull emergency handle firmly
4. Door opens automatically
5. Assist passengers to slide
6. Jump on slide when cabin clear

## Training Requirements

### Initial Qualification
- **Duration**: 8 hours classroom + 4 hours practical
- **Topics**:
  - Door design and operation
  - Normal procedures
  - Emergency procedures
  - Malfunction recognition
  - Safety considerations
- **Evaluation**: Written test + practical demonstration
- **Recurrency**: Annual

### Recurrent Training
- **Duration**: 2 hours annually
- **Topics**:
  - Procedure review
  - Recent incidents/lessons learned
  - Emergency scenario practice
  - Ditching procedures
- **Evaluation**: Practical demonstration

### Emergency Evacuation Drills
- **Frequency**: Twice per year
- **Scenarios**:
  - Fire evacuation
  - Water ditching
  - Night/low visibility
  - Post-crash scenario
- **Participants**: All cabin crew

## Ground Operations

### Servicing Access
- Door can be opened from ground level
- No special equipment required
- Standard aircraft ground power compatible
- External handle clearly marked

### Catering and Cleaning
- Door remains disarmed during servicing
- Visual confirmation of disarm status
- Ground safety pins available if needed
- Re-arm before flight only

### Slide Pack Replacement
- **Frequency**: Per maintenance schedule (typically annual)
- **Duration**: 2 hours
- **Personnel**: 2 certified technicians
- **Special Tools**: Slide pack hoist fixture
- **Verification**: Pressure check + functional test

## Operational Limitations

### Weather Restrictions
- No specific weather restrictions
- Operable in all conditions: rain, snow, ice, wind
- Crew awareness of slippery slide in wet conditions
- Heating elements prevent ice buildup

### MEL Dispatch
- **Category**: Essential equipment
- **MEL Relief**: None - must be operational
- **Placard**: "INOP" if door unusable
- **Alternate Exits**: Passenger load limited if exit unavailable

## Crew Workload Analysis

### Normal Operations
- **Pre-flight**: 2 minutes per door
- **Arming**: 15 seconds
- **Disarming**: 15 seconds
- **Post-flight check**: 1 minute
- **Cognitive Load**: Low

### Emergency Operations
- **Decision Time**: <3 seconds
- **Opening Time**: 8 seconds maximum
- **Passenger Commands**: Concurrent with opening
- **Total Evacuation**: 90 seconds for full aircraft
- **Cognitive Load**: High - training critical

## Operational Safety

### Error Prevention
- **Guard on emergency handle**: Prevents inadvertent activation
- **Armed indicator**: High visibility, dual indication
- **Arming lever**: Requires deliberate action
- **Placards**: Clear, multilingual

### Error Detection
- **Cross-check procedures**: Crew verify each other
- **Cockpit indication**: Door status displayed
- **Master warning**: If armed on ground
- **Pre-departure checks**: Final verification

## Passenger Communication

### Briefing Requirements
- Exit row passengers briefed on door operation
- Demonstration of handle operation
- Slide use instructions
- Emergency command words standardized

### Signage
- "EMERGENCY EXIT" in large letters
- Operating instructions on door
- Pictograms (universal symbols)
- Photoluminescent for visibility

## Night Operations

### Lighting
- Emergency lighting activates automatically
- Floor proximity lighting guides to exit
- Slide lighting built-in
- Crew flashlights as backup

### Procedures
- Same as day operations
- Emphasis on lighting verification
- Passenger guidance more critical
- Crew positioning for visibility

## Ditching Operations

### Preparation
- Crew brief on ditching exits
- Life vests donned by all
- Slide converts to raft mode
- Emergency equipment staged

### Execution
- Open door after impact
- Deploy slide/raft
- Board passengers onto raft
- Detach from aircraft
- Paddle away from aircraft

## International Operations

### Regulatory Compliance
- ICAO Annex 6 requirements
- FAA 14 CFR Part 121
- EASA CS-25 requirements
- National authority variations

### Multilingual Support
- Operating instructions in multiple languages
- Pictograms primary method
- Crew multilingual capability
- Passenger briefing cards translated
