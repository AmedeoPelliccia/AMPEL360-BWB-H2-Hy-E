# CFRP Pyrolysis Process
## Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer Recycling

### Overview
Pyrolysis is the primary method for recycling CFRP components from Door L1 Forward, recovering valuable carbon fibers while minimizing environmental impact.

### Process Description

#### 1. Pre-Treatment
- Remove metallic inserts and fasteners
- Cut panels to manageable sizes (500mm x 500mm)
- Remove surface coatings if necessary
- Document material composition

#### 2. Pyrolysis Parameters
- **Temperature:** 450-600°C
- **Atmosphere:** Inert (Nitrogen)
- **Duration:** 60-90 minutes
- **Pressure:** Atmospheric

#### 3. Fiber Recovery
- **Recovery rate:** 85-90% by weight
- **Fiber length:** Retained 80% of original
- **Tensile strength:** 90-95% of virgin
- **Modulus:** 95-98% of virgin

#### 4. Resin Treatment
- Resin decomposes to pyrolysis oil
- Used for energy recovery
- Clean combustion with emissions control

### Material Specifications

#### Input Material
- Part Numbers: PN-52-10-01-101001, PN-52-10-01-101002
- Material: Carbon fiber/Epoxy composite
- Fiber volume: 60%
- Total weight: 51.6 kg per door

#### Output Material
- Recovered carbon fiber: 43.9 kg
- Pyrolysis oil: 7.7 kg (for energy)

### Environmental Benefits

#### Per Door Unit
- CO2 savings: 280 kg CO2e (vs. virgin fiber)
- Energy savings: 2,400 MJ
- Landfill diversion: 51.6 kg

#### Applications for Recycled Fiber
- Non-structural aerospace components
- Automotive parts
- Sporting goods
- Consumer electronics
- Construction materials

### Quality Control

#### Testing Requirements
- Tensile strength (ISO 5079)
- Fiber length distribution
- Surface cleanliness
- Contamination levels
- Thermal analysis

#### Acceptance Criteria
- Tensile strength: >3,000 MPa
- Fiber length: >10mm (80%)
- Contamination: <1%
- Char content: <2%
