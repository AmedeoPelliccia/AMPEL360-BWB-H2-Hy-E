# Component Segregation
## Material Stream Separation Process

### Overview
Proper component segregation is critical for maximizing material recovery rates and ensuring regulatory compliance. This document provides detailed procedures for separating Door L1 Forward into recyclable material streams.

### Segregation Process

#### Step 1: Workspace Setup
- Clean, dry area
- Adequate lighting
- Labeled containers positioned
- Scales for weighing
- PPE available
- Spill kits staged

#### Step 2: Component Removal
Follow disassembly sequence:
1. Remove hazmat items first
2. Disconnect electronics
3. Remove mechanical systems
4. Separate structural components

#### Step 3: Cleaning
- Remove excess lubricants
- Wipe surfaces clean
- Dry components
- Avoid water on electronics

#### Step 4: Sorting
- Place in appropriate container
- Do not mix material types
- Remove dissimilar fasteners
- Separate coated from bare metal

#### Step 5: Weighing
- Weigh each material stream
- Record on segregation form
- Update tracking system
- Generate transfer notes

#### Step 6: Labeling
Each container must have:
- Material type
- Weight
- Aircraft MSN
- Door serial number
- Date of segregation
- Handler initials
- Destination facility

### Quality Checks

**Purity Verification:**
- Visual inspection for contamination
- Magnet test for ferrous metals
- XRF analysis for alloy verification (if available)
- Reject contaminated materials

**Documentation:**
- Segregation checklist completed
- Weights recorded
- Photos taken
- Transfer notes prepared

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Mixing aluminum and titanium
❌ Leaving batteries in electronics
❌ Not draining fluids from actuators
❌ Contaminating CFRP with metals
❌ Mixing different aluminum alloys
❌ Not removing fasteners from composites
