### Phase 2: Engineering Prototype - $530,000
**Timeline:** Q2 2026 - Q3 2026 (6 months)  
**Request Date:** Q1 2026 (pending Phase 1 success)  
**Milestone:** GVT damping measurement

#### Budget Detail:
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| FEA Analysis | $30,000 |
| Cure Mold Fabrication | $250,000 |
| Assembly Fixtures | $75,000 |
| PROTO-001 Materials | $50,000 |
| PROTO-001 Manufacturing | $50,000 |
| GVT Testing (CRITICAL) | $30,000 |
| Static Ultimate Test | $25,000 |
| Engineering Support | $20,000 |
| **Subtotal** | **$530,000** |

#### Justification:
- **CRITICAL GO/NO-GO GATE**: GVT damping measurement
- If damping <3%, redesign required before proceeding
- Validates structural capability (17 psi ultimate)
- Proves manufacturing feasibility
- Tooling reusable for subsequent units

#### Risk Mitigation:
- Phase 1 demonstrators reduce uncertainty
- FEA analysis before committing to mold
- Tooling designed for modifications
- Budget includes one manufacturing iteration

#### Success Criteria for Phase 3 Funding:
- [ ] Mode 1 damping ≥3%
- [ ] Ultimate strength achieved (17 psi)
- [ ] Manufacturing quality acceptable (<5% voids)
- [ ] FEA correlation within ±20%

## ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED

### Alternative 1: Delay Until Full Funding
**Pros:** All funding secured upfront  
**Cons:** 
- 12+ month delay
- Lose competitive advantage
- Knowledge drain from team
- Miss market window

**Conclusion:** Not recommended

### Alternative 2: Single Phase to Flight
**Pros:** Faster timeline  
**Cons:**
- Higher risk ($2M at once)
- No intermediate decision gates
- Likely redesign iterations
- Cost overruns probable

**Conclusion:** Not recommended

### Alternative 3: Phased Approach (RECOMMENDED)
**Pros:**
- Risk-based funding
- Decision gates at each phase
- Can stop if issues found
- Manageable cash flow

**Cons:**
- Longer overall timeline
- Multiple approval cycles

**Conclusion:** RECOMMENDED
