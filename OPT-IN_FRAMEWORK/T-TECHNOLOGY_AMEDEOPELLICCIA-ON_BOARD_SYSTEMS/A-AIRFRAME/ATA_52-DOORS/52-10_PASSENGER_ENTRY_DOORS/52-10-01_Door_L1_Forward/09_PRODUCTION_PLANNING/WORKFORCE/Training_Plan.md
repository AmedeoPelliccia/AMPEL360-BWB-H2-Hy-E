## 1. TRAINING PHILOSOPHY

### Objective
Develop a highly skilled workforce capable of producing high-quality composite aircraft doors to aerospace standards (AS9100).

### Approach
- **Structured curriculum** with progressive skill development
- **Hands-on training** with actual materials and tools
- **Mentorship program** pairing experienced with new technicians
- **Certification-based** progression with documented competencies
- **Continuous improvement** with ongoing skills enhancement

---

## 2. TRAINING LEVELS

### Level 1: Basic Technician (Entry Level)
**Duration:** 2 weeks  
**Prerequisites:** High school diploma, pass aptitude test

**Curriculum:**
- Safety training (40 hours)
  - Chemical hazards (epoxy, solvents)
  - Personal protective equipment
  - Emergency procedures
  - Ergonomics
- Quality fundamentals (20 hours)
  - AS9100 overview
  - Documentation requirements
  - Inspection basics
- Material handling (20 hours)
  - Composite materials overview
  - Storage requirements
  - Out-time tracking
- Basic hand tools (20 hours)

**Certification:** Basic Safety & Materials Handling

---

### Level 2: Intermediate Technician
**Duration:** 8 weeks  
**Prerequisites:** Level 1 + 3 months experience

**Curriculum:**
- Composite layup fundamentals (80 hours)
  - Ply cutting techniques
  - Hand layup procedures
  - Consolidation methods
  - Debulking operations
- Vacuum bagging (40 hours)
  - Consumables application
  - Bag installation
  - Leak checking
  - Common issues
- Assembly operations (40 hours)
  - Fastener installation
  - Torque procedures
  - Seal installation
- Inspection techniques (40 hours)
  - Visual inspection standards
  - Dimensional measurement
  - Documentation

**Certification:** Composite Technician Level 2

---

### Level 3: Advanced Technician
**Duration:** 12 weeks  
**Prerequisites:** Level 2 + 1 year experience

**Curriculum:**
- Advanced composite techniques (80 hours)
  - Complex geometries
  - Core splicing
  - Repair procedures
  - Advanced troubleshooting
- Trim and drilling (40 hours)
  - CNC operation
  - Delamination prevention
  - Insert installation
- Process control (40 hours)
  - SPC basics
  - Control plans
  - Non-conformance handling
- Train-the-trainer (40 hours)
  - Mentoring skills
  - Work instruction creation

**Certification:** Composite Technician Level 3

---

## 3. SPECIALIZED TRAINING

### NDT Technician
**Duration:** 12 weeks + 6 months supervised  
**Provider:** External certification body (ASNT)

**Requirements:**
- ASNT Level II Ultrasonic Testing
- Aerospace composite experience
- Vision test annually
- Practical exam on aerospace parts

---

### Quality Inspector
**Duration:** 8 weeks  
**Prerequisites:** 2 years manufacturing experience

**Curriculum:**
- AS9100 auditor training (40 hours)
- CMM operation (40 hours)
- Inspection planning (40 hours)
- Statistical methods (40 hours)
- Non-conformance management (20 hours)

**Certification:** AS9100 Internal Auditor

---

### Paint Technician
**Duration:** 4 weeks  
**Prerequisites:** 1 year finishing experience

**Curriculum:**
- Aerospace coating specifications (20 hours)
- Surface preparation (40 hours)
- Spray techniques (40 hours)
- Defect prevention (20 hours)
- Environmental compliance (20 hours)

**Certification:** Aerospace Coating Application

---

## 4. TRAINING SCHEDULE

### Prototype Phase (2026)
- Hire core team 3 months before production start
- All personnel complete Level 1 immediately
- Composite techs complete Level 2 before first build
- Supervisors attend leadership training
- Quality inspector completes AS9100 training

### LRIP Phase (2027)
- New hires complete Level 1 within first week
- Progression to Level 2 after 3 months
- Senior techs begin Level 3 training
- Cross-training program begins
- Continuous improvement training

### FRP Phase (2028+)
- All composite techs certified Level 2 minimum
- At least 2 techs certified Level 3
- Annual refresher training for all
- Skills assessment program implemented
- Advanced techniques training

---

## 5. ON-THE-JOB TRAINING

### Mentorship Program
- Each new technician paired with experienced mentor
- Weekly one-on-one sessions
- Documented skill progression
- Mentor receives training allowance

### Skill Rotation
- Technicians rotate through different operations
- Builds flexibility and understanding
- Reduces single-point dependencies
- Enhances problem-solving ability
