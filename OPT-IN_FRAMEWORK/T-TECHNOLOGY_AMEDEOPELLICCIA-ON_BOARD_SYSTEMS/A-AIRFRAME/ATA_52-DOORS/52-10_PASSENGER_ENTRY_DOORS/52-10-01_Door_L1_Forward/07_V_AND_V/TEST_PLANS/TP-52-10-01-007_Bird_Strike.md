# Test Plan - Bird Strike Test

**Test Plan:** TP-52-10-01-007  
**Priority:** MEDIUM  
**Revision:** 1.0

**Status:** Conceptual

---

## 1. TEST OBJECTIVES

Demonstrate bird strike resistance per CS-25.631 - 1.8 kg bird at 230 knots without penetration.

---

## 2. TEST REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 Impact Conditions
- **Bird mass:** 1.8 kg (4 lb)
- **Impact velocity:** 230 knots (391 km/h, 119 m/s)
- **Impact location:** Panel center (worst case)
- **Bird simulant:** Gelatin projectile per MIL-STD-662F

### 2.2 Acceptance Criteria
**PASS:**
- No penetration of cabin pressure boundary
- Damage is localized (<300 mm diameter)
- Door remains attached to hinges
- Latches remain engaged
- Repairable damage only

---

## 3. TEST SETUP

### 3.1 Test Facility
- Air cannon or gas gun
- Velocity measurement (radar/light gates)
- High-speed video (>10,000 fps)
- Catch tank/backstop

### 3.2 Test Article
- Door installed in fuselage section
- Latches engaged
- Seals inflated
- Pressurized to 8.5 psi (operational condition)

---

## 4. TEST PROCEDURE

### 4.1 Preparation
1. Prepare gelatin bird simulant (1.8 kg)
2. Install door in test fixture
3. Pressurize cabin to 8.5 psi
4. Setup high-speed cameras
5. Verify impact aim point

### 4.2 Impact Test
1. Load bird simulant in cannon
2. Verify velocity (230 knots Â±5%)
3. Trigger cameras
4. Launch bird
5. Verify impact location
6. Record results

### 4.3 Post-Impact
1. Depressurize cabin slowly
2. Visual inspection
3. Measure damage extent
4. NDT inspection
5. Assess repairability
6. Document with photos/video
