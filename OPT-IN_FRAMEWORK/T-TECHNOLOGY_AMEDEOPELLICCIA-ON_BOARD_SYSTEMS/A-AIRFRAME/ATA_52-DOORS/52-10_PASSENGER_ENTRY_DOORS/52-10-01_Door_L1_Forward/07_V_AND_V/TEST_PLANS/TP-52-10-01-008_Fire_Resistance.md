# Test Plan - Fire Resistance Test
## Door L1 Forward

**Test Plan:** TP-52-10-01-008  
**Priority:** LOW  
**Revision:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2025-11-03  
**Status:** Conceptual

---

## 1. TEST OBJECTIVES

Demonstrate fire resistance of door materials and seals per CS-25.853 - 15 minutes at 1000°C without burn-through.

---

## 2. TEST REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 Fire Exposure
- **Temperature:** 1000°C (1832°F)
- **Duration:** 15 minutes
- **Flame source:** Kerosene burner per FAR 25 Appendix F
- **Heat flux:** 84 kW/m²

### 2.2 Acceptance Criteria
**PASS:**
- No burn-through of pressure boundary
- Flame penetration < 100 mm
- Seal integrity maintained for 10 minutes minimum
- Smoke generation within limits

---

## 3. TEST ARTICLE

### 3.1 Specimens
- Full door panel section (600 mm × 600 mm)
- Representative construction (CFRP face sheets + Nomex core)
- Seal materials included
- Frame attachment included

### 3.2 Instrumentation
- Thermocouples: Back face (16 locations)
- Heat flux sensors: 4 locations
- Video cameras: Front and back
- Smoke opacity meter

---

## 4. TEST PROCEDURE

### 4.1 Setup
1. Mount specimen in vertical orientation
2. Install thermocouples on back face
3. Position burner per FAR 25 App F
4. Setup smoke collection hood
5. Verify instrumentation

### 4.2 Fire Exposure
1. Ignite burner and stabilize flame
2. Verify heat flux (84 kW/m²)
3. Expose specimen for 15 minutes
4. Record temperatures continuously
5. Monitor for burn-through
6. Extinguish after 15 minutes

### 4.3 Post-Test
1. Allow cooling (30 minutes)
2. Measure char depth
3. Assess structural integrity
4. Document damage extent
5. Smoke toxicity analysis

---

## 5. PASS/FAIL CRITERIA

**Measurements:**
- Back face temperature < 300°C average
- No flame penetration through panel
- Structural integrity maintained
- Smoke density < FST requirements

---

## 6. TEST FACILITY

- FAA Fire Safety Laboratory
- EASA fire test facility
- Or certified commercial fire test lab
- Must be witnessed by authorities
