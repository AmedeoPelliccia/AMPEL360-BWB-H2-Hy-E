# Test Plan - Environmental Test
## Door L1 Forward

**Test Plan:** TP-52-10-01-009  
**Priority:** LOW  
**Revision:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2025-11-03  
**Status:** Not Started

---

## 1. TEST OBJECTIVES

Demonstrate environmental durability through temperature extremes, humidity, salt fog, and thermal cycling per DO-160G.

---

## 2. TEST REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 Temperature Extremes
- **Low temperature:** -40°C (-40°F) for 24 hours
- **High temperature:** +55°C (+131°F) for 24 hours
- **Operational test:** Door operation at both extremes

### 2.2 Thermal Cycling
- **Cycles:** 100 cycles
- **Range:** -40°C to +55°C
- **Rate:** 5°C/min
- **Dwell:** 30 minutes at each extreme

### 2.3 Humidity
- **Condition:** 95% RH at 35°C
- **Duration:** 10 days
- **Per:** MIL-STD-810G Method 507.6

### 2.4 Salt Fog (Optional)
- **Condition:** 5% NaCl solution
- **Duration:** 48 hours
- **Per:** ASTM B117

---

## 3. TEST ARTICLE

- Door assembly or representative coupons
- Sealing system
- Latching mechanism
- Coating samples

---

## 4. TEST PROCEDURE

### 4.1 Phase 1: Low Temperature
1. Stabilize at -40°C for 24 hours
2. Perform door operation (5 cycles)
3. Measure seal effectiveness
4. Check latch function
5. Inspect for damage

### 4.2 Phase 2: High Temperature
1. Stabilize at +55°C for 24 hours
2. Perform door operation (5 cycles)
3. Measure seal degradation
4. Check mechanism function
5. Inspect for damage

### 4.3 Phase 3: Thermal Cycling
1. Program 100 cycles (-40°C to +55°C)
2. Periodic functional checks (every 25 cycles)
3. Monitor for delamination
4. Inspect seals and coatings

### 4.4 Phase 4: Humidity
1. Expose to 95% RH at 35°C for 10 days
2. Measure moisture ingress
3. Check seal swelling
4. Inspect for corrosion

---

## 5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

**PASS:**
- Door operates at both temperature extremes
- Seals remain effective (leak rate unchanged)
- No delamination or coating degradation
- Mechanisms function normally
- No corrosion on metallic parts
