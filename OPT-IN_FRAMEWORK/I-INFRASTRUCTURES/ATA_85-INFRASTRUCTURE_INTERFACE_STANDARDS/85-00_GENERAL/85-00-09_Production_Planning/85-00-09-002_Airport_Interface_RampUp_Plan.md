# 85-00-09-002 Airport Interface RampUp Plan

## Document Information

- **Document ID**: 85-00-09-002
- **Title**: Airport Interface RampUp Plan
- **Version**: 1.0
- **Date**: 2025-11-21
- **Status**: Draft
- **Category**: Production Planning
- **ATA Chapter**: 85 - Infrastructure Interface Standards

## Purpose

This document defines the wave-based airport deployment strategy for ATA 85 infrastructure interfaces. It establishes airport selection criteria, regional phasing approach, and dependency management for rolling out infrastructure interfaces supporting the AMPEL360 BWB H₂ / CO₂ battery ecosystem.

## Scope

This plan covers:

- Airport selection criteria and evaluation methodology
- Wave-based deployment strategy and timing
- Regional phasing and geographic considerations
- Dependencies on local infrastructure readiness
- Coordination mechanisms with airport authorities
- Rollout risk management and contingency planning

## Airport Selection Criteria

### Primary Selection Criteria

**1. Strategic Importance**
- Hub airport for major operators
- Key route network connectivity
- Expected BWB fleet deployment volume
- Regional market significance

**2. Infrastructure Readiness**
- Existing or planned H₂ infrastructure
- Electrical grid capacity for battery charging
- Airport capital investment program alignment
- Terminal and ramp configuration compatibility

**3. Regulatory Environment**
- Progressive aviation authority support
- Streamlined approval processes
- H₂ handling regulatory framework
- Environmental approval feasibility

**4. Operator Commitment**
- Anchor operator with BWB fleet orders
- Multi-operator interest and coordination
- Ground handler capabilities
- Service level agreement readiness

**5. Commercial Viability**
- Airport investment commitment
- Cost-sharing framework agreement
- Expected utilization and revenue
- Long-term strategic partnership potential

### Secondary Selection Criteria

- Geographic diversity for global coverage
- Technology demonstration opportunities
- Innovation and sustainability leadership
- Regional supply chain capabilities
- Training and development center potential

## Wave-Based Deployment Strategy

### Wave 1: Pilot Airports (EIS-18 months to EIS)

**Objective**: Validate infrastructure interfaces in real-world operations

**Airport Count**: 3-5 airports

**Selection Focus**:
- Maximum infrastructure readiness
- Strong operator partnership
- Regulatory support and flexibility
- Willingness to iterate and refine

**Candidate Airports**:
- Major European hub with H₂ initiatives (e.g., Amsterdam Schiphol, Copenhagen)
- North American gateway with sustainability programs (e.g., San Francisco, Seattle)
- Asia-Pacific innovation leader (e.g., Singapore Changi, Incheon)

**Deployment Scope**:
- Full interface suite deployment
- Extensive monitoring and data collection
- Operational procedure validation
- Feedback and refinement process

**Timeline**: 
- Design freeze: EIS-18 months
- Installation: EIS-12 to EIS-6 months
- Commissioning and testing: EIS-6 to EIS-3 months
- Operational validation: EIS-3 months to EIS

### Wave 2: Initial Scale (EIS to EIS+12 months)

**Objective**: Scale to key operational hubs with refined interfaces

**Airport Count**: 15-25 airports

**Selection Focus**:
- Major hub airports for anchor operators
- Regional distribution across key markets
- Strong commercial commitment
- Proven infrastructure capability

**Regional Distribution**:
- Europe: 8-10 airports
- North America: 4-6 airports
- Asia-Pacific: 4-6 airports
- Middle East: 2-3 airports

**Deployment Scope**:
- Standardized interface configurations
- Regional adaptation within standards
- Established installation procedures
- Production supply chain activated

**Timeline**:
- Planning and design: EIS-9 to EIS
- Installation: EIS to EIS+9 months
- Commissioning: EIS+6 to EIS+12 months
- Operational: EIS+12 months

### Wave 3: Regional Expansion (EIS+12 to EIS+36 months)

**Objective**: Expand to regional hubs and secondary airports

**Airport Count**: 60-80 airports

**Selection Focus**:
- Regional hub airports
- Operator network expansion
- Local H₂ infrastructure development
- Cost-effective deployment opportunities

**Regional Distribution**:
- Europe: 25-30 airports
- North America: 15-20 airports
- Asia-Pacific: 15-20 airports
- Middle East: 3-5 airports
- Latin America: 2-5 airports
- Africa: 2-5 airports

**Deployment Scope**:
- Full standardization and industrialization
- Regional supplier engagement
- Modular deployment approaches
- Phased capability build-up

**Timeline**:
- Continuous deployment over 24 months
- 3-4 airports per month average
- 6-9 months per airport cycle

### Wave 4: Global Coverage (EIS+36 months onward)

**Objective**: Complete global network coverage

**Airport Count**: 100+ airports

**Selection Focus**:
- Network completion requirements
- Regional and secondary airports
- Operator route expansion
- Market demand and business case

**Deployment Approach**:
- Standardized, repeatable deployment model
- Regional partners and licensees
- Simplified interface configurations where appropriate
- Long-term support infrastructure

## Local Infrastructure Dependencies

### H₂ Infrastructure Requirements

**Essential Pre-requisites**:
- H₂ production or delivery capability
- Storage facilities (liquid or compressed)
- Distribution pipeline or truck delivery
- Safety and monitoring systems

**Deployment Scenarios**:
1. **Greenfield H₂**: Airport has no existing H₂ infrastructure
   - Requires 24-36 months lead time
   - Energy partner coordination critical
   - May delay airport selection

2. **Partial H₂**: Airport has industrial H₂ but not aviation-ready
   - 12-18 months upgrade timeline
   - Focus on safety and capacity
   - Faster deployment opportunity

3. **Aviation H₂ Ready**: Airport has aviation-grade H₂ infrastructure
   - 6-9 months interface deployment
   - Priority selection candidate
   - Minimal additional infrastructure

### Electrical Infrastructure Requirements

**CO₂ Battery Charging Requirements**:
- High-power electrical connection (multi-MW capacity)
- Dedicated substations for charging bays
- Grid capacity and reliability
- Backup power systems

**GSE Electrification Requirements**:
- eGSE charging infrastructure
- Ramp-side power distribution
- Load management systems

**Typical Lead Times**:
- Grid connection: 12-24 months
- Substation installation: 6-12 months
- Distribution and equipment: 3-6 months

### Airport Physical Infrastructure

**Apron and Gate Modifications**:
- BWB-compatible gate positions
- Sufficient clearances and maneuvering space
- Service vehicle access paths
- Safety zone demarcation

**Terminal Interfaces**:
- Boarding bridge compatibility or adapters
- Evacuation access arrangements
- Passenger flow modifications
- Signage and wayfinding

**Typical Lead Times**:
- Minor modifications: 3-6 months
- Major reconfigurations: 12-24 months
- New terminal areas: 36+ months

## Coordination Mechanisms

### Airport Authority Engagement

**Phase 1: Initial Assessment** (18-24 months before deployment)
- Strategic discussion and interest confirmation
- Preliminary technical assessment
- Investment and commitment framework
- Regulatory pathway identification

**Phase 2: Detailed Planning** (12-18 months before deployment)
- Technical design and specifications
- Project plan and timeline
- Investment agreement and contracts
- Regulatory submissions

**Phase 3: Implementation** (6-12 months before deployment)
- Procurement and construction
- Installation and integration
- Testing and commissioning

- Operational procedure validation
- Staff training and qualification
- Safety case finalization
- Go-live preparation

### Operator Coordination

- Fleet deployment schedule alignment
- Operational procedure integration
- Crew and ground staff training
- Service level agreements

### Infrastructure Provider Coordination

- H₂ supply and delivery commitments
- Energy supply agreements
- Equipment supply and installation
- Maintenance and support contracts

## Risk Management

### Airport Selection Risks

**Risk**: Selected airports delay or cancel participation
**Mitigation**: 
- Backup airports in each wave
- Flexible wave composition
- Regular commitment reviews
- Early warning indicators

**Risk**: Infrastructure dependencies not met on schedule
**Mitigation**:
- Detailed pre-deployment assessment
- Milestone-based wave entry criteria
- Alternative deployment sequences
- Infrastructure provider guarantees

### Deployment Execution Risks

**Risk**: Installation or commissioning delays
**Mitigation**:
- Buffer time in schedules
- Experienced installation teams
- Standardized procedures
- Issue escalation protocols

**Risk**: Regulatory approval delays
**Mitigation**:
- Early regulatory engagement
- Standardized approval packages
- Parallel approval processes
- Regulatory authority coordination
