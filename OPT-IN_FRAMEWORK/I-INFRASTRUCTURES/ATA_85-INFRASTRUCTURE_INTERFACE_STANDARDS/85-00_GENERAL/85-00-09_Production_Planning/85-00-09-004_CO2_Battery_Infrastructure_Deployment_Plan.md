## Purpose

This document defines the production and rollout strategy for CO₂ battery infrastructure interfaces. It covers battery docking systems, charging/swap stations, logistics integration, thermal management, and the coordination required to support the AMPEL360 BWB's CO₂ capture and battery systems at airports worldwide.

## Scope

This plan addresses:

- CO₂ battery docking interface standards and specifications
- Charging and battery swap station deployment
- Battery logistics and handling systems
- Thermal management infrastructure
- Integration with turnaround operations
- Battery fleet management and tracking systems

## CO₂ Battery System Overview

### Battery System Architecture

**Containerized Battery Modules**:
- Standardized battery container format (compatible with cargo handling)
- Typical capacity: 500-1000 kWh per module
- Multiple modules per aircraft (4-8 depending on configuration)
- Hot-swappable design for rapid turnaround

**CO₂ Capture Integration**:
- CO₂ absorption during flight operations
- Electrical energy storage for auxiliary systems
- Regeneration cycle during ground operations
- Thermal integration with aircraft systems

**Interface Requirements**:
- Mechanical docking (structural, alignment, locking)
- Electrical connections (power, data, monitoring)
- Thermal management (cooling during charging, heating during regeneration)
- CO₂ handling (vent, capture, regeneration interfaces)

### Operating Modes

**Flight Mode**:
- Batteries provide auxiliary power
- CO₂ capture from ambient or systems
- Thermal management from aircraft systems
- Data logging and health monitoring

**Ground Charging Mode**:
- High-power electrical charging
- Active thermal management
- CO₂ regeneration/release
- Battery health assessment

**Swap Mode**:
- Rapid battery module exchange
- Depleted batteries removed
- Charged batteries installed
- Full system checkout

## Battery Infrastructure Interface Standards

### Docking Interface Standards

**Mechanical Interface**:
- Standardized mounting points on aircraft (ventral and lateral locations)
- Precision alignment guides and tolerances
- Automated or manual locking mechanisms
- Load distribution and structural requirements
- Access for installation/removal

**Electrical Interface**:
- High-power charging connector (up to 500 kW per module)
- Low-voltage control and monitoring interface
- Redundant safety interlocks
- Connector durability and cycle life requirements
- Environmental protection (water, dust, de-icing)

**Thermal Interface**:
- Cooling fluid connections for charge cycle
- Heating connections for cold weather operations
- Thermal monitoring and control
- Emergency thermal management
- Fluid quality and compatibility

**Data Interface**:
- Battery management system (BMS) communication
- State of charge and health monitoring
- Diagnostic and prognostic data
- Configuration and calibration
- Firmware update capability

### Charging Station Standards

**Charging Infrastructure**:
- High-power DC charging (250-500 kW per station)
- Multiple charging bays per airport area
- Grid connection and power management
- Energy storage buffers (optional)
- Renewable energy integration (where available)

**Charging Protocol**:
- Standardized charging curve and communication
- Pre-charge safety checks
- Temperature-controlled charging
- Charge termination and disconnect
- Post-charge verification

**Station Configuration**:
- Fixed charging stations (terminal/hangar areas)
- Mobile charging units (ramp operations)
- Battery swap/charge facilities (depot operations)
- Emergency charging capability

### Battery Swap Station Standards

**Swap Station Layout**:
- Drive-through or side-loading configuration
- Automated or semi-automated swap equipment
- Battery storage racks (charged and depleted)
- Charging bays within swap facility
- Quality control and testing areas

**Swap Process**:
- Vehicle positioning and alignment
- Automated battery unlocking and removal
- Battery transport to storage/charging
- Charged battery retrieval and installation
- Automated checkout and data exchange
- Target swap time: 15-30 minutes

**Battery Fleet Management**:
- Inventory tracking (charged, charging, depleted)
- Rotation optimization for battery health
- Predictive maintenance and replacement
- Traceability and certification
- Emergency battery availability

## Deployment Strategy

### Phase 1: Pilot Airport Deployment (EIS-18 to EIS)

**Objectives**:
- Validate battery interface design and operations
- Test charging and swap procedures
- Establish performance baselines
- Refine logistics and handling processes

**Deployment Scope** (3-5 pilot airports):
- 1-2 charging stations per airport (2-4 charging bays each)
- 1 battery swap facility per airport (optional)
- Complete thermal management systems
- Full monitoring and data collection
- Battery fleet for testing (20-40 modules per airport)

**Operating Model**:
- Primarily charging-based operations
- Swap capability for validation
- Extensive monitoring and analysis
- Process refinement and optimization

**Timeline and Milestones**:
- EIS-18 months: Interface design freeze
- EIS-15 months: Station design and procurement
- EIS-12 months: Grid connection and civil works
- EIS-9 months: Equipment installation
- EIS-6 months: Commissioning and testing
- EIS-3 months: Operational validation with test batteries
- EIS: Ready for commercial operations

### Phase 2: Initial Scale Deployment (EIS to EIS+12 months)

**Objectives**:
- Scale to 15-25 airports
- Establish standardized charging infrastructure
- Build battery production and logistics
- Optimize charging vs. swap strategies per airport

**Deployment Scope**:
- 2-4 charging stations per airport
- Battery swap facilities at high-volume hubs
- Regional battery service centers
- Airport-specific battery inventory

**Operating Model Variants**:
- **Large Hubs**: Swap-focused (rapid turnaround)
- **Medium Hubs**: Charging-focused with swap backup
- **Regional Airports**: Charging-only

**Battery Fleet Scaling**:
- 1.5x battery modules vs. installed aircraft (safety margin)
- Regional pooling for efficiency
- Predictive replacement inventory
- Coordinated battery production ramp-up

### Phase 3: Regional Expansion (EIS+12 to EIS+36 months)

**Objectives**:
- Expand to 60-80 airports
- Full standardization and industrialization
- Regional battery service networks
- Cost optimization through scale

**Deployment Scope**:
- Modular charging infrastructure (scalable capacity)
- Selective swap station deployment
- Regional battery redistribution centers
- Coordinated logistics networks

**Battery Fleet Maturity**:
- Mature supply chain and production
- Battery health and lifecycle management
- Refurbishment and recycling programs
- Second-life battery applications

### Phase 4: Global Coverage (EIS+36 months onward)

**Objectives**:
- Complete global network (100+ airports)
- Technology refresh and optimization
- Next-generation battery integration
- Circular economy implementation

**Deployment Characteristics**:
- Demand-driven expansion
- Flexible charging infrastructure
- Battery technology upgrades
- Sustainable battery lifecycle management

## Airport-Specific Configurations

### Large Hub Airports (>10 BWB movements/day)

**Infrastructure**:
- 3-5 charging stations (12-20 charging bays total)
- 1-2 battery swap facilities
- On-site battery service center
- Large battery inventory (100-200 modules)
- High-power grid connection (5-10 MW)

**Operations**:
- Swap-primary for rapid turnaround
- Overnight charging for inventory replenishment
- On-site battery maintenance and testing
- 24/7 operations

### Medium Hub Airports (3-10 BWB movements/day)

**Infrastructure**:
- 2-3 charging stations (6-12 bays)
- Optional swap capability (1 facility)
- Medium battery inventory (40-80 modules)
- Grid connection (2-5 MW)

**Operations**:
- Charging-primary operations
- Swap for quick turnarounds or peak periods
- Scheduled battery maintenance
- Extended operations hours

### Regional Airports (<3 BWB movements/day)

**Infrastructure**:
- 1-2 charging stations (2-4 bays)
- Charging-only operations
- Small battery inventory (10-20 modules)
- Grid connection (0.5-2 MW)

**Operations**:
- Scheduled charging during aircraft ground time
- Battery rotation with regional hubs
- Periodic battery service
- Day operations focus

## Thermal Management Systems

### Charging Thermal Management

**Cooling Requirements**:
- Active cooling during rapid charging
- Temperature maintenance (15-35°C optimal)
- Heat dissipation up to 50 kW per module
- Coolant circulation and heat rejection

**Cooling System Options**:
- Liquid cooling with glycol/water mix
- Air cooling for moderate climates
- Hybrid cooling systems
- Waste heat recovery (optional)

### Cold Weather Operations

**Pre-heating Requirements**:
- Battery warming below 0°C
- Heating power up to 20 kW per module
- Glycol circulation for thermal distribution
- Insulation and thermal retention

**Cold Weather Infrastructure**:
- Heated storage for batteries
- Pre-conditioning before installation
- Heated charging bays
- Cold soak protection

### Hot Weather Operations

**Enhanced Cooling**:
- Increased cooling capacity in hot climates (>35°C)
- Shading and insulation of outdoor equipment
- Pre-cooled batteries before installation
- Continuous monitoring and control

## Battery Logistics and Handling

### Ground Handling Equipment

**Battery Transport Vehicles**:
- Specialized battery carriers (tugs and trailers)
- Powered lifting and positioning equipment
- Environmental protection during transport
- Safety interlocks and monitoring

**Handling Procedures**:
- Trained and certified personnel only
- Standardized attachment/detachment procedures
- Pre-/post-operation inspections
- Safety zone management during handling

### Battery Storage and Warehousing

**Storage Facilities**:
- Climate-controlled battery storage
- Inventory management systems
- Safety and fire protection
- Security and access control

**Storage Configurations**:
- Charged battery ready storage (short-term)
- Depleted battery collection (awaiting charging)
- Maintenance/quarantine storage (defective units)
- Long-term storage (strategic reserves)

### Inter-Airport Battery Distribution

**Regional Distribution**:
- Battery pooling across airport clusters
- Truck/rail transport of battery modules
- Emergency battery redistribution
- Return logistics for maintenance

**Tracking and Management**:
- Real-time battery location tracking
- State of health monitoring during transport
- Chain of custody and certification
- Predictive demand and redistribution

## Grid Integration and Energy Management

### Electrical Infrastructure

**Grid Connection Requirements**:
- High-power service (MW-scale for large hubs)
- Redundancy and reliability
- Power quality and harmonics management
- Grid code compliance

**On-Site Distribution**:
- Medium voltage distribution to charging areas
- Step-down transformers at charging stations
- Load management and peak shaving
- Emergency power backup

### Energy Management Strategies

**Load Management**:
- Coordinated charging to minimize peak demand
- Off-peak charging optimization
- Demand response participation
- Energy storage integration (where feasible)

**Renewable Energy Integration**:
- On-site solar/wind where available
- Renewable energy credits/certificates
- Grid renewable energy procurement
- Carbon footprint optimization

**Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) Potential**:
- Battery discharge to grid during peak demand
- Ancillary services provision
- Revenue generation opportunity
- Grid stability support

## Risk Management

### Technical Risks

**Risk**: Battery interface compatibility issues
**Mitigation**: 
- Rigorous design validation and testing
- Standardization across battery suppliers
- Retrofit provisions in early deployments
- Continuous monitoring and feedback

**Risk**: Charging infrastructure reliability
**Mitigation**:
- Redundant charging capacity
- Preventive maintenance programs
- Backup and emergency procedures
- Supplier performance requirements

### Operational Risks

**Risk**: Battery availability insufficient for operations
**Mitigation**:
- Inventory buffer (1.5x minimum)
- Regional battery pooling
- Expedited logistics procedures
- Emergency lease/rental arrangements

**Risk**: Thermal management failures
**Mitigation**:
- Redundant thermal systems
- Real-time monitoring and alarms
- Alternative cooling methods
- Environmental operating limits

### Safety Risks

**Risk**: Battery thermal runaway or fire
**Mitigation**:
- Battery management system protections
- Fire detection and suppression systems
- Isolation and containment procedures
- Emergency response training
