## 1. Introduction

This document defines the spare parts strategy and logistics services for ATA 85 Infrastructure Interface Standards, ensuring timely availability of critical components and consumables to support infrastructure operations and minimize downtime.

### 1.1 Purpose

- Define spare parts categorization and stocking strategies
- Establish logistics flows and distribution networks
- Document supplier relationships and contracts
- Ensure integration with DPP for lifecycle tracking

---

## 2. Spare Parts Categories

### 2.1 Critical Infrastructure Components

**Category A - Flight Critical**
- H₂ refueling connector assemblies
- Emergency shutdown valves and controllers
- Safety monitoring sensors and alarms
- Primary power interface modules

**Stocking Strategy**: Maintain at each operational airport
**Replenishment**: Automatic upon usage, 24-hour delivery guarantee

### 2.2 Essential Components

**Category B - Operations Impact**
- CO₂ battery docking interfaces
- Boarding bridge interface adapters
- Ground power unit (GPU) connectors
- Data interface modules

**Stocking Strategy**: Regional hub stocking (1-2 hour transport radius)
**Replenishment**: Next business day

### 2.3 Standard Components

**Category C - Scheduled Maintenance**
- Filters and seals for H₂ systems
- Cable assemblies and connectors
- Calibration and test equipment consumables
- Wear items (hoses, gaskets)

**Stocking Strategy**: Central warehouse with regional distribution
**Replenishment**: 48-72 hour delivery

### 2.4 Consumables

**Category D - High-Volume Consumables**
- CO₂ battery cartridges (charged)
- Cleaning supplies and materials
- Inspection tools and calibration standards
- Safety equipment (PPE, leak detection supplies)

**Stocking Strategy**: Just-In-Time (JIT) delivery with local buffer stock
**Replenishment**: Daily or on-demand

## 3. Stocking Locations

### 3.1 Airport-Level Stock

**Location**: Each operational airport maintenance facility

**Inventory**:
- 2× Category A critical components per interface type
- Emergency consumables (72-hour supply)
- Basic tools and test equipment

**Replenishment**: Triggered by usage, automatic order placement

### 3.2 Regional Hubs

**Coverage Area**: 1-2 hour transport radius (multiple airports)

**Inventory**:
- Complete Category A/B component sets
- Category C components for common repairs
- Specialized tools and equipment
- Emergency stock pool

**Number of Hubs**: 
- Global: ~20-25 hubs
- Europe: 8 hubs
- North America: 7 hubs
- Asia-Pacific: 6 hubs
- Other regions: 3-4 hubs

### 3.3 Central Warehouse

**Location**: Optimized global logistics centers (2-3 primary locations)

**Inventory**:
- Full range of all spare parts
- Long-lead-time components
- Reparable component pool
- Technology refresh inventory

**Capabilities**:
- 24/7 operations for critical shipments
- Customs and export compliance
- Repair and overhaul coordination
- Obsolescence management

## 4. Logistics Services

### 4.1 Standard Logistics

**Delivery Commitments**:
- Category A (Critical): <24 hours to any operational airport
- Category B (Essential): <48 hours
- Category C (Standard): <72 hours
- Category D (Consumables): Daily scheduled delivery

**Transport Modes**:
- Ground: Regional deliveries (<300 km)
- Air: Urgent and long-distance
- Courier: Critical emergency shipments

### 4.2 Emergency Logistics

**AOG (Aircraft On Ground) Support**:
- Dedicated emergency hotline (24/7)
- Immediate parts location and allocation
- Expedited transport (air charter if necessary)
- Hand-carry service available
- Real-time tracking and updates

**Response Times**:
- Parts location and allocation: <30 minutes
- Dispatch: <2 hours
- Delivery to major airport: <6 hours (global)

### 4.3 CO₂ Cartridge Logistics

**Specialized Service**: Charged CO₂ battery cartridge delivery

**Service Model**:
- Predictive demand forecasting (based on flight schedule and digital twin data)
- Just-In-Time delivery (4-hour lead time)
- Swap and recharge cycle management
- Empty cartridge return logistics

**Stock Locations**:
- Airport: 2-day buffer stock
- Regional hub: 7-day supply
- Charging facilities: Integrated with logistics network

## 7. Pricing and Commercial Models

### 7.1 Spare Parts Pricing

**Pricing Models**:
- **Fixed Price Catalog**: Standard components with published prices
- **Cost-Plus**: Custom or low-volume items
- **Tiered Pricing**: Volume discounts for large customers
- **Service Agreements**: Bundled spare parts in service contracts

**Price Review**: Annual adjustment based on costs, inflation, and market conditions

### 7.2 Logistics Pricing

**Standard Logistics**: Included in service agreements or catalog pricing

**Emergency Logistics**: Premium charges apply
- AOG surcharge: +50% of standard logistics fee
- Air charter: Cost recovery + handling fee
- Hand-carry: Hourly rate + expenses

### 7.3 Service Agreement Options

**Parts Pool Agreement**:
- Customer pays monthly fee for guaranteed parts availability
- No individual part transactions
- Includes standard logistics
- Emergency logistics at reduced premium

**Power-by-the-Hour**:
- All-inclusive service fee per aircraft operating hour
- Covers parts, logistics, and labor
- Predictable cost structure
- Long-term partnership model

## 8. Integration with Digital Systems

### 8.1 Digital Product Passport (DPP) Integration

**Component Traceability**:

- Full lifecycle history (manufacture, installation, maintenance, removal)
- Current location and status

**Benefits**:
- Automated warranty claims
- Recall management
- Sustainability tracking (reuse, recycling)
- Counterfeit prevention

**Real-Time Visibility**:
