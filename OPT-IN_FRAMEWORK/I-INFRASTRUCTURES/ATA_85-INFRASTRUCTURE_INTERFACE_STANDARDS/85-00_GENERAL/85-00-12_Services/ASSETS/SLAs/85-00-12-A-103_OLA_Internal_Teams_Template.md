## 4. Request and Escalation Process

### 4.1 Request Initiation

**Standard Requests**:
- Submitted via [ticketing system, email, or specific tool]
- Include required information: [issue description, priority, context]
- Automatically routed to supporting team

**Urgent Requests**:
- Phone or messaging platform for immediate needs
- Followed by formal ticket for documentation

### 4.2 Escalation

**Escalation Triggers**:
- Response time SLA missed
- Resolution time at risk
- Complexity requiring senior expertise
- External customer impact imminent

**Escalation Path**:
1. Team Lead (supporting team)
2. Department Manager (supporting team)
3. Service Director (joint escalation)

---

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

### 5.1 Service Operations Team

**Request Management**:
- Submit clear, complete requests with all necessary information
- Prioritize requests appropriately
- Provide context and background for complex issues

**Coordination**:
- Facilitate communication with external customers
- Coordinate access and logistics for supporting team (if site visit needed)
- Provide field feedback to supporting team

**Documentation**:
- Document customer-facing aspects of issue
- Update service tickets with status
- Close requests when service delivered

### 5.2 Supporting Team

**Service Delivery**:
- Respond within committed timeframes
- Provide expert technical support or services
- Escalate internally when needed
- Keep service operations informed of progress

**Quality**:
- Deliver accurate, complete, and effective solutions
- Document technical findings and recommendations
- Follow standard procedures and quality checks

**Collaboration**:
- Participate in joint problem-solving
- Share knowledge and best practices
- Contribute to continuous improvement

## 9. Performance Measurement

### 9.1 Key Metrics

**Operational Metrics**:
- Request volume (by type and priority)
- Response time (by priority)
- Resolution time (by priority)
- Escalation rate

**Quality Metrics**:
- First-time resolution rate
- Rework rate (issue not fully resolved)
- Data accuracy
- Customer satisfaction (service operations as internal customer)

### 9.2 Targets and Thresholds

**Green (Target Met)**: All metrics within target
**Yellow (At Risk)**: One or more metrics within 10% of target
**Red (Target Missed)**: One or more metrics >10% below target

**Action Required**:
- Yellow: Monitor closely, discuss in weekly sync
- Red: Immediate escalation and improvement plan

## 10. Exception Handling

### 10.1 Capacity Constraints

**When Supporting Team at Capacity**:
- Early notification to service operations
- Prioritization of critical requests
- Temporary resource augmentation (if available)
- Adjust external customer expectations if needed

### 10.2 Unavailability

**Planned Unavailability** (vacations, training):
- Advance notice (≥2 weeks)
- Backup coverage arranged
- Handover of active requests

**Unplanned Unavailability** (illness, emergency):
- Immediate notification
- Escalation to team lead for coverage
- Update on return to service
