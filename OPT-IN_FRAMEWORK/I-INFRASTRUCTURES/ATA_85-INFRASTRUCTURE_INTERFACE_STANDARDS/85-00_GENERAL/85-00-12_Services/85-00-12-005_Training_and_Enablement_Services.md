## 1. Introduction

This document defines the training and enablement strategy for ATA 85 Infrastructure Interface Standards, ensuring that all personnel interacting with BWB infrastructure interfaces are properly trained, certified, and enabled to perform their roles safely and effectively.

### 1.1 Purpose

- Define training curricula for all stakeholder roles
- Establish certification and qualification requirements
- Document training delivery methods and resources
- Ensure ongoing competency through recurrent training

---

## 2. Training Strategy

### 2.1 Competency Framework

**Three-Tier Competency Model**:

1. **Awareness**: Basic understanding of BWB infrastructure (all airport personnel)
2. **Operational**: Hands-on capability to perform defined tasks (technical staff)
3. **Expert**: Advanced troubleshooting and system knowledge (specialists)

### 2.2 Training Principles

- **Safety First**: All training emphasizes safety, especially for H₂ systems
- **Practical Focus**: Hands-on training with real equipment and simulators
- **Modular Design**: Building-block approach from basics to advanced
- **Competency-Based**: Demonstrated proficiency, not just attendance
- **Continuous Learning**: Recurrent training and refresher programs

---

## 3. Training Programs by Role

### 3.1 Airport Operations Staff (Awareness Level)

**Target Audience**: General airport personnel, management, security

**Duration**: 4 hours (e-learning + assessment)

**Content**:
- BWB aircraft overview and unique characteristics
- Infrastructure interface types and locations
- Safety considerations (H₂, CO₂, evacuation)
- Emergency procedures and contacts
- Roles and responsibilities

**Certification**: Awareness Certificate (valid 2 years)

**Delivery**: Online self-paced learning with final assessment

---

### 3.2 Ground Handling Technicians (Operational Level)

**Target Audience**: Ground handling staff, ramp agents

**Duration**: 24 hours (theory + practical)

**Content Modules**:
1. **BWB Infrastructure Overview** (4 hours)
   - Aircraft systems and interfaces
   - Safety zones and precautions
   - Standard operating procedures

2. **H₂ Refueling Operations** (8 hours)
   - H₂ properties and safety
   - Coupling and decoupling procedures
   - Leak detection and emergency response
   - Hands-on training with mockup

3. **CO₂ Battery Handling** (4 hours)
   - Battery cartridge types and handling
   - Docking and undocking procedures
   - Thermal management awareness
   - Emergency procedures

4. **Ground Power and Data Interfaces** (4 hours)
   - GPU connection procedures
   - Data interface setup and verification
   - Troubleshooting common issues

5. **Assessment** (4 hours)
   - Written examination (70% pass required)
   - Practical demonstration (all tasks must be completed correctly)

**Certification**: Ground Handling Technician Certificate (valid 1 year)

**Delivery**: Regional training centers with practical equipment

**Recurrent Training**: Annual (8 hours) - refresher and updates

---

### 3.3 Airport Technical Staff (Operational/Expert Level)

**Target Audience**: Airport maintenance teams, facility engineers

**Duration**: 40 hours (theory + practical + advanced topics)

**Content Modules**:
1. **Foundation** (Ground Handling content - accelerated, 12 hours)
2. **Advanced H₂ Systems** (8 hours)
   - H₂ system architecture and components
   - Detailed safety systems and interlocks
   - Maintenance and inspection procedures
   - Advanced troubleshooting

3. **Infrastructure Interface Systems** (8 hours)
   - Gate interface architecture
   - Boarding bridge modifications and alignment
   - Evacuation system interfaces
   - Electrical and data systems integration

4. **Diagnostics and Troubleshooting** (8 hours)
   - Diagnostic tools and techniques
   - Fault tree analysis
   - Interface to digital twin and DPP
   - Escalation procedures

5. **Assessment** (4 hours)
   - Comprehensive written exam (80% pass required)
   - Practical troubleshooting scenarios
   - Safety procedure validation

**Certification**: Airport Technical Specialist Certificate (valid 1 year)

**Delivery**: Specialized training centers with full infrastructure mockups

**Recurrent Training**: Annual (16 hours) - advanced topics and technology updates

---

### 3.4 Service Engineers (Expert Level)

**Target Audience**: L2 support engineers, infrastructure specialists

**Duration**: 80 hours (intensive theory, practical, and field training)

**Content Modules**:
1. **Foundation and Advanced Content** (Airport Technical curriculum - accelerated, 24 hours)
2. **System Integration** (16 hours)
   - Complete BWB infrastructure ecosystem
   - Interface standards and specifications
   - Certification requirements and compliance
   - Design intent and engineering principles

3. **Advanced Diagnostics** (16 hours)
   - Complex failure analysis
   - Root cause investigation techniques
   - Interface with OEM engineering (L3)
   - Digital twin advanced features

4. **Service Management** (8 hours)
   - Incident management procedures
   - SLA obligations and reporting
   - Customer communication
   - Documentation requirements

5. **Specialized Topics** (12 hours)
   - H₂ safety systems deep dive
   - CO₂ battery technology and thermal management
   - Evacuation system certification and testing
   - Emerging technologies and roadmap

6. **Field Training** (4 hours)
   - Supervised field assignments
   - Mentoring by senior engineers

**Certification**: Service Engineer Certificate (valid 1 year)

**Delivery**: OEM training facility with comprehensive labs and field assignments

**Recurrent Training**: Annual (24 hours) - advanced troubleshooting, new technologies

---

### 3.5 Safety Officers and Emergency Responders

**Target Audience**: Airport fire rescue, safety coordinators

**Duration**: 16 hours

**Content Modules**:
1. **H₂ Safety and Emergency Response** (8 hours)
   - H₂ properties, hazards, and risks
   - Leak detection and monitoring
   - Emergency shutdown procedures
   - Fire suppression and rescue procedures
   - Evacuation coordination

2. **BWB-Specific Emergency Procedures** (6 hours)
   - Aircraft evacuation routes and interface points
   - CO₂ battery incidents and response
   - Coordination with airline and ground handling
   - Communication and command structure

3. **Drills and Simulations** (2 hours)
   - Tabletop exercises
   - Field drills (if available)

**Certification**: BWB Emergency Response Certificate (valid 1 year)

**Delivery**: Regional training with coordination from local emergency services

**Recurrent Training**: Annual (8 hours) + quarterly drills

## 4. Training Delivery Methods

### 4.1 E-Learning Platform

**Advantages**: Self-paced, scalable, cost-effective

**Content Types**:
- Interactive modules with simulations
- Video demonstrations
- Knowledge checks and assessments
- Virtual reality (VR) experiences for safety scenarios

**Use Cases**:
- Awareness training
- Theory components of operational training
- Recurrent training refreshers
- Just-in-time performance support

### 4.2 Classroom Training

**Advantages**: Expert instruction, discussion, networking

**Content Types**:
- Instructor-led presentations
- Group exercises and case studies
- Q&A and knowledge sharing
- Assessment and evaluation

**Use Cases**:
- Advanced technical content
- Regulatory and compliance topics
- Scenario-based learning

### 4.3 Hands-On Training

**Advantages**: Practical skill development, realistic experience

**Equipment and Facilities**:
- H₂ refueling mockup stations
- CO₂ battery docking simulators
- Gate interface systems
- Diagnostic tools and test equipment

**Use Cases**:
- Operational certification
- Troubleshooting practice
- Emergency procedure rehearsals

### 4.4 On-the-Job Training (OJT)

**Advantages**: Real-world experience, mentoring, context-specific

**Structure**:
- Structured task-based progression
- Supervised performance
- Mentor feedback and coaching
- Sign-off by qualified assessor

**Use Cases**:
- Service engineer field training
- Competency validation
- Role transition (e.g., L1 to L2)

## 7. Certification and Qualification

### 7.1 Certification Levels

| Certificate | Validity | Recurrent | Scope |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Awareness** | 2 years | E-learning refresh | General airport staff |
| **Ground Handling Technician** | 1 year | 8 hours | Operational roles |
| **Airport Technical Specialist** | 1 year | 16 hours | Maintenance staff |
| **Service Engineer** | 1 year | 24 hours | L2 support |
| **Emergency Response** | 1 year | 8 hours + drills | Safety and rescue |

### 7.2 Qualification Process

1. **Prerequisites**: Confirm baseline qualifications (e.g., technical education, experience)
2. **Training**: Complete required course hours
3. **Assessment**: Pass written and practical examinations
4. **Field Validation**: Supervised OJT (for operational/expert levels)
5. **Certification Issued**: Digital certificate with unique ID
6. **Registration**: Certification tracked in training management system

### 7.3 Recertification

- Complete recurrent training before expiration
- Pass refresher assessments
- Maintain performance standards (no major safety violations)
- If expired >6 months: Re-qualification required (full course)

## 8. Special Training Programs

### 8.1 Train-the-Trainer

**Purpose**: Develop in-house instructors at airlines and airports

**Duration**: 40 hours (training skills + BWB technical content)

**Eligibility**: Experienced technical personnel with strong communication skills

**Certification**: Instructor Certificate (allows delivery of Awareness and Ground Handling courses)

### 8.2 Drills and Simulations

**H₂ Emergency Drills**:
- Leak detection and response
- Emergency shutdown
- Fire suppression
- Evacuation coordination

**Frequency**: Quarterly (major airports), Semi-annually (regional airports)

**Evacuation Rehearsals**:
- Full-scale passenger evacuation exercises
- Coordination with emergency services
- Interface with boarding bridges and evacuation routes

**Frequency**: Annually

### 8.3 Technology Updates

**Ad-hoc Training**: When significant changes occur
- New equipment introduction
- Software/firmware updates
- Regulatory changes
- Safety bulletins

**Delivery**: Technical bulletins + e-learning modules + optional webinars

## 10. Performance Measurement

### 10.1 Training Effectiveness Metrics

- **Course Completion Rate**: ≥95%
- **First-Time Pass Rate**: ≥90% (theory), ≥95% (practical)
- **Student Satisfaction**: ≥4.5/5.0
- **Field Performance**: Post-training error rate <2%

### 10.2 Continuous Improvement

**Feedback Collection**:
- End-of-course evaluations
- Post-deployment surveys (3-6 months)
- Supervisor assessments
- Incident analysis (training gaps)

**Annual Review**:
- Curriculum effectiveness
- Training delivery quality
- Resource adequacy
- Technology and content updates
