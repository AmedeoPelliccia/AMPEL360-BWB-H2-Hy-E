# 85-00-10-007 Interface Standardization and Third-Party Certifications

## Document Information

- **Document ID**: 85-00-10-007
- **Title**: Interface Standardization and Third-Party Certifications
- **Version**: 1.0
- **Date**: 2025-11-21
- **Status**: Draft
- **Category**: Certification
- **ATA Chapter**: 85 - Infrastructure Interface Standards

## Purpose

This document consolidates the approach to interface standardization and third-party certification pathways for **ATA 85 – Infrastructure Interface Standards**. It addresses how airport-side infrastructure, hydrogen suppliers, GSE providers, and other third parties achieve certification or approval for compatibility with AMPEL360 BWB aircraft.

---

## Scope

This document covers:

1. **Interface Standardization Strategy**: Development and adoption of industry standards for interfaces
2. **Airport Infrastructure Certification**: How airports certify/approve infrastructure for AMPEL360 operations
3. **Hydrogen Supplier Certification**: H₂ production and fuelling infrastructure approvals
4. **GSE Provider Certification**: Ground support equipment compatibility certification
5. **Third-Party Conformity Assessment**: Independent verification of interface compliance
6. **Mutual Recognition**: Leveraging existing certifications and approvals

## Interface Standardization Strategy

### Objectives

1. **Industry Adoption**: Create industry-wide standards for interfaces, not proprietary solutions
2. **Interoperability**: Enable multiple airports, suppliers, and GSE providers
3. **Scalability**: Facilitate rapid deployment across global airport network
4. **Certification Efficiency**: Standardized interfaces simplify certification for all parties
5. **Innovation**: Standards evolve to incorporate technology improvements

### Standardization Bodies

#### Aviation Standards

**SAE International – Aerospace Standards**
- **SAE AS6858**: Hydrogen fuel servicing for aircraft (under development)
- **SAE AIR6464**: Aircraft hydrogen fuel system safety (under development)
- **Engagement**: AMPEL360 participates in committee development

**EUROCAE / RTCA**
- Potential development of interface communication protocol standards
- Electronic data exchange standards

**IATA – International Air Transport Association**
- Ground Operations Manual (IGOM) updates
- Airport Handling Manual (AHM) updates
- Engagement through airline and airport working groups

#### Hydrogen Standards

**ISO Technical Committee 197 – Hydrogen Technologies**
- **[ISO 19880](https://www.iso.org/standard/71940.html)** series: Gaseous hydrogen fuelling stations
- **ISO 19881**: Hydrogen fuel quality
- **ISO/TR 15916**: Basic considerations for safety of hydrogen systems
- **Engagement**: Participation in working groups, propose aviation-specific extensions

**IEC Technical Committee 105 – Fuel Cell Technologies**
- Standards for hydrogen fuel cell systems
- Potential relevance for on-board H₂ systems

#### Energy and Charging Standards

**IEC Standards**
- **IEC 62196**: Plugs and connectors for electric vehicles (reference for battery charging)
- **IEC 61851**: Electric vehicle conductive charging system (by analogy)

**SAE Standards**
- Adapt EV charging standards for aviation use
- Develop aircraft-specific charging connector standards

### AMPEL360 Standards Development Approach

**Phase 1: Internal Specification (Months 0-12)**
- Develop detailed interface specifications based on requirements
- Internal validation through design and prototyping
- Prepare draft for industry standardization

**Phase 2: Industry Consultation (Months 12-24)**
- Share specifications with airports, suppliers, GSE manufacturers
- Incorporate feedback and lessons from prototyping
- Build industry support for standardization

**Phase 3: Standardization Body Submission (Months 18-30)**
- Submit to appropriate standards bodies (SAE, ISO, IATA)
- Participate in committee review and balloting process
- Advocate for timely adoption

**Phase 4: Standard Publication (Months 30-42)**
- Standard published by standards body
- Industry implements standard
- AMPEL360 Type Certificate references standard

**Phase 5: Standard Maintenance (Ongoing)**
- Participate in standard revision and improvement
- Incorporate operational experience
- Support industry adoption and compliance

## Airport Infrastructure Certification

### Airport-Side Certification Scope

Airports must certify/approve infrastructure for:
1. **Hydrogen Refuelling Infrastructure**
   - H₂ production or delivery systems
   - Storage tanks and piping
   - Refuelling dispensers and safety systems
   
2. **Battery Charging Infrastructure**
   - High-voltage power supply
   - Charging stations and connectors
   - Power management systems

3. **Boarding/Evacuation Infrastructure**
   - Boarding bridge modifications or new bridges
   - Terminal interface modifications
   - Evacuation routes and equipment

4. **GSE Modifications**
   - Enhanced GSE for H₂-aware operations
   - Power and data interface-capable GSE

### Airport Certification Process

**Step 1: Airport Interest and Assessment**
- Airport expresses interest in AMPEL360 operations
- AMPEL360 provides Interface Control Documents (ICDs)
- Airport assesses feasibility and investment requirements

**Step 2: Infrastructure Design**
- Airport develops infrastructure design based on ICDs
- Design review against interface specifications
- AMPEL360 provides design consultation

**Step 3: Local Authority Approvals**
- Building permits
- Fire safety approvals (especially for H₂)
- Environmental permits
- Aviation authority approval (if required)

**Step 4: Construction and Installation**
- Airport constructs infrastructure
- Installation inspection against specifications

**Step 5: Conformity Verification**
- Interface compatibility testing
- Safety system validation
- Operational readiness assessment
- **AMPEL360 Conformity Assessment** (see below)

**Step 6: Operational Approval**
- Airport operational procedures approved
- Ground crew training and qualification
- Emergency response procedures validated

**Step 7: Service Entry**
- Infrastructure declared operational
- Added to AMPEL360 approved airport list
- Ongoing conformity monitoring

### AMPEL360 Airport Conformity Assessment

AMPEL360 conducts conformity assessment to verify airport infrastructure compliance:

**Assessment Components**:
1. **Document Review**: Design documentation and approval records
2. **Physical Inspection**: On-site inspection of installed infrastructure
3. **Interface Testing**: Compatibility testing with aircraft mockup or test article
4. **Procedure Validation**: Review and exercise of operational procedures
5. **Safety Systems Check**: Verify safety system functionality

**Conformity Certificate**: AMPEL360 issues certificate declaring airport infrastructure compliant with interface standards

## Hydrogen Supplier Certification

### H₂ Supplier Scope

Hydrogen suppliers must demonstrate:
1. **Fuel Quality**: H₂ purity and quality per specifications
2. **Production Safety**: Safe H₂ production or delivery operations
3. **Storage and Distribution**: Compliant storage and distribution systems
4. **Fuelling Operations**: Safe refuelling procedures and equipment
5. **Emergency Response**: Adequate emergency response capabilities

### H₂ Supplier Certification Path

**Option 1: ISO 19880 Compliance**
- Supplier demonstrates compliance with [ISO 19880-1](https://www.iso.org/standard/71940.html)
- Third-party conformity assessment
- AMPEL360 accepts ISO certification as baseline

**Option 2: AMPEL360 Qualification**
- Supplier does not have ISO certification
- AMPEL360 conducts supplier qualification audit
- Audit covers safety, quality, operations, emergency response
- Qualification valid for defined period (e.g., 2 years)

**Option 3: Aviation Authority Approval**
- Some jurisdictions may require aviation authority approval for H₂ aviation fuelling
- AMPEL360 works with supplier to obtain approval
- Authority approval accepted by AMPEL360

### Fuel Quality Verification

**Fuel Specification**: Defined in interface requirements
- H₂ purity (e.g., 99.97% minimum)
- Moisture content limits
- Contaminant limits (particulates, hydrocarbons, etc.)

**Quality Assurance**:
- Supplier provides Certificate of Analysis (CoA) per batch or delivery
- Periodic sampling and testing by AMPEL360 or third party
- On-site fuel quality monitoring at refuelling point (per [ISO 19880-8](https://www.iso.org/standard/67651.html))

**Non-Conformance**:
- Supplier notifies AMPEL360 of any fuel quality excursions
- AMPEL360 assesses impact on aircraft operations
- Corrective action and re-verification required

## GSE Provider Certification

### GSE Compatibility Requirements

Ground Support Equipment must demonstrate:
1. **Power Interface Compatibility**: Electrical connector and power quality
2. **Data Interface Compatibility**: Communication protocol compliance
3. **H₂ Awareness**: GSE safe to operate near H₂ refuelling operations
4. **Electromagnetic Compatibility**: No interference with aircraft systems

### GSE Certification Path

**Path 1: Standards-Based Certification**
- GSE complies with published interface standards (SAE, ISO)
- Third-party test lab certifies compliance
- AMPEL360 accepts standards-based certification

**Path 2: AMPEL360 Type Approval**
- GSE manufacturer submits for AMPEL360 type approval
- AMPEL360 conducts compatibility testing
- Approval granted for specific GSE model/configuration
- Type approval valid for defined period or until design changes

**Path 3: Airport-Specific Qualification**
- Airport GSE tested for compatibility with AMPEL360 at specific airport
- Qualification limited to that airport's equipment
- Used when standardized certification not yet available

### GSE Interface Testing

**Power Interface Tests**:
- Voltage regulation and quality
- Current capacity
- Connector mating and retention
- Ground fault protection

**Data Interface Tests**:
- Protocol conformance
- Data integrity
- Error handling
- Communication latency

**Safety Tests**:
- Electrical safety (ground continuity, insulation resistance)
- EMC/EMI
- Fail-safe behavior

**Test Location**: AMPEL360 test facility or independent test lab

### GSE Approved Equipment List (AEL)

AMPEL360 maintains Approved Equipment List:
- Lists approved GSE by manufacturer, model, configuration
- Specifies interface types supported (power, data, etc.)
- Notes any limitations or special operating procedures
- Updated as new equipment is approved

**Distribution**: Available to airports, airlines, ground handlers

## Third-Party Conformity Assessment

### Conformity Assessment Bodies (CABs)

Independent organizations that assess compliance with standards:
- **Accreditation**: CABs accredited per ISO/IEC 17065 (product certification) or 17020 (inspection)
- **Scope**: Assessment of interface products, infrastructure, or operations
- **Recognition**: AMPEL360 recognizes CABs meeting accreditation requirements

### CAB Services for ATA 85 Interfaces

**Product Certification**:
- H₂ refuelling connectors and hoses
- Battery charging connectors
- GSE interface components
- Certify compliance with interface standards

**Infrastructure Inspection**:
- Airport H₂ infrastructure
- Battery charging stations
- Boarding interface installations
- Verify conformity with specifications

**Quality System Assessment**:
- H₂ supplier quality systems
- GSE manufacturer quality systems
- Airport operations quality systems

### AMPEL360 CAB Recognition Process

**Step 1: CAB Application**
- CAB submits application for recognition
- Provides accreditation certificates and scope

**Step 2: AMPEL360 Review**
- Review CAB credentials and competence
- Assess scope relevance to ATA 85 interfaces
- Review CAB procedures and quality system

**Step 3: Pilot Assessment**
- CAB conducts pilot assessment under AMPEL360 observation
- AMPEL360 evaluates CAB performance

**Step 4: Recognition**
- AMPEL360 grants recognition for defined scope
- CAB added to recognized CAB list
- Recognition valid for defined period (e.g., 3 years)

**Step 5: Ongoing Monitoring**
- AMPEL360 monitors CAB performance
- Periodic audits of CAB operations
- Renewal of recognition

### Use of CAB Certifications

AMPEL360 accepts CAB certifications as evidence of compliance:
- Product certifications used in compliance matrix
- Infrastructure inspection reports support airport conformity assessment
- Quality system certifications support supplier qualification

This reduces AMPEL360 direct assessment burden and leverages independent expertise.

## Mutual Recognition Agreements

### Purpose

Mutual recognition agreements (MRAs) allow:
- Acceptance of certifications/approvals from other parties
- Reduced duplicate testing and inspection
- Streamlined multi-jurisdiction approvals

### MRA Types

**Aviation Authority MRAs**:
- EASA-FAA bilateral agreement
- Other bilateral aviation agreements
- AMPEL360 leverages existing MRAs for aircraft certification

**Industry Standards MRAs**:
- SAE standards recognized by ISO and vice versa
- Regional variations (e.g., EN standards in Europe)
- AMPEL360 designs to most stringent version

**Conformity Assessment MRAs**:
- Accreditation bodies mutual recognition (e.g., IAF MLA)
- Test lab accreditation (ISO/IEC 17025)
- AMPEL360 accepts accredited lab results

### AMPEL360-Specific MRAs

**Airport Agreements**:
- AMPEL360 and airport agree on mutual acceptance criteria
- Airport certifies infrastructure per agreed standard
- AMPEL360 accepts airport certification

**Supplier Agreements**:
- AMPEL360 and H₂/GSE suppliers agree on conformity criteria
- Supplier self-certification or third-party certification
- AMPEL360 accepts with periodic verification

**Airline Agreements**:
- Airlines operating AMPEL360 aircraft
- Acceptance of airline-qualified GSE and procedures
- Mutual support for operational approvals

## Interface Control Documents (ICDs)

### Purpose

ICDs define interfaces between aircraft and infrastructure:
- Precise specifications for physical, electrical, data interfaces
- Operational requirements and procedures
- Safety requirements and constraints
- Form part of certification basis

### ICD Development

**ICD Structure**:
1. **Introduction**: Scope, applicability, references
2. **Interface Description**: Physical, electrical, data specifications
3. **Performance Requirements**: Functional requirements at interface
4. **Safety Requirements**: Hazards, safety functions, protection
5. **Operational Procedures**: Normal and emergency procedures
6. **Conformity Criteria**: How to verify compliance
7. **Testing and Validation**: Required tests and acceptance criteria

**ICD Ownership**:
- AMPEL360 owns and maintains ICDs
- Changes managed through I-CCB-85
- Distributed to airports, suppliers, authorities

**ICD Versions**:
- Controlled versioning
- Backward compatibility considerations
- Migration plans for version changes

### Key ICDs for ATA 85

**ICD-85-H2-001: Hydrogen Refuelling Interface**
- Aircraft receptacle specifications
- Ground dispenser requirements
- Refuelling procedures and safety systems
- Based on SAE AS6858 (when available)

**ICD-85-BAT-001: Battery Charging Interface**
- Charging connector specifications
- Power and communication requirements
- Charging protocols and profiles
- Safety and emergency procedures

**ICD-85-GSE-001: Ground Power Interface**
- Power connector specifications
- Voltage, frequency, and power quality
- Connection and disconnection procedures

**ICD-85-GSE-002: Ground Data Interface**
- Data connector specifications
- Communication protocols (ARINC, CAN, Ethernet)
- Data exchange formats and timing

**ICD-85-PAX-001: Boarding Bridge Interface**
- Door dimensions and clearances
- Boarding bridge compatibility requirements
- Operational procedures

**ICD-85-PAX-002: Evacuation Slide Interface**
- Slide deployment zones
- Ground clearance requirements
- Emergency lighting and signage

## Standardization Roadmap

### Near-Term (0-2 years)

- Publish internal interface specifications
- Share with launch airports and suppliers
- Submit proposals to SAE, ISO, IATA
- Begin standards development process

### Mid-Term (2-4 years)

- Standards committees review and ballot
- Pilot implementations at partner airports
- Refine standards based on operational experience
- Achieve standards publication

### Long-Term (4+ years)

- Industry-wide adoption of standards
- Multiple suppliers and airports certified
- Standards incorporated into regulations
- Continuous improvement based on fleet experience

## Success Metrics

Interface standardization successful when:

1. **Industry Standards Published**: SAE, ISO, or IATA standards for key interfaces
2. **Multi-Supplier Market**: At least 3 independent suppliers for each interface type
3. **Airport Adoption**: At least 10 airports certified for AMPEL360 operations
4. **Certification Efficiency**: Reduced time and cost for airport/supplier certification
5. **Interoperability**: Demonstrated compatibility across multiple equipment providers
