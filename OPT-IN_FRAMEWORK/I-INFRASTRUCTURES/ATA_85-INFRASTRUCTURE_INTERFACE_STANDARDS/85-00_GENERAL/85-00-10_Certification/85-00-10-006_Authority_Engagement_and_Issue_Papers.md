## Purpose

This document establishes the framework for interaction with certification authorities during the development and certification of **ATA 85 – Infrastructure Interface Standards**. It defines processes for meetings, issue papers, special conditions, equivalent safety findings, and general authority correspondence.

---

## Scope

This document covers engagement with:

- **Primary Authorities**: EASA and FAA
- **Secondary Authorities**: National aviation authorities in target markets (e.g., Transport Canada, CAAC)
- **Related Authorities**: Energy regulators, infrastructure agencies, fire safety authorities (as needed)

The document addresses:
1. Authority engagement strategy and schedule
2. Meeting protocols and documentation
3. Issue paper development and submission
4. Special condition proposals
5. Equivalent safety findings
6. Findings and corrective action management

## Authority Engagement Strategy

### Engagement Objectives

1. **Early Alignment**: Establish certification basis and approach early in development
2. **Risk Mitigation**: Identify and resolve novel or complex issues proactively
3. **Trust Building**: Develop collaborative relationship with authorities
4. **Efficient Process**: Minimize findings and delays through proactive engagement
5. **Mutual Understanding**: Ensure authorities understand novel BWB and infrastructure interface technologies

### Engagement Principles

- **Transparency**: Share information openly and honestly
- **Proactive**: Raise issues early before they become problems
- **Responsive**: Address authority questions and concerns promptly
- **Documented**: Maintain clear records of all interactions
- **Collaborative**: Work with authorities as partners, not adversaries

## Meeting Types and Protocols

### Pre-Application Meeting

**Purpose**: Establish certification basis and approach before formal application

**Timing**: 18-24 months before Type Certificate application

**Agenda**:
- Aircraft and interface system overview
- Proposed certification basis
- Novel or non-standard aspects
- Proposed special conditions or equivalent safety findings
- Schedule and milestones
- Questions and initial feedback

**Deliverables**:
- Pre-application meeting package
- Authority meeting minutes
- Action item list

**Follow-up**: Address action items and schedule next meeting

### Quarterly Progress Meetings

**Purpose**: Regular updates on certification progress

**Timing**: Every 3 months during active certification phase

**Agenda**:
- Progress since last meeting
- Upcoming milestones
- Emerging issues or concerns
- Test results and analysis findings
- Open findings status
- Schedule updates

**Deliverables**:
- Progress presentation
- Meeting minutes
- Updated action item list

### Design Reviews

**Purpose**: Authority review of interface designs at key milestones

**Timing**: At design freeze for each interface type

**Design Review Topics**:
1. **H₂ Refuelling Interface Design Review**
   - Aircraft receptacle design
   - Safety systems and interlocks
   - Refuelling procedures
   
2. **CO₂ Battery Charging Interface Design Review**
   - Charging connector design
   - Battery management system interface
   - Safety and protection systems

3. **GSE Interface Design Review**
   - Power and data interface specifications
   - Compatibility with airport GSE
   - Communication protocols

4. **Boarding/Evacuation Interface Design Review**
   - BWB door configurations
   - Evacuation slide systems
   - Boarding bridge interfaces

**Deliverables**:
- Design review package (drawings, specifications, analyses)
- Meeting minutes
- Authority comments and findings

### Witness Test Events

**Purpose**: Authority observation of key certification tests

**Required Witness Events**:
- **Full-Scale Evacuation Demonstration** ([CS-25.807](https://www.easa.europa.eu/document-library/certification-specifications/cs-25-amendment-27))

**Recommended Witness Events**:
- H₂ refuelling system test
- Battery charging system test
- Emergency scenario testing

**Protocol**:
- Notify authority 60 days in advance
- Provide test plan and procedures 30 days in advance
- Conduct pre-test briefing
- Authority observes and documents
- Post-test debrief and preliminary findings
- Formal test report submitted later

**Deliverables**:
- Test notification
- Test plan and procedures
- Authority witness statement
- Test report

### Findings Review Meetings

**Purpose**: Review and resolve authority findings

**Timing**: As needed when findings are issued

**Agenda**:
- Review finding details and authority concerns
- Present proposed corrective actions
- Discuss acceptability of approach
- Agree on timeline for closure

**Deliverables**:
- Finding closure plan
- Meeting minutes
- Updated finding status

## Issue Papers

### Purpose and Scope

Issue papers address:
- Novel designs or technologies not covered by existing regulations
- Interpretations of regulatory requirements
- Proposed alternative means of compliance
- Special conditions or equivalent safety findings
- Significant changes to certification basis

### Issue Paper Development Process

**Step 1: Issue Identification**
- Identify novel aspect or regulatory gap
- Assess certification impact
- Determine if issue paper needed

**Step 2: Technical Development**
- Research applicable regulations and precedents
- Develop technical position and rationale
- Identify proposed means of compliance
- Conduct supporting analyses or studies

**Step 3: Internal Review**
- Review by certification team
- Review by engineering and safety teams
- Review by I-CCB-85
- Approval by certification lead

**Step 4: Authority Submission**
- Format per authority requirements
- Submit with supporting documentation
- Request meeting if needed

**Step 5: Authority Engagement**
- Respond to authority questions
- Provide additional information as requested
- Negotiate agreed position

**Step 6: Closure**
- Document agreed position
- Update certification basis or compliance approach
- Update compliance matrix

### Issue Paper Structure

**Standard Sections**:
1. **Executive Summary**: Issue and proposed resolution
2. **Background**: Context and why issue is arising
3. **Regulatory Analysis**: Applicable regulations and interpretation
4. **Technical Discussion**: Detailed technical analysis
5. **Proposed Approach**: Recommended means of compliance
6. **Safety Justification**: Why approach is safe and acceptable
7. **Precedents**: Similar issues in other programs (if applicable)
8. **Conclusion**: Request for authority agreement

**Template**: Developed for each issue, following authority preferences

**Tracking Fields**:

## Special Conditions

### When Special Conditions Are Needed

Special conditions required when:
- Novel design feature not addressed by existing regulations
- Regulations written for conventional configurations don't apply well
- New technology requires specific certification requirements

### Special Condition Proposal Process

1. **Develop Proposal**: Draft special condition language and justification
2. **Internal Review**: Review by certification and legal teams
3. **Submit to Authority**: Formal proposal with technical justification
4. **Authority Review**: Authority drafts special condition (may differ from proposal)
5. **Public Comment**: Authority publishes for public comment (may be required)
6. **Finalization**: Special condition issued by authority
7. **Compliance**: Demonstrate compliance with special condition

### Anticipated Special Conditions

**SC-85-001: Hydrogen Fuel System**
- Special conditions for aircraft hydrogen fuel storage, distribution, and refuelling
- Based on [CS-25.981](https://www.easa.europa.eu/document-library/certification-specifications/cs-25-amendment-27) with H₂-specific requirements
- Addresses leak detection, ventilation, ignition prevention, emergency procedures

**SC-85-002: High Voltage Battery Charging**
- Special conditions for high voltage battery charging interface
- Addresses electrical safety, thermal management, communication protocols
- Based on automotive standards adapted for aviation

## Equivalent Safety Findings

### Purpose

Equivalent Safety Findings (ESF) allow:
- Alternative means of compliance with regulations
- Demonstration that approach provides "equivalent level of safety"
- Flexibility for novel designs while maintaining safety

### ESF Development Process

1. **Identify Need**: Regulation difficult to meet with novel design, but safety can be demonstrated by other means
2. **Develop Technical Case**: Show how alternative approach provides equivalent safety
3. **Safety Analysis**: Detailed analysis demonstrating equivalent or better safety
4. **Submit Proposal**: Formal ESF request to authority
5. **Authority Evaluation**: Authority reviews technical case and safety justification
6. **ESF Issuance**: Authority issues finding of equivalent safety
7. **Compliance**: Demonstrate compliance with approved alternative approach

### ESF Tracking

**Tracking Fields**:

## Findings Management

### Finding Types

**Type 1: Findings Requiring Immediate Action**
- Safety-critical issues
- Non-compliance with certification basis
- Require resolution before TC issuance

**Type 2: Findings Requiring Action Before EIS**
- Important but not safety-critical
- Can be resolved during production phase
- Must be closed before Entry Into Service

**Type 3: Observations**
- Recommendations or suggestions
- Not mandatory but should be considered
- May be addressed post-EIS

### Finding Response Process

**Step 1: Finding Receipt and Review**
- Document finding details
- Assess impact on certification and schedule
- Assign owner and priority

**Step 2: Root Cause Analysis**
- Determine why finding occurred
- Identify systemic issues if applicable

**Step 3: Corrective Action Development**
- Develop corrective action plan
- Identify verification method
- Estimate schedule impact

**Step 4: CCB Review**
- Present to I-CCB-85
- Approve corrective action approach
- Allocate resources

**Step 5: Implementation**
- Execute corrective action
- Document changes (design, test, analysis)
- Update compliance matrix

**Step 6: Verification**
- Verify corrective action effectiveness
- Generate evidence package

**Step 7: Closure Request**
- Submit finding closure package to authority
- Include corrective action description and evidence
- Request authority acceptance

**Step 8: Closure**
- Authority reviews closure package
- May request additional information
- Issues finding closure notice

### Finding Tracking

Integrated into [ASSETS/Authorities/85-00-10-A-302_Issue_Papers_Tracker.xlsx](./ASSETS/Authorities/)

**Tracking Fields**:
- Finding ID and description
- Finding type (Type 1, 2, or 3)
- Interface affected
- Issue date
- Owner and status
- Corrective action summary
- Target closure date
- Closure date and reference

## Multi-Authority Coordination

### Harmonization Strategy

- **Primary Authority**: EASA (European certification)
- **Secondary Authority**: FAA (US validation)
- **Approach**: Seek harmonized requirements and mutual acceptance

**Coordination Activities**:
- Joint meetings when possible
- Share information with both authorities simultaneously
- Highlight any differences in interpretation or requirements
- Negotiate mutual acceptance agreements

### Market-Specific Authorities

For other markets (Canada, China, etc.):
- Assess market entry priority
- Engage early to understand specific requirements
- Plan validation activities
- Leverage EASA/FAA certifications where possible

## Success Metrics

Authority engagement is successful when:

1. **No Surprise Findings**: Issues identified and resolved proactively
2. **Timely Responses**: Authority reviews completed on schedule
3. **Issue Paper Agreement**: All issue papers resolved to mutual satisfaction
4. **Special Conditions Issued**: On schedule with no contentious requirements
5. **Efficient Findings Closure**: Findings closed promptly with minimal rework
6. **Authority Confidence**: Authorities express confidence in program and certification approach
