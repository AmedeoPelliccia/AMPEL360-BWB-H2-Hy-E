# 03-00-02-007 — Safety Interfaces with Operations and Maintenance

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **safety interfaces and coordination mechanisms** between Ground Support Equipment (GSE) safety, flight operations, turnaround operations, maintenance, and airport authorities under [ATA 03 – Support Information GSE](https://www.ata.org/resources/specifications/ispec2200).

It establishes:

- Interface points and responsibilities between GSE safety and other operational areas
- Communication protocols and information sharing
- Coordination mechanisms for safe operations
- Escalation procedures for safety conflicts
- Joint safety activities and reviews

---

## 2. Interface Framework

### 2.1 Key Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Primary Interface | Safety Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Flight Operations** | Turnaround coordination, aircraft servicing | Safe aircraft ground handling, FOD prevention |
| **Ground Operations** | Ramp/apron operations, GSE allocation | Safe GSE operations, traffic management |
| **Maintenance (Aircraft)** | Aircraft defect reporting, ground power | Safe aircraft access, defect communication |
| **Maintenance (GSE)** | GSE serviceability, inspections | Maintain GSE in safe operating condition |
| **Airport Authority** | Ramp safety, emergency response | Compliance with airport safety rules |
| **Air Traffic Control (ATC)** | Taxiway crossings, ramp clearances | Coordination of GSE movements near active areas |
| **Fire/Rescue Services** | Emergency response, H₂ safety | H₂ incident response, fire suppression |
| **Fueling Operations** | H₂ refueling, Jet-A refueling | Safe fuel handling, ignition source control |

## 3. Flight Operations Interface

### 3.1 Turnaround Coordination

**Objective**: Ensure safe and efficient aircraft turnaround operations.

**Interface Points:**
- **Pre-Arrival**: GSE staging, ramp clearance, equipment readiness
- **Aircraft Arrival**: Marshalling, chocking, ground power connection
- **Turnaround**: Passenger boarding, cargo loading, refueling, catering
- **Departure**: Equipment disconnection, aircraft release, ramp clearance

**Safety Coordination:**
- **Turnaround Safety Briefing**: Conducted by Ground Operations Manager before each turnaround
  - Hazards identified (weather, special cargo, MEL items)
  - Task allocation and sequencing
  - Communication protocols and emergency procedures
- **Flight Crew Communication**: Direct communication between GSE supervisor and flight crew
  - Aircraft serviceability and defects
  - Special requirements (disabled passengers, special cargo)
  - Departure readiness and equipment status

### 3.2 Aircraft Ground Handling Safety

**GSE Responsibilities:**
- **Aircraft Protection**: Prevent FOD, damage to aircraft surfaces, doors, and control surfaces
- **Exclusion Zones**: Maintain clearances around aircraft (see [03-00-02-003_Operational_Safety_Rules_GSE.md](./03-00-02-003_Operational_Safety_Rules_GSE.md))
- **Wing Walking**: Provide wing walkers during aircraft towing and pushback
- **Chocking**: Ensure chocks in place before connecting any GSE to aircraft

**Flight Operations Responsibilities:**
- **Aircraft Configuration**: Ensure aircraft is configured for ground operations (parking brake, APU shutdown)
- **Defect Communication**: Inform GSE of any aircraft defects affecting ground handling
- **Authorization**: Authorize GSE connection to aircraft systems (electrical, pneumatic, hydraulic)

### 3.3 Foreign Object Debris (FOD) Prevention

**Joint Responsibility:**
- **FOD Walks**: Daily FOD walks of ramp/apron areas conducted by GSE and flight operations personnel
- **FOD Reporting**: Immediate reporting of FOD to ramp control and removal from operational area
- **Tool Control**: GSE maintenance personnel follow strict tool control procedures
- **Debris Containment**: GSE operations (e.g., catering) ensure no loose items left near aircraft

## 4. Maintenance Interface

### 4.1 GSE Maintenance Interface

**Objective**: Ensure GSE equipment is maintained in safe operating condition.

**Interface Points:**
- **Daily Inspections**: GSE operators conduct pre-operation inspections, report defects to GSE maintenance
- **Scheduled Maintenance**: GSE maintenance follows manufacturer-recommended maintenance schedules
- **Unserviceability**: GSE maintenance tags unserviceable equipment, removes from service until repaired
- **Return to Service**: GSE maintenance certifies equipment as serviceable after repair/maintenance

**Safety Coordination:**
- **Defect Reporting**: GSE operators report defects via electronic system or paper form
- **Critical Defects**: GSE maintenance immediately notifies GSE Safety Manager of critical safety defects
- **Safety Bulletins**: GSE maintenance reviews and implements manufacturer safety bulletins
- **Maintenance Records**: GSE maintenance maintains detailed records of all maintenance activities

### 4.2 Aircraft Maintenance Interface

**Objective**: Safe coordination between GSE operations and aircraft maintenance activities.

**Interface Points:**
- **Maintenance Zones**: Aircraft under maintenance may have special requirements (e.g., no ground power, specific towing procedures)
- **Work Permits**: GSE operations near aircraft under maintenance require work permit and coordination with maintenance supervisor
- **Defect Communication**: GSE operators report aircraft defects observed during servicing to aircraft maintenance
- **Hot Work**: GSE operations (e.g., refueling) prohibited during aircraft maintenance involving hot work (welding, cutting)

**Safety Coordination:**
- **Exclusion Zones**: Aircraft maintenance establishes exclusion zones around aircraft under maintenance, GSE operations respect zones
- **Communication**: Direct communication between GSE supervisor and aircraft maintenance supervisor before GSE operations near aircraft under maintenance
- **H₂ Operations**: H₂ refueling prohibited during aircraft maintenance unless specifically authorized by H₂ Safety Specialist and aircraft maintenance supervisor

## 5. Airport Authority Interface

### 5.1 Airport Safety Rules and Compliance

**Objective**: Ensure GSE operations comply with airport safety rules and regulations.

**Interface Points:**
- **Airport Safety Rules**: GSE operations follow airport-specific safety rules (speed limits, traffic patterns, restricted areas)
- **Airside Permits**: GSE operators hold valid airside driving permits issued by airport authority
- **Safety Inspections**: Airport authority conducts periodic safety inspections of GSE operations
- **Incident Reporting**: GSE incidents reported to airport authority as required by airport regulations

**Safety Coordination:**
- **Airport Safety Committee**: GSE Safety Manager participates in airport safety committee meetings
- **Safety Audits**: Airport authority conducts safety audits of GSE operations, findings addressed by GSE Safety Manager
- **Regulatory Changes**: Airport authority communicates regulatory changes to GSE Safety Manager for implementation
- **Emergency Response**: GSE operations coordinate with airport fire/rescue services for emergency response planning

### 5.2 Ramp and Apron Safety Management

**Joint Responsibility:**
- **Surface Maintenance**: Airport authority maintains ramp/apron surfaces (pavement, markings, lighting)
- **Hazard Reporting**: GSE personnel report surface hazards (potholes, markings faded, lighting out) to airport authority
- **Winter Operations**: Airport authority coordinates snow/ice removal, GSE operations adjust for surface conditions
- **Construction/Maintenance**: Airport authority coordinates ramp/apron construction/maintenance activities, GSE operations notified of closures and detours

## 6. Air Traffic Control (ATC) Interface

### 6.1 Taxiway and Runway Crossings

**Objective**: Prevent unauthorized GSE incursions into active taxiways and runways.

**Interface Points:**
- **ATC Clearance**: GSE operations crossing active taxiways require ATC clearance
- **Stop Bars**: GSE operations obey lighted stop bars at taxiway/runway entry points
- **Radio Communication**: GSE supervisors communicate with ATC on designated GSE frequency

**Safety Coordination:**
- **Clearance Procedures**: GSE supervisors request ATC clearance before crossing active taxiways
- **Read-Back**: GSE supervisors read back ATC clearances to confirm understanding
- **Incursion Prevention**: GSE training includes runway/taxiway incursion prevention

### 6.2 Apron Management

**Interface Points:**
- **Stand Allocation**: ATC/Ramp Control assigns aircraft stands, GSE operations stage equipment accordingly
- **Movement Coordination**: GSE movements near active taxiways coordinated with ATC/Ramp Control
- **Emergency Situations**: ATC notified immediately of GSE emergencies affecting aircraft or taxiway operations

## 7. Fire/Rescue Services Interface

### 7.1 H₂ Emergency Response Coordination

**Objective**: Ensure fire/rescue services are prepared to respond to H₂ incidents.

**Interface Points:**
- **H₂ Familiarization**: Fire/rescue personnel receive H₂ safety familiarization training
- **Emergency Response Plans**: Joint development of H₂ emergency response plans
- **Equipment Staging**: Fire/rescue equipment staged near H₂ refueling operations (within 50m buffer zone)
- **Incident Response**: Fire/rescue services respond to all H₂ incidents, GSE personnel support as directed

**Safety Coordination:**
- **Pre-Refueling Notification**: GSE supervisor notifies fire/rescue before each H₂ refueling operation
- **H₂ Detection**: GSE provides fire/rescue with H₂ detection equipment and training
- **Joint Drills**: Quarterly joint emergency response drills involving GSE and fire/rescue personnel
- **Debrief**: Post-incident debriefs jointly conducted by GSE Safety Manager and fire/rescue chief

### 7.2 Fire Response

**Interface Points:**
- **Fire Alarms**: GSE personnel activate fire alarms and notify fire/rescue of any fire
- **First Response**: GSE personnel use fire extinguishers for small fires, evacuate for large fires
- **Incident Command**: Fire/rescue assumes incident command for all fires, GSE personnel support as directed
- **Fire Drills**: GSE personnel participate in airport fire drills

## 8. Fueling Operations Interface

### 8.1 H₂ Refueling Coordination

**Objective**: Safe coordination between H₂ refueling and other GSE operations.

**Interface Points:**
- **Exclusion Zones**: H₂ refueling exclusion zones established (10m inner, 25m outer, 50m buffer), all other GSE operations excluded
- **Ignition Source Control**: All nearby GSE engines shutdown, no hot work within 25m
- **Communication**: H₂ refueling supervisor communicates with GSE supervisor and ramp control
- **Emergency Response**: All GSE personnel aware of H₂ refueling operations and emergency procedures

**Safety Coordination:**
- **Pre-Refueling Briefing**: H₂ refueling crew conducts safety briefing with nearby GSE personnel
- **Monitoring**: GSE supervisor monitors H₂ refueling operations, ensures exclusion zones respected
- **Emergency**: All GSE operations cease immediately if H₂ emergency occurs, personnel evacuate as directed

### 8.2 Jet-A Refueling Coordination

**Interface Points:**
- **Bonding**: Aircraft bonded to fuel source, GSE equipment also bonded if connected during refueling
- **Ignition Source Control**: No GSE engines running within 15m of Jet-A refueling operation
- **Communication**: Jet-A refueling operator communicates with GSE supervisor before commencing refueling
- **Spill Response**: GSE personnel assist with fuel spill response if required

## 9. Communication Protocols

### 9.1 Daily Safety Communications

- **Pre-Shift Safety Briefings**: Conducted by GSE supervisor at start of each shift
  - Daily hazards and safety reminders
  - Weather conditions and limitations
  - Special operations (VIP flights, H₂ refueling, maintenance activities)
- **Radio Communications**: GSE operations use designated radio frequency
  - Standard phraseology for clarity
  - Emergency communications on 121.5 MHz if required
- **Hand Signals**: Standard ICAO marshalling signals used for aircraft handling

### 9.2 Safety Information Sharing

- **Weekly**: GSE Safety Manager shares safety bulletins, lessons learned, and near-miss summaries with all stakeholders
- **Monthly**: Safety performance metrics shared with flight operations, maintenance, and airport authority
- **Quarterly**: Joint safety meetings with all stakeholders to review safety performance and emerging hazards
- **Annual**: Safety performance report presented to senior management and stakeholders

### 9.3 Escalation Procedures

**Safety Conflicts:**
If a safety conflict arises between GSE operations and another stakeholder:

1. **Immediate**: Stop work, secure area, prevent harm
2. **Supervisor Level**: GSE supervisor and other stakeholder's supervisor discuss and resolve
3. **Manager Level**: If unresolved, escalate to GSE Safety Manager and stakeholder's manager
4. **Senior Management**: If still unresolved, escalate to senior management for decision
5. **Stop Work Authority**: Any personnel may exercise stop work authority to prevent unsafe operations

## 10. Joint Safety Activities

### 10.1 Joint Safety Walk-Throughs

- **Monthly**: Joint safety walk-throughs of ramp/apron areas conducted by GSE Safety Manager, Ground Operations Manager, and airport authority safety representative
- **Focus**: Identify hazards, verify compliance with safety rules, assess effectiveness of controls
- **Follow-Up**: Findings documented, corrective actions assigned and tracked

### 10.2 Joint Safety Audits

- **Quarterly**: Joint safety audits of GSE operations conducted by cross-functional team
- **Scope**: Compliance with safety procedures, training records, equipment inspections, incident management
- **Reporting**: Audit report shared with all stakeholders, corrective actions tracked

### 10.3 Joint Emergency Drills

- **Quarterly**: Joint emergency response drills involving GSE personnel, fire/rescue, and other stakeholders
- **Scenarios**: H₂ leak, fire, medical emergency, aircraft incident
- **Debrief**: Post-drill debrief to identify lessons learned and improvement opportunities

### 10.4 Safety Improvement Initiatives

- **Continuous Improvement**: Joint safety improvement initiatives to address systemic issues and emerging hazards
- **Examples**: New technology implementation (e.g., collision avoidance systems), process improvements (e.g., automated defect reporting), infrastructure upgrades (e.g., improved lighting)
