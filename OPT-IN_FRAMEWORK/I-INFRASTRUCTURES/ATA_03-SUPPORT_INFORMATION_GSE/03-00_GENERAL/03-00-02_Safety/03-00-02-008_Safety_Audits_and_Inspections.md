# 03-00-02-008 — Safety Audits and Inspections for GSE

## 1. Purpose

This document defines **periodic safety audits, inspections, checklists, and compliance follow-up** for Ground Support Equipment (GSE) operations under [ATA 03 – Support Information GSE](https://www.ata.org/resources/specifications/ispec2200).

It establishes:

- Safety audit and inspection schedules
- Audit/inspection scopes and methodologies
- Checklists and assessment criteria
- Finding classification and corrective action processes
- Compliance verification and follow-up

---

## 2. Audit and Inspection Framework

### 2.1 Hierarchy of Audits and Inspections

| Level | Type | Frequency | Scope | Conducted By |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | Daily Pre-Operation Checks | Daily | Individual GSE equipment | GSE Operators |
| **Level 2** | Supervisor Safety Observations | Daily | Operational activities | GSE Supervisors |
| **Level 3** | Weekly Safety Walk-Throughs | Weekly | Ramp/apron areas, GSE staging | GSE Safety Manager |
| **Level 4** | Monthly Equipment Inspections | Monthly | GSE fleet (sample) | GSE Maintenance Supervisor |
| **Level 5** | Quarterly Safety Audits | Quarterly | Safety management system | GSE Safety Manager + team |
| **Level 6** | Annual Comprehensive Audits | Annual | Full safety management system | Independent auditor |

### 2.2 Audit vs. Inspection

**Audit**: Systematic, independent examination of the safety management system to determine whether activities comply with planned arrangements and are implemented effectively.

**Inspection**: Physical examination of equipment, facilities, or operations to verify condition and compliance with standards.

## 3. Daily Pre-Operation Checks (Level 1)

### 3.1 Objective

Ensure GSE equipment is in safe operating condition before each use.

### 3.2 Scope

All GSE equipment before each operational shift or use.

### 3.3 Checklist

**Pre-Operation Checklist** (see [ASSETS/03-00-02-A-006_Safety_Checklist_GSE_Operations.pdf](./ASSETS/03-00-02-A-006_Safety_Checklist_GSE_Operations.pdf)):

#### General
- [ ] Equipment is clean and free from obvious damage
- [ ] Tires properly inflated, no visible damage or excessive wear
- [ ] Lights (headlights, brake lights, indicators) functional
- [ ] Mirrors clean and properly adjusted
- [ ] Fire extinguisher present, charged, and within service date
- [ ] First aid kit present and stocked

#### Safety Systems
- [ ] Brakes functional (service brake and parking brake)
- [ ] Steering responsive, no excessive play
- [ ] Horn/audible warning device functional
- [ ] Emergency stop button functional (if equipped)
- [ ] Seatbelt present and functional (if applicable)
- [ ] Safety interlocks functional (if applicable)

#### Fluids and Leaks
- [ ] No fuel, oil, or hydraulic fluid leaks
- [ ] Engine oil level adequate
- [ ] Coolant level adequate
- [ ] Hydraulic fluid level adequate (if applicable)
- [ ] Windshield washer fluid adequate

#### H₂ Refueling Bowser (Additional Checks)
- [ ] H₂ detection system functional, alarms tested
- [ ] Pressure relief devices (PRDs) intact and functional
- [ ] Breakaway coupling functional
- [ ] Emergency shutdown system tested
- [ ] Bonding cable and clamps in good condition
- [ ] Refueling hose and coupling inspected, no damage or degradation

### 3.4 Defect Reporting

- Any defect identified during pre-operation check must be immediately reported to GSE supervisor
- **Critical Defects** (affecting safety): Equipment tagged out of service, not used until repaired
- **Minor Defects**: Equipment may be used if safe, but defect must be repaired at next scheduled maintenance

## 4. Supervisor Safety Observations (Level 2)

### 4.1 Objective

Verify GSE operations are conducted in accordance with safety rules and procedures.

### 4.2 Scope

Random observations of GSE operations throughout each shift.

### 4.3 Observation Focus

- **Compliance**: Operators following speed limits, traffic rules, exclusion zones
- **PPE**: Operators wearing required PPE
- **Equipment Condition**: GSE equipment appears serviceable, no obvious defects
- **Communication**: Operators using proper communication methods (radio, hand signals)
- **Safe Behaviors**: Operators demonstrating situational awareness, defensive driving

### 4.4 Feedback

- **Positive Observations**: Recognized and reinforced ("Good job maintaining safe clearance from aircraft")
- **Unsafe Observations**: Immediately corrected, coaching provided
- **Serious Violations**: Reported to GSE Safety Manager, may result in operator stand-down and retraining

### 4.5 Documentation

- Supervisor observations documented in daily log
- Trends analyzed weekly by GSE Safety Manager

## 5. Weekly Safety Walk-Throughs (Level 3)

### 5.1 Objective

Identify hazards and unsafe conditions in GSE operational areas.

### 5.2 Scope

- Ramp and apron areas
- GSE staging and parking areas
- H₂ refueling zones
- GSE maintenance facilities

### 5.3 Walk-Through Focus

**Physical Hazards:**
- Surface conditions (pavement damage, spills, FOD)
- Lighting (adequate illumination, damaged lights)
- Signage and markings (clear, visible, up-to-date)
- Barriers and fencing (intact, properly positioned)

**Operational Hazards:**
- Congestion and traffic patterns
- Exclusion zone compliance
- Storage and housekeeping (equipment properly stored, clutter-free)
- Emergency equipment (fire extinguishers, first aid kits accessible)

**H₂ Safety:**
- H₂ detection systems functional
- Exclusion zone boundaries marked
- Ignition source controls in place
- Ventilation adequate

### 5.4 Documentation

- Walk-through findings documented in standardized form
- Photos attached for significant findings
- Findings reviewed with Ground Operations Manager
- Corrective actions assigned and tracked

---

## 6. Monthly Equipment Inspections (Level 4)

### 6.1 Objective

Verify GSE equipment is maintained in safe operating condition through detailed inspection.

### 6.2 Scope

- Sample of GSE fleet inspected each month (aim to inspect all equipment at least once per year)
- High-risk equipment (H₂ refueling bowsers, tow tractors) inspected more frequently

### 6.3 Inspection Checklist

**Detailed Equipment Inspection** (see [ASSETS/03-00-02-A-006_Safety_Checklist_GSE_Operations.pdf](./ASSETS/03-00-02-A-006_Safety_Checklist_GSE_Operations.pdf)):

#### Structural
- Frame and chassis (no cracks, corrosion, damage)
- Body panels and guards (secure, no sharp edges)
- Working platforms (guardrails, toe boards intact)
- Steps and handrails (secure, non-slip surfaces)

#### Mechanical
- Engine (oil leaks, unusual noise, vibration)
- Transmission (smooth operation, no slipping)
- Brakes (pad/shoe thickness, hydraulic system integrity)
- Steering (linkage, power steering system)
- Suspension (shocks, springs, bushings)

#### Electrical
- Battery (terminals clean, electrolyte level, load test)
- Wiring (no exposed wires, chafing, damage)
- Lights and signals (all functional)
- Instruments and gauges (functional, accurate)

#### Hydraulic/Pneumatic (if applicable)
- Hoses and fittings (no leaks, damage, proper routing)
- Pumps and cylinders (no leaks, smooth operation)
- Pressure levels (within specifications)

#### Safety Systems
- Emergency stops (functional, accessible)
- Alarms and warnings (audible, visible)
- Safety interlocks (functional)
- Fire suppression system (if equipped, charged, within service date)

### 6.4 Documentation

- Inspection findings documented in equipment maintenance record
- **Defects**: Classified as critical (immediate action) or non-critical (schedule for repair)
- Equipment tagged out of service if critical defects found
- Re-inspection after repairs to verify serviceability

## 7. Quarterly Safety Audits (Level 5)

### 7.1 Objective

Systematic assessment of the safety management system effectiveness.

### 7.2 Scope

Selected elements of the safety management system, rotated quarterly to cover all elements over the year.

### 7.3 Audit Elements (Rotated Quarterly)

**Q1: Safety Policy and Organization**
- Safety policy current and communicated
- Safety roles and responsibilities defined and understood
- Safety objectives and KPIs established and monitored
- Management commitment demonstrated

**Q2: Training and Competency**
- Training programs current and effective
- Personnel qualifications up-to-date
- Competency assessments conducted
- Training records complete and accurate

**Q3: Operational Safety and Compliance**
- Operational safety rules followed
- Pre-operation checks conducted
- Exclusion zones and clearances maintained
- PPE used correctly
- H₂ safety procedures followed

**Q4: Incident Management and Improvement**
- Incidents reported and investigated
- Root cause analysis conducted
- Corrective actions implemented
- Lessons learned disseminated
- Safety performance trends analyzed

### 7.4 Audit Methodology

#### Preparation
- **Audit Plan**: Define scope, criteria, schedule
- **Audit Team**: GSE Safety Manager + subject matter experts
- **Notification**: Audited areas notified 2 weeks in advance

#### Execution
- **Opening Meeting**: Introduce audit team, explain scope and process
- **Document Review**: Review procedures, training records, incident reports, maintenance logs
- **Interviews**: Interview personnel (operators, supervisors, maintenance staff)
- **Observations**: Observe operations and equipment
- **Closing Meeting**: Present preliminary findings

#### Reporting
- **Audit Report**: Findings categorized as compliant, minor non-conformance, or major non-conformance
- **Distribution**: Report distributed to audited areas, senior management, and relevant stakeholders
- **Corrective Actions**: Non-conformances require corrective action plans with target dates

### 7.5 Finding Classification

| Classification | Definition | Response Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Compliant** | Meets requirements | Continue current practices |
| **Minor Non-Conformance** | Isolated deviation, low risk | Corrective action within 30 days |
| **Major Non-Conformance** | Systematic deviation or high risk | Immediate corrective action, root cause analysis required |
| **Observation** | Opportunity for improvement | Optional improvement action |

## 8. Annual Comprehensive Audits (Level 6)

### 8.1 Objective

Independent, comprehensive assessment of the entire safety management system.

### 8.2 Scope

- All elements of the safety management system
- Compliance with [IATA ISAGO](https://www.iata.org/en/programs/safety/audit/isago/) standards (if applicable)
- Regulatory compliance (EASA, ICAO, local regulations)
- Industry best practices

### 8.3 Auditor

- **Independent Auditor**: External auditor with no direct responsibility for GSE operations
- **Qualifications**: Certified auditor (e.g., [IATA ISAGO Auditor](https://www.iata.org/en/programs/safety/audit/isago/), ISO 45001 Lead Auditor)
- **Aviation Safety Experience**: Proven experience in aviation ground operations safety

### 8.4 Audit Process

- Similar to quarterly audits but more comprehensive and in-depth
- Duration: 3-5 days
- Includes off-site document review and on-site observations and interviews
- Benchmarking against industry standards and best practices

### 8.5 Management Review

- Audit report presented to senior management
- Findings and corrective actions reviewed
- Safety management system effectiveness assessed
- Resources allocated for improvements
- Safety policy and objectives reviewed and updated if required

## 9. Corrective Action and Follow-Up

### 9.1 Corrective Action Process

For all audit/inspection findings requiring corrective action:

1. **Assignment**: Finding assigned to responsible individual (owner)
2. **Root Cause Analysis**: Owner conducts root cause analysis (for major non-conformances)
3. **Corrective Action Plan**: Owner develops corrective action plan with:
   - Description of corrective action
   - Target completion date
   - Resources required
4. **Approval**: Corrective action plan approved by GSE Safety Manager
5. **Implementation**: Owner implements corrective action
6. **Verification**: GSE Safety Manager verifies corrective action is completed and effective
7. **Closure**: Finding closed when corrective action verified

### 9.2 Tracking

- All findings tracked in safety management system
- Status reviewed monthly by GSE Safety Manager
- Overdue corrective actions escalated to senior management

### 9.3 Effectiveness Review

- Corrective actions reviewed 3 months after implementation to assess effectiveness
- If ineffective, additional corrective actions developed

## 10. Compliance Monitoring

### 10.1 Key Compliance Areas

| Compliance Area | Standard/Regulation | Verification Method |
|---|---|---|
| **GSE Equipment Safety** | Manufacturer specifications, ISO standards | Monthly equipment inspections |
| **Operational Safety Rules** | Internal procedures, [IATA IGOM](https://www.iata.org/en/publications/manuals/igom/) | Supervisor observations, quarterly audits |
| **H₂ Safety** | [ISO 19880-8](https://www.iso.org/standard/71940.html), [SAE J2601](https://www.sae.org/standards/content/j2601/), [NFPA 2](https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/all-codes-and-standards/list-of-codes-and-standards/detail?code=2) | H₂-specific audits, equipment inspections |
| **Training and Competency** | [IATA Training Standards](https://www.iata.org/en/programs/training/), internal requirements | Training record audits, competency assessments |
| **Incident Reporting** | [EU Regulation 376/2014](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32014R0376), internal procedures | Incident report audits, regulatory submissions |

### 10.2 Non-Compliance Escalation

- **Minor Non-Compliance**: Corrective action by supervisor, monitored by GSE Safety Manager
- **Repeated Non-Compliance**: Escalated to Ground Operations Manager, additional training or discipline
- **Regulatory Non-Compliance**: Escalated to senior management, immediate corrective action, regulatory notification if required

---

## 11. Continuous Improvement

### 11.1 Audit and Inspection Performance Metrics

- **Finding Closure Rate**: Target ≥ 90% on time
- **Repeat Findings**: Target < 10% (indicates effective corrective actions)
- **Audit/Inspection Coverage**: Target 100% of GSE fleet inspected annually
- **Corrective Action Effectiveness**: Target ≥ 95% effective on first implementation

### 11.2 Audit and Inspection Program Review

- Annual review of audit and inspection program by GSE Safety Manager
- Assessment of program effectiveness, resource requirements, and improvement opportunities
- Updates to checklists, scopes, and frequencies based on findings and risk assessment
