# 03-00-02-005 — Safety Training and Competency for GSE Personnel

## 1. Purpose

This document defines **training, competency, and recurrent check requirements** for Ground Support Equipment (GSE) operators, supervisors, and maintenance staff under [ATA 03 – Support Information GSE](https://www.ata.org/resources/specifications/ispec2200).

It establishes:

- Initial training and qualification requirements
- Competency assessment methods
- Recurrent training schedules
- H₂-specific training requirements
- Training record management

---

## 2. Training Framework

### 2.1 Training Categories

| Category | Target Audience | Duration | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GSE Safety Awareness** | All ramp/apron personnel | 2 hours | Annual |
| **GSE Operator Qualification** | GSE operators | 24 hours + practical | Initial + 3-year renewal |
| **H₂ Safety Training** | H₂ refuelling crew | 8 hours | Initial + annual refresher (4h) |
| **GSE Maintenance Training** | GSE maintenance staff | 16 hours | Initial + biennial refresher (8h) |
| **GSE Supervisor Training** | Operations supervisors | 16 hours | Initial + annual refresher (4h) |
| **Emergency Response** | All GSE personnel | 4 hours | Annual |

### 2.2 Training Delivery Methods

- **Classroom**: Theory, regulations, procedures
- **E-Learning**: Self-paced modules for theory and regulations
- **Practical/Hands-On**: Equipment operation, emergency drills
- **On-the-Job Training (OJT)**: Supervised operations under qualified instructor
- **Simulation**: Virtual reality (VR) or simulator-based training for complex scenarios

---

## 3. GSE Safety Awareness Training

### 3.1 Scope

**All personnel** working on the ramp or apron (including flight crew, ground crew, contractors, visitors) must complete GSE Safety Awareness training.

### 3.2 Content

- Overview of GSE types and operations
- Ramp safety rules (speed limits, right-of-way, exclusion zones)
- Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) requirements
- Hazards and risks of GSE operations
- Emergency procedures and stop work authority
- Communication and marshalling basics

### 3.3 Assessment

- Written exam (20 questions, 80% pass mark)
- Valid for 12 months

---

## 4. GSE Operator Qualification

### 4.1 Prerequisites

- Minimum age: 18 years
- Valid driver's license (for powered GSE)
- Medical fitness (vision, hearing, physical capability)
- GSE Safety Awareness training current

### 4.2 Initial Training (24 hours theory + 16 hours practical)

#### Theory (24 hours)
1. **GSE Safety Principles** (4 hours)
   - Safety management framework
   - Hazard identification and risk assessment
   - Operational safety rules
2. **GSE Equipment Types** (4 hours)
   - Tugs, tractors, loaders, belt loaders, catering trucks
   - Equipment capabilities and limitations
3. **Pre-Operation Checks** (2 hours)
   - Walk-around inspection
   - Safety system verification
   - Defect reporting
4. **Operational Procedures** (6 hours)
   - Speed limits, traffic rules, right-of-way
   - Exclusion zones and clearances
   - Connection/disconnection procedures
   - Towing operations
5. **Communication and Marshalling** (2 hours)
   - Radio procedures
   - Hand signals and marshalling
   - Coordination with flight crew and ground crew
6. **Emergency Procedures** (4 hours)
   - Fire, collision, equipment failure
   - Emergency stops and shutdown
   - Incident reporting
7. **Human Factors** (2 hours)
   - Fatigue management
   - Situational awareness
   - Error prevention and recovery

#### Practical Training (16 hours)
- Equipment familiarization and controls
- Pre-operation inspections
- Basic maneuvers (straight driving, turning, reversing, parking)
- Aircraft approach and positioning
- Connection procedures (electrical, pneumatic, hydraulic)
- Towing practice (with qualified tow instructor)
- Emergency procedure drills
- Supervised on-the-job training (OJT)

### 4.3 Competency Assessment

#### Written Exam
- 50 multiple-choice questions
- 80% pass mark
- 90-minute time limit

#### Practical Assessment
- Pre-operation checks (checklist-based)
- Equipment operation (maneuvering, speed control, parking)
- Aircraft servicing task (appropriate to equipment type)
- Emergency scenario response
- **Pass Criteria**: Demonstrate safe and competent operation without errors

### 4.4 Qualification Validity

- **Initial Qualification**: Valid for 3 years
- **Renewal**: Requires recurrent training and competency re-assessment every 3 years
- **Currency**: Operators must perform GSE operations at least once per 90 days to maintain currency
  - If gap > 90 days: Supervised OJT required before independent operations

## 5. H₂ Safety Training

### 5.1 Scope

**All personnel** involved in H₂ refuelling operations (operators, supervisors, maintenance, emergency responders) must complete H₂ Safety Training.

### 5.2 Initial H₂ Safety Training (8 hours)

1. **H₂ Properties and Hazards** (2 hours)
   - Physical and chemical properties of hydrogen
   - Flammability, ignition energy, flame characteristics
   - Cryogenic hazards (liquid H₂)
   - Comparison with conventional fuels
2. **H₂ Detection and Monitoring** (1 hour)
   - Fixed and portable H₂ detectors
   - Leak detection methods
   - Alarm levels and response
3. **Ignition Source Control** (1 hour)
   - Static electricity and bonding/grounding
   - Prohibited ignition sources
   - Intrinsically safe equipment
4. **H₂ Refuelling Safety Zones** (1 hour)
   - Inner, outer, and buffer zones
   - Access control and signage
   - Ventilation requirements
5. **H₂ Emergency Procedures** (2 hours)
   - H₂ leak response
   - H₂ fire suppression (or controlled burn)
   - Cryogenic injury treatment
   - Asphyxiation response
6. **H₂ PPE and Equipment** (1 hour)
   - Flame-resistant clothing
   - Cryogenic gloves and face shield
   - Personal H₂ detectors
   - H₂ refuelling equipment safety features

### 5.3 Practical H₂ Refuelling Training (16 hours)

- H₂ refuelling bowser familiarization
- Pre-refuelling safety checks and zone establishment
- Bonding and grounding procedures
- Refuelling connection and operation
- H₂ leak detection practice
- Emergency scenario drills:
  - H₂ leak response
  - H₂ fire scenario
  - Equipment malfunction
  - Personnel injury
- Competency assessment (supervised refuelling operations)

### 5.4 Recurrent H₂ Safety Training (annual, 4 hours)

- Refresher on H₂ hazards and safety principles
- Review of H₂ incidents and lessons learned (internal and industry)
- Updates to procedures, equipment, or regulations
- Emergency response drill (tabletop or practical)
- Competency check (written exam, 80% pass mark)

## 6. GSE Maintenance Training

### 6.1 Scope

GSE maintenance staff responsible for inspecting, servicing, and repairing GSE equipment.

### 6.2 Initial Maintenance Training (16 hours)

1. **GSE Maintenance Safety** (4 hours)
   - Lockout/tagout procedures
   - Working at height and confined space
   - Chemical and fluid hazards
   - PPE for maintenance tasks
2. **GSE Systems and Components** (6 hours)
   - Hydraulic, pneumatic, electrical systems
   - Brakes, steering, suspension
   - Safety devices (emergency stops, alarms, interlocks)
3. **Inspection and Testing** (4 hours)
   - Pre-service inspections (daily, weekly, monthly)
   - Non-destructive testing (NDT) methods
   - Load testing and functional checks
   - Defect identification and reporting
4. **Maintenance Records and Compliance** (2 hours)
   - Maintenance log management
   - Manufacturer service bulletins
   - Regulatory compliance (inspections, certifications)

### 6.3 Equipment-Specific Training

Maintenance staff must complete manufacturer-specific training for complex equipment:
- **H₂ Refuelling Bowsers** (24 hours)
- **High Loaders / Catering Trucks** (8 hours)
- **Aircraft Tow Tractors** (8 hours)

### 6.4 Recurrent Maintenance Training (biennial, 8 hours)

- Refresher on maintenance safety procedures
- Review of maintenance-related incidents
- Updates to equipment, procedures, or regulations
- Practical skills assessment

---

## 7. GSE Supervisor Training

### 7.1 Scope

Operations supervisors responsible for overseeing GSE operations and personnel.

### 7.2 Initial Supervisor Training (16 hours)

1. **Safety Leadership** (4 hours)
   - Safety culture and leadership behaviors
   - Stop work authority and just culture
   - Incident investigation and root cause analysis
2. **GSE Operations Management** (4 hours)
   - Daily safety briefings and pre-shift checks
   - Work allocation and task planning
   - Monitoring and observing operations
   - Performance management and coaching
3. **Risk Assessment and Hazard Management** (4 hours)
   - Dynamic risk assessment
   - Permit-to-work systems
   - Change management
   - Interfacing with other stakeholders (operations, maintenance, ATC)
4. **Emergency Response Coordination** (4 hours)
   - Emergency response procedures
   - Coordination with fire/rescue and medical
   - Incident command and communication
   - Post-incident recovery and investigation

### 7.3 Recurrent Supervisor Training (annual, 4 hours)

- Leadership refresher
- Review of safety performance and KPIs
- Incident case studies and lessons learned
- Regulatory updates

---

## 8. Emergency Response Training

### 8.1 Scope

All GSE personnel must complete emergency response training.

### 8.2 Content (4 hours, annual)

1. **Fire Response** (1 hour)
   - Fire types and extinguisher selection
   - Fire extinguisher operation (practical)
   - Evacuation procedures
2. **First Aid** (1 hour)
   - Basic first aid for common injuries
   - CPR and AED use (practical)
   - Cryogenic burn treatment
3. **H₂ Emergency Response** (1 hour)
   - H₂ leak and fire response
   - Asphyxiation rescue (theory only, actual rescue by trained responders)
4. **Emergency Drills** (1 hour)
   - Tabletop or practical emergency scenarios
   - Coordination with fire/rescue and medical

### 8.3 Advanced Emergency Response (Optional)

Personnel designated as **Emergency Response Team (ERT)** members receive additional training:
- **Advanced Fire Fighting** (16 hours)
- **Confined Space Rescue** (16 hours)
- **H₂ Incident Response** (8 hours)

## 9. Competency Assessment and Records

### 9.1 Competency Assessment Methods

- **Written Exams**: Theory knowledge assessment
- **Practical Assessments**: Hands-on demonstration of skills
- **Observations**: Supervisor observations during operations
- **Simulations**: Performance in simulated emergency scenarios

### 9.2 Training Records

Training records shall be maintained for each individual and include:
- Name and employee ID
- Training course title and date
- Training hours (theory + practical)
- Assessment results (written exam score, practical pass/fail)
- Qualification validity dates
- Instructor name and signature
- Next recurrent training due date

### 9.3 Training Record Management

- **Central Database**: Electronic training records system
- **Accessibility**: Records accessible to supervisors and safety manager
- **Audit Trail**: All changes to training records logged
- **Retention**: Training records retained for 5 years after employee departure

### 9.4 Training Currency Tracking

- **Automated Alerts**: System generates alerts 30 days before training expiry
- **Monthly Reports**: Training compliance reports reviewed by GSE Safety Manager
- **Non-Compliance**: Personnel with expired training not authorized to perform related tasks

---

## 10. Instructor Qualifications

### 10.1 GSE Instructor Requirements

GSE training instructors must have:
- Minimum 5 years GSE operational experience
- Current GSE operator qualification
- Train-the-Trainer course completion (40 hours)
- Strong communication and teaching skills
- Safety record with no serious violations in past 3 years

### 10.2 H₂ Safety Instructor Requirements

H₂ safety instructors must have:
- H₂ Safety Training current
- Minimum 2 years H₂ operations experience (or equivalent technical background)
- H₂ Train-the-Trainer course completion (24 hours)
- Knowledge of H₂ regulations and standards

## 11. Training Program Evaluation

### 11.1 Training Effectiveness Metrics

- **Pass Rates**: Written exam and practical assessment pass rates (target ≥ 90%)
- **Incident Correlation**: Analysis of incidents vs. training gaps
- **Feedback Surveys**: Post-training participant feedback (target satisfaction ≥ 4.0/5.0)
- **Observation Assessments**: Supervisor observations of trained personnel performance

### 11.2 Continuous Improvement

- Annual review of training program by GSE Safety Manager
- Incorporation of lessons learned from incidents
- Updates to training content based on regulatory changes or new equipment
- Benchmarking against industry best practices ([IATA Training Standards](https://www.iata.org/en/programs/training/))
