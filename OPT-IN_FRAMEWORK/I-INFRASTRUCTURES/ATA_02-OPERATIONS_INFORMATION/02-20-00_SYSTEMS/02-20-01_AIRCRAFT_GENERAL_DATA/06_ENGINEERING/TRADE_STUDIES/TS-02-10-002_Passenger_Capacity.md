# TS-02-10-002: Passenger Capacity Trade Study

**Document ID:** TS-02-10-002  
**Title:** Passenger Capacity Optimization for BWB Configuration  
**Project:** AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E Q100 INTEGRA  
**Date:** 2025-11-05  
**Status:** Complete - 220 Passengers Selected  
**Classification:** Engineering Analysis

---

## Executive Summary

This trade study evaluates optimal passenger capacity for the AMPEL360 BWB, balancing market demand, operational efficiency, and design constraints. **Selected capacity: 220 passengers (single class)** provides optimal economics while maintaining BWB advantages.

**Key Findings:**
- 220-seat configuration maximizes seat-mile cost efficiency
- Single-class wide-body comfort differentiator
- Optimal utilization of BWB center body volume
- Competitive with narrow-body and regional aircraft

## 11. Recommendations

### 11.1 Selected Configuration

**Capacity: 220 Passengers (single-class)**

**Rationale:**
1. ✅ Optimal unit cost economics ($0.0111/ASM)
2. ✅ Good revenue potential ($28,100 profit/flight)
3. ✅ Competitive with A321XLR/737MAX
4. ✅ Wide-body comfort differentiator
5. ✅ Efficient BWB volume utilization
6. ✅ Manageable weight and systems impact
7. ✅ Adequate emergency egress margin
8. ✅ Versatile for multiple route types

### 11.2 Variant Options

**Premium Configuration (180 seats):**
- 2-3-2 throughout
- 36" pitch
- Target: premium routes
- Premium pricing strategy

**High-Density Configuration (250 seats):**
- 3-4-3 center section
- 30" pitch
- Target: leisure/charter
- Requires structural reinforcement

## 12. Conclusions

The 220-passenger configuration represents the optimal balance:
- Best unit cost economics
- Competitive market positioning
- Wide-body comfort in narrow-body mission
- Efficient BWB configuration utilization
- Flexible for route network development

**Decision:** Proceed with 220-seat baseline, maintain flexibility for variants
