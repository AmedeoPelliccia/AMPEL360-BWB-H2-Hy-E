# RQ-02-10-60-002: Certification Basis

**Requirement ID:** RQ-02-10-60-002  
**Category:** Operational  
**Priority:** Critical  
**Status:** Approved

## Requirement

The aircraft shall be certified under EASA CS-25 (Large Aeroplanes) with appropriate special conditions for the BWB configuration, hydrogen propulsion system, and CAOS integration.

## Primary Certification Basis

**EASA CS-25 (Amendment 27 or current):**
- Subpart B: Flight
- Subpart C: Structure
- Subpart D: Design and Construction
- Subpart E: Powerplant
- Subpart F: Equipment
- Subpart G: Operating Limitations and Information

**Issue Date:** [To be determined by project schedule]  
**Target Certification:** 2028 Q4

## Special Conditions

### 1. Blended Wing Body Configuration

**SC-BWB-01: Flight Characteristics and Handling**
- Departure from conventional tail-aft configuration
- Demonstration of adequate control in all flight regimes
- Stall warning and characteristics
- Deep stall protection
- Spin resistance

**SC-BWB-02: Structural Design**
- Load distribution across integrated wing-body
- Pressure cabin in lifting surface
- Emergency landing structural integrity
- Ditching considerations

**SC-BWB-03: Emergency Evacuation**
- Exit distribution for wide-body configuration
- Demonstration per CS 25.803 adapted for BWB
- Evacuation analysis accounting for BWB geometry

### 2. Hydrogen Fuel System

**SC-H2-01: Fuel System Safety**
- Cryogenic fuel storage and handling
- Leak prevention and detection
- Fire protection for hydrogen
- Crash safety and fuel containment
- Ventilation and purging

**SC-H2-02: Fuel System Design**
- Material compatibility with LH₂
- Thermal management and insulation
- Fuel quantity indication
- Fuel transfer and management

**SC-H2-03: Ground and In-Flight Operations**
- Refueling procedures and safety
- Emergency fuel dumping (if applicable)
- Boil-off management
- Operational limitations

### 3. Fuel Cell Propulsion

**SC-FC-01: Fuel Cell Power System**
- Reliability and redundancy
- Performance in all conditions
- Failure modes and effects
- Power availability and distribution

**SC-FC-02: Electric Propulsion**
- Electric motor reliability
- Electromagnetic interference (EMI)
- Lightning protection
- Battery backup systems

### 4. CAOS Digital Twin System

**SC-CAOS-01: Software Assurance**
- DO-178C Level A for safety-critical functions
- Cybersecurity per DO-326A/ED-202A
- Human-machine interface design
- Crew alerting and warnings

**SC-CAOS-02: Predictive Systems**
- Validation of AI/ML algorithms
- Fail-safe design principles
- Crew override capability
- System monitoring and validation

## Equivalent Level of Safety (ELOS)

Where special conditions are established, compliance will be shown by demonstrating an Equivalent Level of Safety through:
- Analysis
- Testing
- Operational procedures
- Crew training
- Maintenance procedures
- Limitations in flight manual

## Environmental Standards

**Noise Certification:**
- ICAO Annex 16 Volume I Chapter 14
- Target: 75% quieter than Chapter 4 (65 dB vs 85 dB)

**Emissions:**
- Zero CO₂ emissions (H₂ propulsion)
- Zero NOx emissions (fuel cell operation)
- Water vapor only byproduct
- Net carbon-negative with CO₂ capture system

## Certification Program

**Phase 1: Type Design (24 months)**
- Detailed design reviews
- System safety assessments
- Material and process approvals
- Special conditions negotiation

**Phase 2: Compliance Demonstration (18 months)**
- Ground testing
- Component testing
- System integration testing
- Structural testing

**Phase 3: Flight Test (12 months)**
- Flight test program
- Performance validation
- Handling qualities
- Systems validation
- Environmental testing

**Phase 4: Type Certificate (6 months)**
- Compliance documentation
- TCDS preparation
- Flight Manual approval
- Type Certificate issuance

**Post-Certification Requirements:**
- Continuing Airworthiness: EASA Part-M

- Maintenance Program Approval
- Operator's requirements: EASA Part-OPS

## Rationale

Certification basis ensures:
- Legal operation in EASA member states
- FAA validation for U.S. operations
- Safety equivalence to conventional aircraft
- Novel technology acceptance
- International recognition
