# As-Built Inspection

**Component Code:** 02-10-00  
**Component Name:** AIRCRAFT_GENERAL_DATA  
**Subfolder:** 09_PRODUCTION_PLANNING/QUALITY_CONTROL  
**Platform:** AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E Q100 INTEGRA

## Overview

This document establishes comprehensive procedures for as-built inspection of AMPEL360 aircraft to verify conformance to design, build standard, and regulatory requirements. As-built inspection is critical for ensuring aircraft safety, certification compliance, and configuration accuracy.

## Inspection Philosophy

### Objectives
- **Safety:** Verify aircraft is safe for flight operations
- **Compliance:** Ensure conformance to type design and certification basis
- **Quality:** Confirm aircraft meets quality standards
- **Configuration:** Verify as-built configuration matches documentation
- **Traceability:** Establish complete record of aircraft configuration

### Inspection Approach
- **Progressive Inspection:** Inspection at key stages during assembly
- **Hold Points:** Mandatory inspection before proceeding to next stage
- **Risk-Based Sampling:** Focus on critical and high-risk areas
- **100% for Critical:** All safety-critical items inspected
- **Documentation:** Complete photographic and written records

## Inspection Stages

### Stage 1: Component and Assembly Inspection

**Timing:** During component manufacturing and sub-assembly

**Scope:**
- Component dimensions and tolerances
- Material verification and certifications
- Surface finish and quality
- Part marking and identification
- Fastener installation
- Composite layup and cure verification

**Hold Points:**
- Major structural component acceptance
- Composite part post-cure inspection
- Wing section assembly completion
- Center body section completion
- H₂ tank installation

**Inspection Activities:**
1. **Dimensional Inspection**
   - Measure critical dimensions
   - Verify tolerances
   - CMM measurement of complex features
   - Laser scanning of aerodynamic surfaces

2. **Visual Inspection**
   - Surface defects (cracks, voids, delaminations)
   - Workmanship quality
   - Cleanliness
   - Foreign object debris (FOD)

3. **Material Verification**
   - Check material certifications
   - Verify correct material used
   - Chemical composition verification (if required)
   - Material traceability

4. **Traceability Inspection**
   - Part numbers correct
   - Serial numbers recorded
   - Lot numbers documented
   - Supplier documentation present

**Documentation:** Component Acceptance Report, photos, dimensional data

### Stage 2: Final Assembly Inspection

**Timing:** During final aircraft assembly

**Scope:**
- Major assembly joining
- Systems installation
- Wiring and fluid line routing
- Equipment installation
- Software loading
- Ground testing

**Hold Points:**
- Wing-to-center body join
- Landing gear installation
- H₂ fuel system pressure test
- Fuel cell system installation
- Flight control system rigging
- Power-on test completion

**Inspection Activities:**
1. **Structural Join Inspection**
   - Verify correct fasteners and torques
   - Check alignment and fit
   - Inspect for gaps and interference
   - Seal integrity verification

2. **Systems Installation Inspection**
   - Equipment installed per design
   - Mounting hardware correct and torqued
   - Clearances adequate
   - Accessibility for maintenance verified

3. **Wiring and Plumbing Inspection**
   - Routing per design
   - Clamps and supports adequate
   - No chafing or interference
   - Connectors properly mated and secured

4. **Functional Testing Inspection**
   - Witness functional tests
   - Verify test results acceptable
   - Check for leaks (hydraulic, H₂, water)
   - Software version verification

**Documentation:** Assembly Inspection Reports, test results, photos

### Stage 3: Pre-Flight Inspection

**Timing:** After final assembly, before first flight

**Scope:**
- Complete aircraft visual inspection
- Systems functionality verification
- Flight control rigging and function
- Weight and balance verification
- Conformity to type design
- Airworthiness review

**Hold Points:**
- Conformity inspection completion
- Weight and balance approval
- Airworthiness certificate issuance

**Inspection Activities:**
1. **Conformity Inspection**
   - Verify aircraft conforms to type design
   - Check Type Certificate Data Sheet compliance
   - Equipment list verification
   - Software versions correct

2. **Flight Control Inspection**
   - Control surface rigging
   - Full range of motion verified
   - No binding or interference
   - Control force and displacement
   - Trim system functionality

3. **Propulsion System Inspection**
   - Fuel cell system pre-flight check
   - H₂ fuel system integrity
   - Cooling system functionality
   - Emergency shutdown systems

4. **Safety Systems Inspection**
   - Emergency equipment present and serviceable
   - Fire detection and suppression systems
   - Escape routes and exits
   - Emergency lighting

5. **Documentation Review**
   - Review all inspection records
   - Verify all hold points cleared
   - Check non-conformance closure
   - Confirm configuration accuracy

**Documentation:** Conformity Inspection Report, Airworthiness Certificate application

### Stage 4: Post-Flight Inspection

**Timing:** After flight testing, before delivery

**Scope:**
- Flight test induced issues
- System performance verification
- Interior completion inspection
- Final configuration audit
- Delivery preparation inspection

**Hold Points:**
- Flight test completion
- All squawks resolved
- Customer acceptance inspection

**Inspection Activities:**
1. **Flight Test Review**
   - Review flight test reports
   - Verify all test points completed
   - Confirm squawks resolved
   - Performance validation

2. **Systems Performance Inspection**
   - Systems operated per design
   - No anomalies during flight test
   - Performance meets specifications
   - CAOS system functionality

3. **Interior Inspection**
   - Cabin completion quality
   - Seat installation and functionality
   - Galley and lavatory systems
   - In-flight entertainment systems
   - Placards and markings

4. **Final Configuration Audit**
   - Physical audit of installed configuration
   - Compare to as-built documentation
   - Verify equipment list accuracy
   - Confirm serial numbers

5. **Delivery Preparation**
   - Aircraft cleaned and detailed
   - Protective covers removed
   - Fluids serviced to delivery levels
   - Documentation package complete

**Documentation:** Final Inspection Report, Flight Test Summary, Delivery Certificate

## BWB-Specific Inspection Considerations

### Blended Wing Body Structure

**Unique Inspection Points:**
1. **Wing-Body Blending**
   - Smooth transition surfaces
   - No steps or gaps at blend
   - Aerodynamic surface quality
   - Structural integrity of blended area

2. **Large Composite Structure**
   - Extensive non-destructive testing (NDT)
   - Ultrasonic inspection for voids and delaminations
   - Bond quality verification
   - Surface waviness measurement

3. **Distributed Load Paths**
   - Load path continuity verification
   - Attach fitting inspection
   - Stress concentration areas
   - Fatigue-critical areas

**Inspection Methods:**
- Laser shearography for composite inspection
- Thermography for bond quality
- Ultrasonic testing for internal defects
- Tap testing for delaminations

### H₂ Fuel System

**Unique Inspection Points:**
1. **Cryogenic Tanks**
   - Tank certification documents
   - Vacuum insulation integrity
   - Pressure test results (hydrostatic and pneumatic)
   - Leak testing with helium

2. **H₂ Fuel Lines**
   - Proper material for cryogenic service
   - Insulation quality
   - No mechanical damage
   - Proper routing and support

3. **Safety Systems**
   - H₂ detection system functionality
   - Ventilation system capacity
   - Emergency shutoff valves
   - Fire suppression system

4. **Ground Interface**
   - Refueling coupling accessibility
   - Ground bonding provisions
   - Safety interlocks functionality
   - Pressure relief system

**Inspection Methods:**
- Helium leak detection
- Pressure decay testing
- Thermography for insulation
- Functional testing of safety systems

### Fuel Cell Propulsion System

**Unique Inspection Points:**
1. **Fuel Cell Stacks**
   - Stack serial numbers and documentation
   - Mounting and vibration isolation
   - Cooling system connections
   - Electrical connections

2. **Power Distribution**
   - Bus bar torque verification
   - Insulation integrity
   - Protective device settings
   - Emergency disconnect functionality

3. **Thermal Management**
   - Heat exchanger installation
   - Coolant system pressure test
   - No leaks in cooling system
   - Temperature sensor calibration

4. **Control System**
   - Software version verification
   - Sensor calibration
   - Control logic testing
   - Emergency shutdown testing

**Inspection Methods:**
- Megger testing for insulation resistance
- Thermal imaging for hot spots
- Pressure testing of cooling system
- Functional testing of control system

### CAOS Digital Twin System

**Unique Inspection Points:**
1. **Sensor Installation**
   - All sensors installed per design
   - Proper calibration
   - Data transmission verified
   - Redundancy verified

2. **Data Acquisition System**
   - Hardware installation
   - Software version correct
   - Data logging functionality
   - Ground station communication

3. **Digital Twin Initialization**
   - Aircraft configuration loaded
   - Baseline performance models installed
   - Sensor mapping correct
   - Real-time data flow verified

4. **AI System Integration**
   - AI algorithms version
   - Training data loaded
   - Inference engine functionality
   - Human-machine interface

**Inspection Methods:**
- Functional testing of all sensors
- Data integrity verification
- Communication link testing
- Digital twin synchronization verification

## Inspection Documentation

### Inspection Stamps and Signatures

**Requirements:**
- Quality inspector stamp/signature on all inspection records
- Engineering stamp for engineering dispositions
- Test pilot signature on flight test records
- Certification authority stamp for conformity inspection

**Stamp Format:**
- Inspector name or ID number
- Date of inspection
- Stamp or signature
- "Inspected and Approved" or "Inspected - See NCR [number]"

### Photographic Documentation

**Requirements:**
- Photograph critical installations before closeout
- Serial number plates photographed
- Non-conformances photographed
- Overall aircraft photos at key stages

**Photo Management:**
- Digital photos linked to aircraft MSN in database
- Organized by ATA chapter or assembly stage
- Metadata includes date, location, inspector
- Secure storage and backup

### Inspection Records

**Record Retention:**
- Permanent retention for aircraft life
- Digital and paper backup
- Secure storage with controlled access
- Retrievable for audits and investigations

**Record Contents:**
- Inspection checklist (what was inspected)
- Inspection results (measurements, pass/fail)
- Non-conformance records (if any)
- Inspector identification
- Date and time of inspection
- Signatures and approvals

## Non-Conformance Management

### Non-Conformance Detection

**During Inspection:**
- Any deviation from design or specification
- Damage discovered during inspection
- Missing or incorrect parts
- Functional test failures
- Documentation discrepancies

**NCR Generation:**
1. Inspector documents non-conformance
2. NCR number assigned automatically
3. Affected aircraft and components identified
4. Severity classification (critical, major, minor)
5. Production hold if safety-critical

### Engineering Disposition

**Disposition Options:**
1. **Use As-Is:** Engineering accepts deviation (must justify safety)
2. **Rework:** Correct to design specification
3. **Repair:** Restore to airworthy condition (may differ from design)
4. **Scrap:** Component cannot be made acceptable

**Disposition Process:**
- Engineering reviews non-conformance
- Root cause analysis
- Disposition decision with justification
- Approval by Chief Engineer (if critical)
- Certification authority approval (if required)

### Corrective Action

**Implementation:**
- Corrective action per engineering disposition
- Re-inspection after correction
- Verification of corrective action effectiveness
- NCR closure with inspector signature

**Preventive Action:**
- Identify why non-conformance occurred
- Implement process change to prevent recurrence
- Training or procedure update
- Monitor for recurrence

## Quality Metrics

### Inspection Performance Metrics

**Metrics Tracked:**
- Non-conformance rate (NCRs per aircraft)
- Rework rate (hours/aircraft)
- Inspection findings by category
- Repeat non-conformances
- Inspection cycle time

**Targets:**
- NCR rate: < 5 per aircraft (by MSN-010)
- Rework rate: < 2% of direct labor
- Zero repeat critical non-conformances
- Inspection on schedule (no delays)

**Trending:**
- Track metrics over serial numbers
- Identify improvement or deterioration
- Compare to industry benchmarks
- Adjust processes based on trends

### First-Time Quality

**Measurement:**
- Percentage of work accepted on first inspection
- Reduction in rework over serial numbers
- Elimination of common non-conformances

**Goal:** 98% first-time quality by serial production

### Inspector Qualification

**Requirements:**
- Technical training in aircraft systems
- Quality inspection procedures training

- Certification/authorization by Quality Manager

**Ongoing Training:**
- Annual refresher training
- New process/procedure training

**Requirements:**
- Designated Engineering Representative (DER) or equivalent
- Experience in aircraft design and certification
- Authority granted by Chief Engineer
- Training in disposition procedures

## Certification Authority Involvement

### Conformity Inspection

**Authority Role:**
- Witness or conduct conformity inspection
- Verify aircraft conforms to type design
- Review inspection records and non-conformances
- Approve issuance of airworthiness certificate

**Preparation:**
- All inspection records complete and organized
- All NCRs closed or dispositioned
- Aircraft configuration documented
- Test results available for review

### Production Oversight

**Authority Audits:**
- Periodic audits of quality system
- Review of inspection procedures
- Witness selected inspections
- Review non-conformance trends

**Continuous Surveillance:**
- Ongoing authority presence (if Production Certificate)
- Real-time review of selected inspections
- Approval authority for critical NCR dispositions
