# Weight Distribution Philosophy

## Core Principles

### Balance and Stability
The AMPEL360 BWB design requires careful weight distribution to maintain CG within limits across all operating conditions.

### Design Strategy

**Distributed Loading**
- Passengers spread across 38m width
- Minimize CG shift during boarding/deboarding
- Zone-based seating assignment

**H2 Tank Placement**
- **Center Tanks:** Primary fuel, near CG (25% MAC)
- **Wing Tanks:** Trim fuel, forward/aft of CG
- **Automated Transfer:** CAOS-controlled fuel sequencing

**Cargo Distribution**
- **Forward Cargo:** Balances aft passenger loads
- **Aft Cargo:** Balances forward passenger loads
- **Load Planning:** Optimized by ground systems

## CG Management Strategy

### Static Balance
- OEW CG at 27% MAC (mid-envelope)
- Allows maximum loading flexibility
- Accommodates various load distributions

### Dynamic Balance
- CAOS real-time CG monitoring
- Automated H2 transfer during flight
- Maintains CG within 20-32% MAC (optimal L/D)

### Load Planning
- Ground software computes load distribution
- Passenger seating zones assigned
- Cargo compartment loading sequence
- H2 tank loading sequence

## Safety Margins

### Regulatory Compliance
- CS-25.27: CG limits
- 5% margin from structural limits
- 2% operational warning margins

### CAOS Integration
- Continuous weight and balance monitoring
- Predictive alerting before limit exceedance
- Load redistribution recommendations
