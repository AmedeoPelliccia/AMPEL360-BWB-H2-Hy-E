## Aircraft General Specifications

### Dimensions
- **Wing Span:** 52.0 m (170.6 ft)
- **Overall Length:** 48.5 m (159.1 ft)
- **Overall Height:** 12.8 m (42.0 ft)
- **Wing Area:** 845 m² (9,095 ft²)

### BWB Configuration
- **Blended Wing Body design:** Integrated wing-fuselage
- **Aspect Ratio:** 8.2:1
- **Sweep Angle (Leading Edge):** 37°
- **Center Body Width:** 18.5 m

### Weights (Reference)
- **MTOW:** 185,000 kg (407,855 lb)
- **MLW:** 155,000 kg (341,717 lb)
- **MZFW:** 145,000 kg (319,670 lb)
- **OEW (Operating Empty Weight):** 105,000 kg (231,485 lb)

## Propulsion System

### Fuel Cell Configuration
- **Type:** PEM (Proton Exchange Membrane) Fuel Cell Stacks
- **Number of Stacks:** 6 × 3 MW units
- **Total Power:** 18 MW installed, 15 MW continuous
- **Efficiency:** 58% HHV (Higher Heating Value)

### Electric Motors
- **Type:** Distributed electric propulsion
- **Number of Motors:** 12 units (boundary layer ingestion)
- **Individual Power:** 1.25 MW per motor
- **Total Thrust:** 280 kN at sea level

### Hydrogen Fuel System
- **Fuel Type:** Liquid Hydrogen (LH₂)
- **Storage Temperature:** -253°C (20 K)
- **Total Capacity:** 4,500 kg H₂
- **Usable Fuel:** 4,300 kg H₂
- **Tank Configuration:** 6 cryogenic tanks in BWB center section

## Performance Characteristics

### Speed Performance
- **Cruise Speed:** Mach 0.80 / 470 KTAS
- **Maximum Speed (VMO/MMO):** 350 KIAS / Mach 0.82
- **Stall Speed (Landing Config):** 115 KIAS
- **Best Rate of Climb Speed:** 210 KIAS

### Range and Endurance
- **Maximum Range:** 4,000 km (2,160 nm) with full passenger load
- **Ferry Range:** 5,200 km (2,808 nm) with minimal payload
- **Endurance:** 7.5 hours maximum
- **Reserve Requirement:** 500 kg H₂

### Altitude Capability
- **Service Ceiling:** 43,000 ft (13,106 m)
- **Optimum Cruise Altitude:** 37,000 - 41,000 ft
- **Maximum Certified Altitude:** 43,000 ft

### Climb Performance
- **Initial Rate of Climb:** 2,800 ft/min at MTOW
- **Time to 37,000 ft:** 28 minutes at MTOW
- **Time to 41,000 ft:** 35 minutes at typical cruise weight

## Passenger and Cargo Capacity

### Passenger Configuration
- **Standard Layout:** 220 passengers (single class)
- **Two-Class Layout:** 186 passengers (36 business, 150 economy)
- **Maximum Density:** 275 passengers (emergency exit limit)

### Cargo Capacity
- **Forward Cargo Hold:** 45 m³ (1,589 ft³)
- **Aft Cargo Hold:** 35 m³ (1,236 ft³)
- **Total Volume:** 80 m³ (2,825 ft³)
- **Maximum Payload:** 25,000 kg

## Standard Operating Conditions

### ISA (International Standard Atmosphere)
- **Sea Level:** 15°C, 1013.25 hPa
- **Temperature Lapse:** -1.98°C per 1,000 ft to 36,089 ft
- **Stratosphere:** -56.5°C above 36,089 ft

### Runway Requirements
- **Takeoff Distance (MTOW, SL, ISA):** 2,450 m (8,038 ft)
- **Landing Distance (MLW, SL, ISA):** 1,850 m (6,070 ft)
- **Balanced Field Length:** 2,650 m (8,694 ft)

## Fuel Consumption Data

### Typical Mission Profile
- **Taxi Out:** 15 kg H₂
- **Takeoff and Climb:** 320 kg H₂
- **Cruise (3,500 km):** 2,800 kg H₂
- **Descent and Landing:** 85 kg H₂
- **Taxi In:** 10 kg H₂
- **Reserve:** 500 kg H₂
- **Total:** 3,730 kg H₂

### Fuel Efficiency Comparison
- **H₂ Consumption:** 0.8 kg H₂ per 100 ASK
- **Equivalent Jet-A:** 2.5 kg per 100 ASK
- **Efficiency Improvement:** 68% better than conventional

## Environmental Performance

### Emissions
- **CO₂ Direct:** 0 kg (hydrogen combustion → water)
- **CO₂ Indirect (DAC System):** -5 kg net per flight
- **NOx:** 0 kg (no combustion)
- **Particulates:** 0 kg

### Noise Levels
- **Approach (3.0° glide slope):** 85 dB EPNdB
- **Sideline (450 m):** 80 dB EPNdB
- **Flyover (6,500 m):** 75 dB EPNdB
- **Reduction vs Stage 5:** -15 dB average

## Corrections and Adjustments

### Temperature Corrections
- Takeoff distance: +3% per 10°C above ISA
- Climb rate: -5% per 10°C above ISA
- Fuel cell efficiency: -1% per 10°C above optimal

### Altitude Corrections
- Takeoff distance: +7% per 1,000 ft elevation
- Climb rate: -8% per 1,000 ft elevation

### Weight Corrections
- Performance penalties proportional to weight ratio
- Reference charts provided in Section 5.2

### Wind Corrections
- Headwind: Decrease takeoff distance, increase climb angle
- Tailwind: Increase takeoff distance, decrease climb angle
- Crosswind: See AFM 1.3 for limits (35-38 kt)

## CG Effects on Performance

### Forward CG (15-20% MAC)
- Increased stability
- Reduced control authority
- Slightly higher fuel consumption (+2%)

### Mid CG (25-30% MAC)
- Optimal performance
- Balanced control
- Best fuel efficiency

### Aft CG (35-42% MAC)
- Reduced stability margin
- Enhanced control response
- Increased fuel consumption (+3%)
