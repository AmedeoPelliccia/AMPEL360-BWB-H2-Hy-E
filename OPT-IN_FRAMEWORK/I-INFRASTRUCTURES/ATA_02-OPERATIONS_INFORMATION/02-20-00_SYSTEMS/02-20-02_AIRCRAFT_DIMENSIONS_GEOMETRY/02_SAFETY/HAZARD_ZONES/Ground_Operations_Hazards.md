# Ground Operations Hazards

**Document ID:** AMPEL360-02-11-00-SAF-020  
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Overview

This document identifies and categorizes ground operation hazards specific to the AMPEL360 BWB H₂ aircraft, providing safety procedures and mitigation strategies for each hazard category.

## Hazard Categories

### Category 1: Critical Hazards (Red)

**Definition:** Immediate danger to personnel or aircraft; potential for catastrophic consequences.

#### H₂ Refueling Operations

**Hazards:**
- Hydrogen leak and ignition
- Static discharge ignition
- High-pressure system failure
- Cryogenic exposure (-253°C)

**Safety Measures:**
- 50m exclusion zone (no personnel)
- Ground bonding verified (<3 milliohm)
- Fire suppression pre-positioned (2 vehicles)
- Windward approach mandatory
- Continuous leak detection monitoring

#### Engine Intake Zones

**Hazards:**
- Personnel ingestion risk
- FOD ingestion (engine damage)
- Suction injury potential
- Hearing damage (noise)

**Safety Measures:**
- 10m exclusion zone when engines running
- High-visibility barriers/cones
- FOD walk-down before engine start
- Protective equipment mandatory (hearing)
- Ground crew training/awareness

#### Propeller/Propulsor Safety Zones

**Hazards:**
- Contact with rotating propellers
- Blade throw (failure condition)
- Airflow hazards
- Noise exposure

**Safety Measures:**
- 25m safety perimeter marked
- No entry when engines operating
- Visual indicators (rotating beacon)
- Approach procedures defined
- Emergency shutdown accessible

### Category 2: Major Hazards (Orange)

**Definition:** Significant risk to personnel or aircraft; potential for serious injury or major damage.

#### Exhaust Zones

**Hazards:**
- High-temperature exhaust (water vapor + heat)
- Jet blast effects
- Noise exposure
- Thermal burns

**Safety Measures:**
- 8m minimum clearance from exhaust
- Heat-resistant equipment required
- Personnel protective equipment
- Approach from forward only
- Speed restrictions near exhaust path

#### Wingtip Strike Zones

**Hazards:**
- Personnel struck by moving wingtip
- Equipment collision with wingtip
- Low wingtip clearance (1.2m at MTOW)
- Visibility limitations (wide wingspan)

**Safety Measures:**
- 5m clearance zone maintained
- Wing walkers when clearance <3m
- Visual position markers on wingtips
- CAOS proximity monitoring
- Enhanced marshalling procedures

#### Belly Strike Risk Zones

**Hazards:**
- Ground contact during taxi/turns
- Pavement irregularity impact
- Structural damage potential
- H₂ system component damage

**Safety Measures:**
- Pavement inspection before taxi
- CAOS ground clearance monitoring
- Speed restrictions (rough surfaces)
- Route planning for clearance
- Post-event inspection procedures

### Category 3: Moderate Hazards (Yellow)

**Definition:** Potential for injury or damage; requires caution and adherence to procedures.

#### Service Vehicle Approach Zones

**Hazards:**
- Vehicle-aircraft collision
- Equipment positioning errors
- Visibility obstructions
- Communication failures

**Safety Measures:**
- Designated approach paths
- Speed limits (5 km/h near aircraft)
- Marshaller/coordinator required
- Radio communication mandatory
- Clearance verification procedures

#### Loading/Unloading Operations

**Hazards:**
- Door clearance inadequate
- Equipment tip-over risk
- Load shift potential
- Cargo handling injuries

**Safety Measures:**
- Equipment positioning verification (1.0m clearance)
- Loader stabilization checks
- Load securing procedures
- Personnel protective equipment
- Supervisor oversight required

#### Passenger Boarding Operations

**Hazards:**
- Jetway misalignment
- Stairs instability
- Weather exposure
- Crowd control issues

**Safety Measures:**
- Jetway alignment verification (±0.2m)
- Stairs positioning checks
- Weather monitoring
- Personnel training
- Emergency procedures defined

### Category 4: Low Hazards (Blue)

**Definition:** Minor risks requiring standard safety precautions.

#### General Ground Traffic

**Hazards:**
- Vehicle conflicts
- Pedestrian safety
- Equipment movement
- Visibility issues

**Safety Measures:**
- Designated traffic lanes
- Speed limits enforced
- High-visibility clothing
- Communication protocols
- Awareness training

## BWB-Specific Hazards

### Wide Center Body (38m)

**Unique Hazards:**
- Multiple simultaneous operations complexity
- Increased blind spots for ground crew
- Visual communication limitations
- Coordination challenges

**Mitigation:**
- Ground operations coordinator mandatory
- Radio communication required (all personnel)
- Distributed service positions
- Enhanced visual aids (cameras, lights)
- Standardized communication protocols

### Large Wing Planform (845 m²)

**Unique Hazards:**
- Wind sensitivity (weathervaning)
- Snow/ice accumulation
- De-icing complexity
- Surface contamination detection

**Mitigation:**
- Wind limits enforced (35 kt taxi, 50 kt parking)
- Nose-into-wind parking preferred
- Extended de-icing time (45 min full aircraft)
- Enhanced surface inspection procedures

### Low Ground Clearances

**Unique Hazards:**
- Pavement quality critical
- FOD risk increased
- Limited approach angles
- Equipment height restrictions

**Mitigation:**
- Pavement condition standards
- Enhanced FOD control
- Equipment certification required
- Clearance monitoring (CAOS)

## Environmental Hazards

### Weather Conditions

#### High Winds

**Thresholds:**
- Taxi: 35 kt crosswind maximum
- Ground service: 30 kt maximum
- Door operations: 25 kt maximum
- Jetway operations: 20 kt maximum

**Procedures:**
- Continuous wind monitoring
- Nose-into-wind positioning
- Equipment securing requirements
- Personnel briefings

#### Low Visibility

**Thresholds:**
- RVR <550m: Enhanced procedures
- RVR <400m: Wing walkers mandatory
- RVR <200m: Operations suspended

**Procedures:**
- Additional lighting activated
- Taxi speed reductions
- Continuous communication
- Visual aid enhancement

#### Precipitation

**Snow/Ice:**
- Accumulation >50mm: Ops prohibited
- Any ice: Towing only (3 kt max)
- De-icing procedures: Extended time

**Rain:**
- Normal ops with speed reduction (50%)
- Visibility considerations
- Pavement condition monitoring
- Lightning: Refueling prohibited

### Surface Conditions

**Dry:** No restrictions (normal procedures)

**Wet:**
- Speed reduction: 50%
- Increased stopping distance
- Hydroplaning awareness
- Brake effectiveness monitoring

**Ice:**
- Ground operations prohibited
- Towing only: 3 kt maximum
- Enhanced traction verification
- Alternative handling procedures

**Snow:**
- Depth <50mm: Reduced operations
- Depth >50mm: Operations prohibited
- Accumulation monitoring
- Removal before operations

- Landing lights: Active taxi

**Ground:**
- Red barriers: No entry zones
- Yellow cones: Caution zones
- Blue lights: Equipment zones
- Green lights: Safe passage

- Air horn: Emergency alert
- Sirens: Equipment emergency
- PA system: General warnings/instructions

## Incident Response

### Emergency Situations

**H₂ Leak:**
1. Activate emergency alert
2. Evacuate 100m radius
3. Eliminate ignition sources
4. Fire suppression ready
5. Leak containment procedures

**Aircraft Fire:**
1. Alert fire services
2. Evacuate area
3. Position fire equipment
4. Coordinate with crew
5. Establish command post

**Personnel Injury:**
1. Immediate medical response
2. Secure the area
3. Preserve scene (investigation)
4. Notify safety department
5. Document thoroughly

**Near-Miss:**
1. Stop operations
2. Assess situation
3. Report immediately
4. Document event
5. Corrective action planning
