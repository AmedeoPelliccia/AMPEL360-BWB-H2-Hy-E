# H₂ Tank Layout and Segmentation Trade Study

**Trade Study ID:** 02-11-00-TS-004  
**Platform:** AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E Q100  
**Status:** Draft  
**Date:** 2025-11-10

---

## 1. Objective

Optimize H₂ tank layout to balance:
- Fuel capacity (range requirement)
- CG management during fuel burn
- Safety (separation and containment)
- Structural integration

---

## 2. Requirements

### 2.1 Fuel Requirements
- **Total H₂ capacity:** ~30,000 kg for 8,000 km range
- **Liquid H₂ density:** 70.8 kg/m³
- **Required volume:** ~424 m³

### 2.2 Safety Requirements
- **Separation:** Redundant tanks with isolation
- **Crash protection:** Tanks protected in crash scenarios
- **Leak detection and venting:** Per special conditions

---

## 3. Options Evaluated

| Option | Tank Count | Location | Total Capacity (kg) | CG Shift (% MAC) | Safety Rating |
|--------|------------|----------|---------------------|------------------|---------------|
| A | 2 large | Center body only | 32,000 | 8.5 | Medium |
| B | 4 medium | Center + wings | 31,500 | 5.2 | High |
| C | 6 small | Distributed | 30,500 | 3.8 | High |

---

## 4. Recommendation

**Selected Option: B (4 main tanks + 2 auxiliary)**

- 2 tanks in center body (15,000 kg each)
- 2 tanks in wing roots (7,500 kg each)
- 2 small trim tanks for CG control (1,500 kg each)

**Rationale:**
- Adequate capacity (31,500 kg)
- Manageable CG shift (5.2% MAC)
- Good safety (redundancy and separation)
- Reasonable structural integration
