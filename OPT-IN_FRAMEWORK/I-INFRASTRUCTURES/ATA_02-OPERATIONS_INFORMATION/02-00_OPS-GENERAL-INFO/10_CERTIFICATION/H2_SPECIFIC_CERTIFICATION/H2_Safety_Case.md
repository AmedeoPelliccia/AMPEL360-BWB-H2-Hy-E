## 1. Executive Summary

This safety case demonstrates that the AMPEL360 H₂ propulsion system provides equivalent or superior safety to conventional jet fuel systems through defense-in-depth architecture and comprehensive risk mitigation.

---

## 2. Safety Objectives

### 2.1 Primary Objectives
- Zero catastrophic H₂-related accidents
- Leak rate <0.1% volume per system
- Fire risk equivalent or better than jet fuel
- Emergency response time <30 seconds

### 2.2 Safety Architecture
**Defense-in-Depth Layers:**
1. Inherent safety (design)
2. Engineered safety systems
3. Procedural controls
4. Emergency response

---

## 3. Hazard Analysis

### 3.1 Identified Hazards
1. **H₂ Leakage**
   - Risk: Fire/explosion
   - Mitigation: 8 sensors/zone, continuous monitoring, automatic ventilation

2. **Cryogenic Temperature**
   - Risk: Material embrittlement, personnel injury
   - Mitigation: Material selection, thermal barriers, protective equipment

3. **Fuel Cell Malfunction**
   - Risk: Loss of power
   - Mitigation: Redundant stacks, graceful degradation, battery backup

4. **Ground Refueling**
   - Risk: Leak during refueling
   - Mitigation: Safety zone, grounding, leak detection, fire equipment

### 3.2 Risk Assessment
- Quantitative risk analysis (QRA) performed
- Risk levels: All hazards reduced to acceptable levels
- Safety targets met or exceeded

## 4. Safety Systems

### 4.1 Leak Detection
- 8 sensors minimum per H₂ zone
- LEL detection threshold
- Continuous monitoring during all operations
- Automatic crew alerting
- Automatic emergency response initiation

### 4.2 Ventilation
- Normal: 10 air changes/hour
- Emergency: 50 air changes/hour
- Automatic activation on leak detection
- Zone isolation capability

### 4.3 Fire Protection
- H₂-specific fire detection (UV/IR)
- H₂-capable suppression agents
- Zone isolation
- Crew notification

### 4.4 Emergency Systems
- Emergency ventilation
- Emergency shutdown (<30 seconds)
- Emergency defueling (ground, <60 minutes)
- Crew override capability

## 6. Comparative Safety Analysis

### 6.1 H₂ vs Jet Fuel

| Factor | Hydrogen | Jet Fuel | Assessment |
|--------|----------|----------|------------|
| Flammability Range | Wide (4-75%) | Narrow (1-7.5%) | Requires more control |
| Ignition Energy | Low (0.02 mJ) | High (0.24 mJ) | More sensitive |
| Flame Speed | Fast | Moderate | Faster detection |
| Buoyancy | High (14× air) | Liquid/vapor | Better dispersion |
| Toxicity | Non-toxic | Toxic fumes | H₂ advantage |
| Storage | Cryogenic | Ambient | More complex |

### 6.2 Safety Advantage
With comprehensive safety systems, H₂ provides equivalent or superior safety through:
- Rapid dispersion (buoyancy)
- Non-toxic properties
- Better detection systems
- Comprehensive monitoring
