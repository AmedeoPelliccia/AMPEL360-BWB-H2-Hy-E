# Master Minimum Equipment List (MMEL)
## AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E Q100 INTEGRA

**Document Control:**
- Version: Draft 0.1
- Status: Planning
- Last Updated: 2025-11-05

---

## 1. Purpose

The Master Minimum Equipment List (MMEL) defines equipment that may be inoperative while still allowing aircraft dispatch.

---

## 2. MMEL Structure

Per regulatory requirements:
- Section 1: General
- Section 2-99: ATA chapter systems

---

## 3. H₂-Specific Items

### 3.1 Hydrogen Fuel System
- **Item:** H₂ Leak Detection System
- **MMEL Category:** (C) - Operational restrictions apply
- **Remarks:** Minimum 6 of 8 sensors per zone required for dispatch

- **Item:** H₂ Fuel Quantity Indication
- **MMEL Category:** (A) - No dispatch allowed
- **Remarks:** Critical for flight safety

- **Item:** Fuel Cell Stack
- **MMEL Category:** (C) - 3 of 4 stacks required
- **Remarks:** Performance limitations with 3 stacks

### 3.2 Ventilation Systems
- **Item:** Emergency Ventilation System
- **MMEL Category:** (A) - No dispatch allowed
- **Remarks:** H₂ safety critical

---

## 4. CAOS-Specific Items

### 4.1 CAOS AI System
- **Item:** CAOS AI Advisory System
- **MMEL Category:** (D) - No operational restrictions
- **Remarks:** Advisory function only, not required for dispatch

- **Item:** CAOS Override Capability
- **MMEL Category:** (A) - No dispatch allowed if CAOS operational
- **Remarks:** Required if CAOS engaged

---

## 5. BWB-Specific Items

### 5.1 CG Management
- **Item:** Real-Time CG Calculation System
- **MMEL Category:** (B) - Manual calculation procedures required
- **Remarks:** BWB CG critical, manual backup acceptable

- **Item:** Ground Clearance Monitoring
- **MMEL Category:** (C) - Visual procedures required
- **Remarks:** BWB ground clearance limitations
