## 1. INTRODUCTION

This document establishes the comprehensive emergency response framework for AMPEL360 aircraft operations, covering classification, procedures, coordination, and post-emergency actions.

---

## 2. EMERGENCY CLASSIFICATION

### 2.1 Emergency Levels

**LEVEL 1 - Alert:**
- Potential emergency developing
- Enhanced monitoring required
- Precautionary measures initiated
- Normal operations continue

**LEVEL 2 - Emergency:**
- Actual emergency condition
- Immediate actions required
- Significant safety concern
- May require priority handling

**LEVEL 3 - Distress:**
- Immediate threat to aircraft/occupants
- Maximum priority required
- Emergency services on standby
- Possible aircraft loss scenario

### 2.2 Emergency Categories

**Aircraft Technical:**
- Propulsion system failures
- Flight control malfunctions
- H2 system emergencies
- Fuel cell emergencies
- Electrical failures
- Structural damage

**Medical:**
- Passenger medical emergencies
- Crew incapacitation
- Multiple casualties
- Infectious disease events

**Security:**
- Unlawful interference
- Bomb threats
- Cybersecurity incidents
- Dangerous goods incidents

**Environmental:**
- Severe weather encounters
- Volcanic ash
- Airport closures
- Airspace restrictions

## 3. EMERGENCY RESPONSE PROCEDURES

### 3.1 In-Flight Emergency Response

**Phase 1: Recognition and Assessment**
1. Identify emergency condition
2. Assess severity and immediacy
3. Classify emergency level
4. Alert crew and CAOS

**Phase 2: Immediate Actions**
1. Execute memory items (if applicable)
2. Stabilize aircraft
3. Ensure immediate safety
4. Maintain aircraft control

**Phase 3: Follow-Up Actions**
1. Complete checklists
2. Assess options
3. Coordinate with ATC
4. Brief cabin crew
5. Prepare passengers

**Phase 4: Landing Preparation**
1. Select suitable airport
2. Coordinate with ground services
3. Brief cabin crew on evacuation plan
4. Prepare passengers
5. Configure aircraft

**Phase 5: Post-Landing**
1. Evacuate if required
2. Secure aircraft
3. Coordinate with emergency services
4. Account for all persons
5. Preserve evidence

### 3.2 Ground Emergency Response

**H2 Refueling Emergency:**
1. Emergency shutdown of refueling
2. Evacuate safety zone
3. Alert airport fire services
4. Activate H2 emergency response team
5. Monitor and assess

**Ground Fire:**
1. Evacuate aircraft immediately
2. Alert fire services (CODE RED)
3. Establish safety perimeter
4. Account for all persons
5. Assist fire services as requested

## 4. COORDINATION AND COMMUNICATION

### 4.1 Internal Coordination

**Flight Deck:**
- Captain commands emergency response
- First Officer assists, flies aircraft
- Communication with cabin crew
- CAOS provides decision support

**Cabin Crew:**
- Cabin manager coordinates cabin response
- Prepare cabin and passengers
- Execute evacuation if ordered
- Provide first aid

**Operations Control:**
- Monitor emergency flight
- Coordinate ground resources
- Notify stakeholders
- Support crew decisions

### 4.2 External Coordination

**Air Traffic Control:**
- Declare emergency (PAN-PAN or MAYDAY)
- Request priority handling
- Inform of intentions
- Coordinate emergency services

**Airport Authority:**
- Request airport services
- Specify requirements
- Coordinate emergency equipment
- Arrange post-landing support

**Emergency Services:**
- Fire and rescue
- Medical services
- Law enforcement
- H2 specialists (if H2 emergency)

## 6. POST-EMERGENCY ACTIONS

### 6.1 Immediate Post-Emergency

**Aircraft:**
- Secure aircraft
- Preserve evidence
- Quarantine systems
- Photo documentation

**Personnel:**
- Medical assessment (all crew)
- Psychological support
- Rest and recovery
- Defusing/debriefing

### 6.2 Investigation

**Initial Response:**
- Notify regulatory authority
- Preserve aircraft and evidence
- Secure data (FDR, CVR, CAOS logs)
- Witness statements

**Investigation Process:**
- Appoint investigation team
- Gather data and evidence
- Analyze findings
- Identify root causes
- Develop recommendations

### 6.3 Corrective Actions

**Immediate Actions:**
- Address imminent hazards
- Fleet-wide alerts if applicable
- Interim procedures
- Enhanced monitoring

**Long-Term Actions:**
- Design modifications
- Procedure improvements
- Training enhancements
- System upgrades

## 7. EMERGENCY DRILLS AND TRAINING

### 7.1 Crew Emergency Training

**Annual Requirements:**
- Emergency procedures review
- Hands-on drill (evacuation, fire fighting)
- Scenario-based training
- CRM in emergencies

### 7.2 Emergency Response Drills

**Full-Scale Exercise (Annual):**
- Aircraft emergency simulation
- All stakeholders participate
- Airport emergency services involved
- H2 emergency scenario included
- Lessons learned documented

**Tabletop Exercise (Semi-Annual):**
- Emergency scenarios discussed
- Decision-making practiced
- Coordination rehearsed
- Improvements identified
