## Key Concepts

### Circularity Strategy

The ATA 02 circularity strategy is organized around **four strategic pillars**:

1. **Resource Optimization** — Minimize energy and material consumption
2. **Lifecycle Extension** — Maximize hardware and software system longevity
3. **Circular Flows** — Enable reuse, refurbishment, and recycling loops
4. **Transparency & Governance** — Provide visibility and accountability through metrics and reporting

### Carbon Accounting

Carbon accounting for digital operations covers:
- **Scope 1**: Minimal direct emissions (backup generators)
- **Scope 2**: Electricity for data centers, edge devices, network infrastructure
- **Scope 3**: Hardware procurement, cloud services, e-waste disposal

Target: **60% reduction in total carbon footprint by 2028** (vs. 2024 baseline)

### Regulatory Landscape

Key regulations affecting ATA 02 circularity:

- **[EU Taxonomy Regulation](https://finance.ec.europa.eu/sustainable-finance/tools-and-standards/eu-taxonomy-sustainable-activities_en)**: Technical screening criteria for sustainable activities
- **[EU AI Act](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/)**: Environmental sustainability requirements for AI systems
- **[EASA CS-25](https://www.easa.europa.eu/document-library/certification-specifications/cs-25-amendment-27)**: Aircraft certification (indirectly relevant for safety-critical digital systems)
- **GHG Protocol**: Corporate carbon accounting standard
