## 8. Maintenance & Notes

### 8.1 Special Handling

* Store in dry environment to prevent staining (though stainless resists corrosion)
* Keep separate from carbon steel to avoid contamination
* Handle in bulk quantities

### 8.2 Inspection Points

* Verify thread condition - no damage
* Check head for damage or deformation
* Verify material grade marking if critical application
* Confirm dimensions with go/no-go gauges if quality concerns

### 8.3 Maintenance Requirements

* Inspect for damage during disassembly
* Replace if threads damaged or bolt stretched/yielded
* Check torque during periodic maintenance if critical joint
* Replace per maintenance schedule or if corrosion/damage noted

### 8.4 Design Notes

* **Material Selection Rationale:**
  - A2 stainless provides good corrosion resistance
  - Suitable for most indoor/outdoor applications
  - Non-magnetic (mostly) if magnetic properties are a concern
  - Marine grade A4 (316) stainless if higher corrosion resistance needed
* **Installation Notes:**
  - Verify adequate thread engagement (minimum 1x diameter = 8mm)
  - Use appropriate washer to distribute load and protect surface
  - Apply anti-seize compound for dissimilar metal joints
  - Do not over-torque - stainless steel can gall
* **Substitution:**
  - Can substitute A4 (316) stainless if higher corrosion resistance required
  - Do NOT substitute carbon steel in corrosive environments
  - Verify strength requirements if considering different grade
