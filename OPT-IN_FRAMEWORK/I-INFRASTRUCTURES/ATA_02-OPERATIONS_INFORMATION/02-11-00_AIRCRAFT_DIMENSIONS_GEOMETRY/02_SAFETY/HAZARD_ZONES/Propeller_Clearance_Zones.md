# Propeller Clearance Zones

**Document ID:** AMPEL360-02-11-00-SAF-021  
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Overview

The AMPEL360 BWB aircraft uses electric propulsors driven by fuel cell power. While not traditional propellers, these propulsor units present similar hazards during ground operations. This document defines safety zones and procedures.

## Propulsor Configuration

### Physical Specifications

**Number of Propulsors:** 4 (2 per wing)  
**Type:** Electric ducted fans  
**Diameter:** 2.4m per unit  
**Location:** Wing trailing edge, STA 28-32m  
**Height Above Ground:** 3.2m (static, MTOW)

**Power System:**
- Electric drive: Fuel cell powered
- Maximum power: 2.5 MW per unit
- Rotation speed: Variable (0-1800 RPM)
- Noise level: 75 dB at 25m

### Danger Zones

**Primary Danger Zone (Red):**
- Radius: 10m from propulsor center
- Height: Full height (ground to 5m)
- Status: NO ENTRY when propulsors operating
- Marking: Red barrier cones, warning signs

**Secondary Danger Zone (Orange):**
- Radius: 10-25m from propulsor center
- Height: Ground to 5m
- Status: CAUTION, restricted access
- Marking: Orange cones, caution signs

**Awareness Zone (Yellow):**
- Radius: 25-50m from propulsor center
- Height: Ground to 5m
- Status: BE AWARE, hearing protection
- Marking: Yellow awareness signs

## Hazards

### Physical Hazards

**Blade Contact:**
- Rotating blades at high speed
- Severe injury or fatality potential
- Blade throw risk (failure condition)
- Impact zone extends beyond danger zone

**Ingestion Risk:**
- Personnel or equipment suction
- FOD ingestion (propulsor damage)
- Small objects become projectiles
- Airflow extends forward of propulsor

**Airflow Effects:**
- High-velocity air stream (>100 kt at full power)
- Debris and loose items blown
- Personnel knocked off balance
- Equipment displacement

### Environmental Hazards

**Noise:**
- Continuous exposure: >85 dB within 25m
- Peak noise: 110 dB at full power (5m)
- Hearing damage risk
- Communication interference

**Vibration:**
- Ground-transmitted vibration
- Equipment interference
- Personnel discomfort
- Structural resonance (nearby objects)

### Electrical Hazards (Specific to Electric Propulsors)

**High Voltage:**
- 800V DC bus system
- Shock hazard if accessed improperly
- Arc flash risk
- Required PPE and procedures

**Electromagnetic Interference:**
- Strong EM fields during operation
- Electronic device interference
- Pacemaker caution
- Compass deviation

## Safety Procedures

### Pre-Start Procedures

**Flight Crew:**
1. Propulsor area clear verification
2. External power disconnected
3. Ground crew clear confirmation
4. Rotating beacon activation

**Ground Crew:**
1. All personnel clear of danger zones
2. Equipment removed from danger zones
3. FOD walk-down completed
4. Ready-to-start signal to crew

### Start Sequence

**Standard Start:**
1. Rotating beacon ON (visual warning)
2. External horn (3-second warning)
3. Propulsor start sequence initiated
4. Monitor for abnormalities

**Personnel Actions:**
- Maintain 25m minimum distance
- Hearing protection required
- Visual monitoring of propulsors
- Communication via radio only

### Operating Procedures

**Propulsors Running:**
- No entry to red zones (0-10m)
- Orange zone access: Authorized only, radio clearance
- Yellow zone: Hearing protection mandatory
- Equipment operations: Coordinate with crew

**Power Changes:**
- Crew announces power increases
- Personnel acknowledge and verify clearance
- Increase airflow awareness
- Secure loose items

### Shutdown Procedures

**Propulsor Shutdown:**
1. Crew announces shutdown
2. Propulsors spool down (60 seconds)
3. Rotating beacon remains ON
4. Crew confirms all-stopped

**Ground Crew Actions:**
- Wait for crew confirmation
- Verify all propulsors stopped
- Visual inspection before approach
- Approach from forward/side (not aft)

## Emergency Procedures

### Propulsor Fire

**Indications:**
- Visual flames or smoke
- Crew fire warning
- Unusual sounds/vibrations
- Burning smell

**Actions:**
1. Crew: Propulsor emergency shutdown
2. Ground: Evacuate 50m radius
3. Fire services: Approach/suppress
4. Personnel: Upwind position
5. Aircraft: Power removal if possible

### Propulsor Failure

**Blade Separation:**
- Immediate 100m evacuation
- Debris field assessment
- No aircraft approach until cleared
- Investigation team notification

**Electrical Failure:**
- Propulsor may not stop normally
- Enhanced caution required
- Electrical isolation procedures
- Maintenance coordination

### Personnel in Danger Zone

**Discovery:**
- Immediate propulsor shutdown (crew)
- Emergency stop signal (ground)
- Assess personnel status
- Medical response if needed

**Prevention:**
- Visual sweep before start
- Camera system monitoring
- Ground crew accountability
- Barrier systems

- Height limit: 3m near propulsors
- Lateral clearance: 10m when operating
- Equipment securing: Mandatory
- Operator training: Required

### FOD Control

**Pre-Start:**
- FOD walk-down mandatory
- Intake screens checked (if installed)
- Area swept clean
- Debris removal

**During Operations:**
- Monitor for debris
- Secure all equipment
- No loose items

## Special Conditions

### Towing Operations

**Propulsors Stopped:**
- Verification: Visual + crew confirmation
- Tow bar attachment clearance: 2m minimum
- Tow vehicle positioning: Avoid propulsor zones
- Communication: Continuous

**Emergency Towing:**
- Propulsor safety pins installed (if applicable)
- Enhanced clearances
- Additional personnel monitoring
- Documentation required

### Maintenance Operations

**Propulsor Maintenance:**
- Lock-out/tag-out procedures
- Electrical isolation verified
- Rotation prevention (if applicable)
- Safety barriers erected

**Adjacent Maintenance:**
- Propulsor safety verified
- Clearance maintained
- Communication established
- Supervision required

### Weather Considerations

**High Winds:**
- Increased airflow effects
- Debris control critical
- Personnel stability awareness
- Operations limitations (>30 kt)

**Precipitation:**
- Visibility reduced
- Slippery surfaces
- Equipment security
- Enhanced caution
