---

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the comprehensive development plan for geometry prototyping and validation of the AMPEL360 Blended Wing Body aircraft. The plan encompasses scale model fabrication, measurement fixture development, wind tunnel testing, and full-scale geometry validation through a systematic, phase-gated approach.

**Program Duration:** 18 months  
**Total Budget:** $1,450,000  
**Key Deliverable:** Validated aircraft geometry ready for detailed design and manufacturing

---

## Program Objectives

### Primary Objectives

1. **Validate BWB Geometry Concept**
   - Verify aerodynamic shape achieves design performance
   - Confirm structural feasibility of blended wing-body design
   - Validate ground clearances meet operational requirements

2. **Establish Reference Coordinate System**
   - Define primary aircraft datum (FS 0, BL 0, WL 0)
   - Install precision measurement infrastructure
   - Enable traceable dimensional control throughout development

3. **Develop Measurement Capabilities**
   - Procure and calibrate precision measurement equipment
   - Train personnel in metrology best practices
   - Establish quality control procedures

4. **Generate Validation Data**
   - Wind tunnel test data for aerodynamic validation
   - Ground clearance measurements under various attitudes
   - Dimensional verification of scale models and prototypes

### Success Criteria

| Metric | Target | Status Indicator |
|--------|--------|------------------|
| Geometry validation complete | 100% | All critical dimensions within tolerance |
| Wind tunnel testing | Complete | Aerodynamic database established |
| Measurement system operational | 100% | All fixtures calibrated and in use |
| Documentation | Complete | All deliverables approved |
| Budget compliance | ≤ $1.45M | On budget |
| Schedule compliance | 18 months | On schedule |

## Risk Management

### Top Risks

| Risk ID | Risk Description | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---------|------------------|-------------|--------|------------|
| R-01 | Scale model fabrication delay | Medium | High | Early start, contingency in schedule |
| R-02 | Wind tunnel availability | Low | High | Reserve tunnel time 6 months in advance |
| R-03 | Measurement system calibration issues | Medium | Medium | Backup calibration lab identified |
| R-04 | Design changes during prototyping | High | Medium | Change control process, frozen baseline |
| R-05 | Budget overrun | Medium | High | Monthly reviews, management reserve |
| R-06 | Personnel availability | Medium | Medium | Cross-training, backup resources |
| R-07 | Supplier delays | Medium | Medium | Multiple suppliers, long-lead procurement |
| R-08 | Dimensional tolerances not met | Low | High | Progressive testing, early verification |

### Risk Response Strategies

**For R-01 (Scale model delay):**
- Weekly progress reviews with supplier
- Milestone payments tied to deliverables
- Backup CNC shop identified

**For R-04 (Design changes):**
- Design freeze after Phase 1
- Change review board for any modifications
- Impact assessment required before approval

**For R-05 (Budget overrun):**
- Monthly cost tracking
- Variance analysis and corrective actions
- Management reserve for unforeseen issues

## Quality Assurance

### Quality Control Plan

**Design Reviews:**
- Preliminary Design Review (PDR) - End of Month 2
- Critical Design Review (CDR) - End of Month 3
- Test Readiness Reviews (TRR) - Before each test phase

**Inspections:**
- First Article Inspection (scale models)
- In-Process Inspections (fixtures, mockups)
- Final Acceptance Inspection (all hardware)

**Audits:**
- Monthly project audits (schedule, cost, quality)
- Supplier audits (for critical components)
- Process audits (measurement procedures)

### Acceptance Criteria

**Scale Models:**
- Dimensional accuracy: ±0.5mm (1:20), ±1mm (1:50)
- Surface finish: Ra ≤ 1.6 μm
- Instrumentation functional: 100%

**Measurement Fixtures:**
- Position accuracy: ±0.5mm
- Repeatability: ±0.2mm
- Calibration valid: Current certification

**Test Data:**
- Measurement uncertainty documented
- Outliers investigated and resolved
- Data traceability established

## Conclusion

This Geometry Prototype Development Plan provides a comprehensive roadmap for validating the AMPEL360 BWB aircraft geometry through systematic prototyping, testing, and measurement. The 18-month, $1.45M program de-risks the full-scale aircraft development by:

1. Validating aerodynamic performance via wind tunnel testing
2. Confirming ground operational feasibility through mockup testing
3. Establishing precision measurement infrastructure for production
4. Generating validated geometry data for detailed design

Successful execution of this plan will provide high confidence in the BWB geometry and enable transition to the next phase of aircraft development.
