## 4. Trade Study Results

### 4.1 Wingspan Optimization

**Selected: 70.0 m (ICAO Code E)**

**Rationale:**
- L/D: 21.0 (optimal performance)
- Airport access: 3,000+ Code E airports
- Structural weight: 8,500 kg (acceptable)
- Manufacturing: Feasible complexity

**Alternatives Considered:**
- 65 m: Insufficient range (-280 km)
- 75 m: Excessive structural weight (+800 kg)
- 80 m: Airport limitations (Code F = 500 airports only)

### 4.2 Passenger Capacity

**Selected: 220 Passengers (single-class)**

**Rationale:**
- Optimal CASM: $0.0111/ASM
- Competitive with A321XLR/737MAX
- Wide-body comfort differentiator
- Efficient BWB volume utilization

**Configuration:**
- Forward (Rows 1-10): 2-3-2 = 70 seats
- Center (Rows 11-20): 2-4-2 = 80 seats
- Aft (Rows 21-25): 2-4-2 = 70 seats
- Seat pitch: 32" standard
- Seat width: 18" wide-body comfort

### 4.3 H₂ Tank Location

**Selected: Three tanks, centerline, below cabin floor**

**Rationale:**
- Optimal safety (9/10 score)
- Maximum CG control (8.5 m range)
- Best maintenance access
- Lowest system cost
- Minimal performance impact

## 6. Critical Findings

### 6.1 Design Achievements

✅ **Weight Efficiency:** 51% OEW/MTOW (excellent for capability)  
✅ **Range Performance:** 6,915 km (6.4% margin over requirement)  
✅ **Aerodynamic Efficiency:** L/D = 21.0 (31% better than conventional)  
✅ **CG Envelope:** All load cases within limits (15-42% MAC)  
✅ **Field Performance:** Meets Code E requirements  
✅ **Safety:** Comprehensive H₂ safety system design  

### 6.2 Critical Design Points

⚠️ **Most Aft CG:** 32.5% MAC (full aft seating + forward cargo)
- Within limits but requires careful loading
- Loading manual must emphasize this case
- Automated load planning recommended

⚠️ **Wingtip Clearance:** 70 m span on Code E taxiways
- Only 1.5 m margin from taxiway edge
- Requires precise centerline tracking
- Enhanced pilot training essential

⚠️ **H₂ Infrastructure:** Initial airport network limited
- 2027: 5-10 airports with H₂ capability
- 2030: 25 airports (network viable)
- 2035: 100+ airports (full operations)

### 6.3 Risk Areas

| Risk | Impact | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-------------|------------|
| Weight growth | Medium | Medium | 5,000 kg margin allocated |
| H₂ infrastructure delay | High | Medium | Airport partnerships active |
| CG excursion in ops | Medium | Low | Automated fuel management |
| Certification challenges | Medium | Low | Early EASA engagement |
