# PNR-02-10-201 - Aircraft Weighing Kit 200t

**Part Number:** PNR-02-10-201  
**Type:** GSE (Ground Support Equipment)  
**Manufacturer:** Weightech  
**Status:** Active

## Description

Complete aircraft weighing kit designed for accurate weight and balance measurements of the AMPEL360 BWB H2 Hy-E aircraft. Suitable for aircraft up to 200 metric tons maximum weight.

## Kit Components

### Primary Equipment
1. **Load Cells (3x)**
   - Capacity: 100,000 kg each
   - Accuracy: ±0.1% of reading
   - Type: Hydraulic compression
   - Calibration: Annual

2. **Weighing Platform Jacks (3x)**
   - Lift capacity: 100,000 kg each
   - Stroke: 600mm
   - Platform size: 800mm × 600mm
   - Locking mechanism: Automatic safety locks

3. **Digital Display Unit**
   - Multi-channel input (3 load cells)
   - Resolution: 1 kg
   - Display: LCD, backlit
   - Data logging: USB interface
   - Battery backup: 8 hours

### Accessories
- Leveling plates (3 sets)
- Calibration weights (certified)
- Connecting cables (20m, shielded)
- Transit case (ruggedized)
- Operating manual
- Calibration certificates

## Specifications

- **Total System Capacity:** 300,000 kg
- **System Accuracy:** ±0.15% full scale
- **Operating Temperature:** -20°C to +50°C
- **Power Requirements:** 110-240VAC or battery
- **Calibration Interval:** 12 months
- **Certification:** ISO 17025 accredited

## Operating Procedure Summary

1. **Setup:**
   - Position jacks under aircraft jack points
   - Level aircraft using spirit levels
   - Connect load cells to display unit

2. **Weighing:**
   - Zero all load cells
   - Slowly raise aircraft (max 50mm)
   - Allow stabilization (5 minutes)
   - Record readings from all three points

3. **Calculations:**
   - Total weight = Sum of all load cells
   - CG calculation using moment arms
   - Compare with limits

## Maintenance Requirements

- Daily inspection before use
- Monthly function check
- Annual calibration by certified lab
- Load cell replacement: 10 years or 1000 cycles

## Safety Precautions

⚠️ **Critical:**
- Maximum capacity must not be exceeded
- Aircraft must be on level surface
- Safety locks engaged at all times
- Minimum 2 qualified operators required
- Emergency lowering system tested before each use
