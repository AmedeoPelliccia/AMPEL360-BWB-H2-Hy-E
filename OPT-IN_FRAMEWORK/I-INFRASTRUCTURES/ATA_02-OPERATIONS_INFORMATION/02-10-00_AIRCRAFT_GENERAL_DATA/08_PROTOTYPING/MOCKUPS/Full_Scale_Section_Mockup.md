## 1. Mockup Overview

### 1.1 Purpose and Objectives

**Primary Objectives:**
1. **Validate cabin dimensions and ergonomics** - Passenger comfort, seat pitch, aisle width
2. **Verify door locations and access** - Emergency egress, boarding efficiency
3. **Confirm systems integration** - Overhead bins, lighting, HVAC distribution
4. **Ground handling assessment** - Loading procedures, ground equipment access
5. **Human factors evaluation** - Crew movement, sightlines, controls reach

**Secondary Objectives:**
1. Visual representation for stakeholders and customers
2. Marketing and sales tool (airline presentations)
3. Training environment for ground crew
4. Regulatory demonstration (EASA/FAA coordination)

**Selected Section:** Forward fuselage from nose to aft of passenger door L2
- Fuselage Station (FS): 0 to 18,000 mm

## 4. Evaluation Program

### 4.1 Dimensional Validation

**Objectives:**
- Confirm compliance with CS-25.785 (Seats, berths, safety belts)
- Verify CS-25.807 (Emergency exits)
- Validate aisle width, seat pitch, headroom

**Measurement Campaign:**
1. Laser scan entire interior (3D point cloud)
2. Compare to CAD nominal geometry
3. Document deviations > Â±5 mm
4. Photographic documentation (500+ images)

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- 95% of measured points within Â±10 mm of nominal
- Zero deviations > Â±20 mm in critical zones (doors, aisles, emergency equipment)

### 4.2 Ergonomic Assessment

**Participants:**
- 50 volunteers (range of anthropometry: 5th percentile female to 95th percentile male)
- 10 flight attendants (operational procedures evaluation)
- 4 pilots (cockpit ergonomics)

**Test Scenarios:**
1. **Passenger Boarding:**
   - Timed boarding (44 passengers, single door)
   - Baggage stowage (carry-on, overhead bins)
   - Seat access (window seats with occupied aisle seats)

2. **Emergency Egress:**
   - Evacuation time (all passengers, 2 doors)
   - Target: < 90 seconds (CS-25.803 requirement)
   - Crew coordination and command

3. **In-Service Operations:**
   - Galley workflow (meal service preparation)
   - Lavatory accessibility
   - Flight attendant movement in aisle

4. **Maintenance Access:**
   - Overhead bin removal/installation
   - PSU access for maintenance
   - Under-floor access panels

**Measurement:**
- Time-motion study (video analysis)
- Questionnaires (NASA-TLX workload, comfort ratings)
- Anthropometric fit analysis (reach, clearances)

### 4.3 Human Factors Evaluation

**Focus Areas:**
1. **Sightlines:** Cockpit windows, cabin attendant visibility
2. **Reach Envelopes:** Controls, overhead bins, emergency equipment
3. **Wayfinding:** Signage, lighting, exit identification
4. **Comfort:** Seat pitch, knee room, shoulder room, headroom
5. **Accessibility:** PRM (Persons with Reduced Mobility) considerations

**Deliverable:** Human Factors Evaluation Report (HFER-02-10-001)

**Process:**
1. Daily log of observations during evaluation
2. Weekly design review meetings
3. Change requests categorized (critical, important, nice-to-have)
