# RQ-02-10-30-001: Passenger Capacity

**Requirement ID:** RQ-02-10-30-001  
**Category:** Capacities  
**Priority:** High  
**Status:** Approved

## Requirement

The aircraft shall accommodate 220 passengers in a typical two-class configuration with adequate comfort and safety provisions.

**Seating Configurations:**

| Configuration | Economy | Business | Total | Layout |
|--------------|---------|----------|-------|--------|
| **Typical 2-Class** | 180 | 40 | 220 | 9-abreast economy, 6-abreast business |
| **High Density** | 260 | 0 | 260 | 9-abreast single class |
| **Premium** | 150 | 60 | 210 | 8-abreast economy, 4-abreast business |

**Passenger Facilities:**
- Lavatories: Minimum 8 (1 per 35 passengers)
- Galleys: 4 full-service galleys
- Overhead bins: 0.06 mÂ³ per passenger
- Emergency exits: Per CS-25.807 (10 Type-A or Type-I exits)

## Rationale

Passenger capacity requirements ensure:
- Market competitiveness (comparable to A320/737 replacement)
- Economic viability for operators
- BWB wide cabin advantage utilized
- Comfort standards maintained or exceeded
- Emergency evacuation compliance

## BWB Cabin Layout Advantages

- **Wide Cabin (32m):** Panoramic window views, reduced claustrophobia
- **Open Plan:** Flexible seating arrangements, premium space utilization
- **Natural Light:** Extensive window area throughout cabin
- **Low Noise:** Engines mounted on top, reduced cabin noise

## Verification

- **Method:** Inspection and Demonstration
- **Procedure:** Mock-up evaluation and certification tests
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Seating layouts meet anthropometric requirements
  - Emergency evacuation demonstration successful (90 seconds)
  - Egress paths compliant with regulations
  - Passenger comfort validated through surveys

## Compliance

- CS-25.807: Emergency exits
- CS-25.809: Emergency exit arrangement
- CS-25.813: Emergency exit access
- CS-25.815: Width of aisle
- CS-25.817: Maximum number of seats abreast
