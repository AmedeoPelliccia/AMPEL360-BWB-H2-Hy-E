# RQ-02-10-60-001: Crew Requirements

**Requirement ID:** RQ-02-10-60-001  
**Category:** Operational  
**Priority:** High  
**Status:** Approved

## Requirement

All flight crew members operating the AMPEL360 aircraft shall meet specific training, licensing, and recency requirements tailored to the BWB configuration and hydrogen propulsion system.

## Flight Crew Licensing

**Minimum Qualifications:**
- EASA ATPL or CPL with Type Rating
- Multi-crew cooperation certificate
- Instrument rating
- English Language Proficiency Level 4 minimum
- Medical Certificate: Class 1 (for commercial operations)

**Type Rating Requirements:**
- AMPEL360 BWB Type Rating
- Minimum 25 hours training (simulator + aircraft)
- BWB-specific handling characteristics
- H₂ systems and emergency procedures
- CAOS system operation

## BWB-Specific Training

**Flight Characteristics:**
- Wide body aerodynamics and handling
- Different pitch and yaw response
- Modified stall characteristics
- Ground handling (wide track landing gear)
- Crosswind landing techniques
- Engine-out procedures (distributed propulsion)

**Simulator Training (20 hours minimum):**
- Normal procedures (all phases)
- Abnormal procedures
- Emergency procedures
- H₂ system failures
- CAOS system failures
- Low visibility operations

## H₂ System Training

**Ground School (16 hours):**
- H₂ properties and safety
- Fuel cell operation principles
- H₂ fuel system operation
- Emergency procedures (leak, fire, system failure)
- Refueling procedures and safety
- Ground operations safety
- Regulatory requirements

**Practical Training:**
- Pre-flight H₂ system inspection
- Fuel management procedures
- Abnormal H₂ system operations
- Emergency shutdown procedures
- Fire fighting (H₂-specific)

## CAOS System Training

**System Operation (8 hours):**
- CAOS interface and displays
- Digital twin interpretation
- Predictive maintenance alerts
- Performance optimization tools
- Decision support features
- Override procedures
- System limitations

**Philosophy:**
- Human remains in command
- CAOS as advisory system
- Crew resource management with AI
- Appropriate trust and skepticism

## Recency Requirements

**Currency:**
- 3 takeoffs and landings in last 90 days (type)
- 2 ILS approaches in last 90 days
- Annual simulator proficiency check
- Biennial line check by check pilot

**Recurrent Training (Annual):**
- Emergency procedures review (4 hours simulator)
- H₂ safety refresher (2 hours)
- CAOS updates and new features (2 hours)
- Regulatory updates

## Cabin Crew Requirements

**Basic Training:**
- Cabin safety procedures
- Emergency evacuation (BWB exits)
- Fire fighting
- First aid and CPR
- Dangerous goods

**H₂-Specific Training (4 hours):**
- H₂ safety awareness
- H₂ leak detection and response
- Passenger briefings (H₂ safety)
- Emergency procedures (H₂ related)

**BWB-Specific:**
- Wide cabin layout familiarization
- Exit locations and operation (10 exits)
- Evacuation procedures (90-second rule)
- Passenger service in BWB configuration

## Medical Requirements

**Flight Crew:**
- EASA Class 1 Medical Certificate
- Renewal: Every 12 months (over 40 years old)
- No specific H₂ exposure restrictions

**Cabin Crew:**
- Cabin crew attestation
- First aid training current
- Physical fitness for duties

## Operational Experience

**New Type Rating Holders:**
- Minimum 25 hours supervised operating experience (SOE)
- Release to line by training captain
- 100 hours monitored by check pilots

**Captain Upgrade:**
- Minimum 250 hours as First Officer on type
- Captain's course and checkride
- Command training under supervision

## Crew Resource Management (CRM)

**CRM with CAOS:**
- Human-AI interaction training
- Decision-making with AI recommendations
- Maintaining situational awareness
- Appropriate trust calibration
- Crew coordination in CAOS environment

## Rationale

Crew requirements ensure:
- Safe operation of novel aircraft configuration
- Proper handling of H₂ propulsion system
- Effective use of CAOS capabilities
- Regulatory compliance
- Crew confidence and competence
- Passenger safety

## Verification

- **Method:** Demonstration and Inspection
- **Procedure:**
  - Training program approval by authority
  - Instructor qualification verification
  - Training device qualification
  - Crew checkride observations
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Training program approved by EASA
  - 100% pass rate on final checks (with retrain if needed)
  - Crew feedback positive (>4.0/5.0)
  - Safety record demonstrates competence

## Training Providers

**Initial Training:**
- AMPEL360 Training Center
- Authorized Training Organizations (ATO)
- Simulator: AMPEL360 Level D Full Flight Simulator

**Recurrent Training:**
- Operator's approved training program
- Online modules (CAOS updates)
- Simulator sessions at approved facilities

## Compliance

- EASA Part-FCL: Flight Crew Licensing
- CS-FSTD(A): Flight Simulation Training Devices
- EASA Part-OPS: Air Operations
- ICAO Annex 1: Personnel Licensing
- Operator's Operations Manual
