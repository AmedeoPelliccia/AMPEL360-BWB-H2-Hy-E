## AMPEL360 BWB H2 Hy-E Type Rating Syllabus

### Course Overview
- **Duration:** 42 days (6 weeks)
- **Total Hours:** 168 hours
- **Ground School:** 80 hours
- **Simulator:** 60 hours
- **Flight Training:** 28 hours

### Prerequisites
- Valid ATPL or CPL
- Multi-engine rating
- English Language Proficiency Level 4+
- Medical Certificate Class 1

## Module 1: Aircraft Systems (20 hours)

### Week 1: BWB Configuration & H₂ Systems
**Day 1-2: Blended Wing Body Fundamentals**
- BWB aerodynamics and characteristics
- Unique handling qualities
- Control surface configuration
- Performance characteristics vs conventional aircraft

**Day 3-4: Hydrogen Fuel System**
- Cryogenic hydrogen storage principles
- Tank design and safety features
- Pressure and temperature monitoring
- H₂ refueling procedures
- Emergency H₂ procedures

**Day 5: Fuel Cell Technology**
- PEM fuel cell operation
- Dual stack configuration
- Power management
- Fuel cell limitations and emergencies

## Module 2: CAOS System Training (16 hours)

### Week 2: AI-Powered Operations
**Day 1-2: CAOS Introduction**
- Philosophy of human-AI collaboration
- System architecture and capabilities
- Advisory vs. control modes
- Pilot authority and override procedures

**Day 3: CAOS Interface Training**
- Primary advisory display
- Explanation system usage
- Digital twin dashboard
- Data interpretation

**Day 4: CAOS in Normal Operations**
- Flight planning with CAOS
- In-flight optimization
- Predictive maintenance awareness
- Fleet learning benefits

## Module 3: Normal Procedures (24 hours)

### Week 3: Standard Operations
**Pre-flight & Start**
- Aircraft inspection (H₂ specific)
- Cockpit preparation
- CAOS initialization
- Fuel cell startup procedures

**Taxi, Takeoff & Climb**
- BWB ground handling
- V-speeds and rotation technique
- Climb performance
- CAOS climb optimization

**Cruise & Descent**
- Cruise procedures
- CAOS fuel management
- Descent planning
- Approach preparation

**Landing & Shutdown**
- BWB landing characteristics
- Fuel cell shutdown sequence
- Post-flight procedures

## Module 4: Abnormal & Emergency Procedures (20 hours)

### Week 4: Emergency Response
**H₂ System Emergencies**
- H₂ leak detection and response
- Tank overpressure
- Emergency H₂ venting
- Ground H₂ emergencies

**Fuel Cell Emergencies**
- Single stack failure
- Dual stack failure
- Fuel cell fire
- Battery emergency mode

**BWB-Specific Emergencies**
- Asymmetric control issues
- Center of gravity emergencies
- Unusual attitude recovery

## Module 5: Simulator Training (60 hours)

### Weeks 5-6: Practical Application

**Simulator Sessions 1-4: Normal Operations**
- Complete flight profiles
- CAOS interaction practice
- H₂ system management
- BWB handling practice

**Simulator Sessions 5-8: Abnormal Procedures**
- System failures
- Degraded operations
- CAOS unavailable scenarios

**Simulator Sessions 9-12: Emergency Scenarios**
- H₂ leak scenarios
- Fuel cell failures
- Multiple system failures
- Forced landings

**Simulator Sessions 13-15: Proficiency Check Prep**
- Mixed normal and abnormal
- Crew coordination
- Decision making under pressure

## Module 6: Flight Training (28 hours)

### Flight Training Progression

**Flights 1-4: Familiarization (8 hours)**
- Aircraft handling
- Normal procedures
- Basic maneuvers
- Pattern work

**Flights 5-8: Advanced Operations (8 hours)**
- Cross-country
- CAOS utilization
- Different weights and CGs
- Various weather conditions

**Flights 9-11: Emergency Training (6 hours)**
- Simulated emergencies
- Single engine operations (single stack)
- Abnormal procedures

**Flights 12-14: Line Operations (6 hours)**
- Revenue-style flights
- Complex airports
- Full procedures
- Crew coordination

## Final Assessment

### Type Rating Check
- Oral examination (3 hours)
- Simulator check (4 hours)
- Line check (flight)

### Success Criteria
- 80% minimum on written exams
- Satisfactory completion of simulator check
- Successful line check
- CAOS competency demonstration

## Continuing Qualification

**Recurrent Training:** Every 12 months
- 8 hours ground review
- 4 hours simulator
- Line check
