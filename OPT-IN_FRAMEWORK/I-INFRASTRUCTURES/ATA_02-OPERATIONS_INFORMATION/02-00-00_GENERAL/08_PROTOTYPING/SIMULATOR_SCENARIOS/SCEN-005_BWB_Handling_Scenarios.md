# SCEN-005 - BWB Handling Scenarios

**Scenario ID:** SCEN-005  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2025-09-28  
**Status:** Active  
**Complexity:** Moderate

## Scenario 5.1: BWB Ground Handling

**Phase:** Ground operations
**Location:** Various airports

**Training Objectives:**
- BWB taxi characteristics
- Turning radius awareness
- Wing tip clearance
- Nose wheel steering

**Scenario Elements:**
- Narrow taxiways
- Tight turns
- Wing tip clearance challenges
- Variable wind conditions

**BWB-Specific Considerations:**
- Wide wing span
- Different visual references
- Center of rotation
- Ground effect preparation

**Success Criteria:**
- Safe taxi operations
- No wing tip contacts
- Smooth directional control
- Proper speed management

## Scenario 5.2: BWB Takeoff Rotation

**Phase:** Takeoff from various runways

**Training Objectives:**
- BWB rotation technique
- Pitch rate control
- Lift-off characteristics
- Ground effect differences

**Scenario Variations:**
- Different weights
- Various CG positions
- Crosswind conditions
- Short vs. long runways

**BWB Characteristics:**
- Gradual rotation required
- Different pitch attitude for liftoff
- Ground effect more pronounced
- Unique visual cues

**Success Criteria:**
- Smooth rotation at VR
- Proper pitch rate
- Lift-off at calculated speed
- No tail strike

## Scenario 5.3: BWB Approach and Landing

**Phase:** Approach and landing

**Training Objectives:**
- BWB approach characteristics
- Landing flare technique
- Touchdown technique
- Go-around if needed

**Scenario Elements:**
- Various wind conditions
- Different runway lengths
- Day and night operations
- Wet vs. dry runways

**BWB Considerations:**
- Lower approach angle
- Different flare characteristics
- Wide fuselage touchdown area
- Unique visual references

**Success Criteria:**
- Stabilized approach
- Smooth flare and touchdown
- Centerline accuracy
- Proper speed control

## Scenario 5.4: BWB in Turbulence

**Phase:** Cruise through turbulent area

**Training Objectives:**
- BWB response to turbulence
- Passenger comfort management
- Control inputs in turbulence
- Speed management

**Scenario Elements:**
- Light to moderate turbulence
- Various altitudes
- Speed adjustments required
- CAOS turbulence advisory

**BWB Characteristics:**
- Different turbulence response vs conventional
- Large wing area effects
- Passenger comfort considerations
- Load factor management

**Success Criteria:**
- Aircraft control maintained
- Appropriate speed reduction
- Smooth control inputs
- Passenger comfort prioritized

## Scenario 5.5: Asymmetric Thrust (Single Fuel Cell)

**Phase:** Multiple phases

**Training Objectives:**
- Asymmetric power handling
- Rudder/aileron coordination
- Performance limitations
- Safe operations with power loss

**Scenario Elements:**
- Takeoff with single stack failure
- Cruise with one stack
- Approach and landing single stack
- Go-around consideration

**Challenges:**
- Limited power available
- Possible thrust asymmetry
- Performance degradation
- Increased pilot workload

**Success Criteria:**
- Aircraft control maintained
- Proper technique used
- Safe landing accomplished
- Performance limitations respected

**Training Objectives:**

- Speed management
