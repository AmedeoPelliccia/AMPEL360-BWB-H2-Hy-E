## H₂ LEAK DETECTION

**Condition:** H₂ LEAK warning illuminated or detected

---

### MEMORY ITEMS (Immediate Action - Bold)

1. **H₂ MAIN VALVE** ........................... **CLOSE**
2. **FUEL CELL SHUTDOWN** .............. **EXECUTE**
3. **BATTERY MODE** .......................... **ACTIVATE**
4. **VENTILATION** .............................. **MAXIMUM**

---

### END OF MEMORY ITEMS

---

### SUBSEQUENT ACTIONS

5. H₂ Leak Location ................................ IDENTIFY
   - Check CAOS diagnostic display
   - Note leak severity indication

6. Fire Detection .................................... MONITOR
   - Check all fire loops
   - Prepare fire suppression if needed

7. Fuel Cell Status ................................. VERIFY OFF
   - Both stacks shutdown
   - No H₂ flow

8. Electrical Power ................................ CHECK
   - Battery mode active
   - Essential systems powered
   - Non-essential systems off

9. Aircraft Status .................................. ASSESS
   - Altitude
   - Fuel (H₂) remaining
   - Distance to suitable airports
   - Weather at divert airports

10. ATC ................................................. NOTIFY
    - PAN-PAN or MAYDAY as appropriate
    - Request priority handling
    - Inform of H₂ leak situation

11. Cabin Crew ....................................... BRIEF
    - Situation (H₂ leak contained)
    - Landing time estimate
    - Preparation level required

12. Landing ............................................. PLAN
    - Nearest suitable airport
    - Emergency services requested
    - Consider H₂ handling capability

13. Passengers ....................................... BRIEF
    - Routine announcement style
    - Landing time estimate
    - Normal safety brief sufficient

---

### LAND AS SOON AS POSSIBLE

---

### NOTES

- H₂ leak can be explosive hazard
- Maximum ventilation reduces H₂ concentration
- Battery provides 30-45 minutes power (essential loads)
- Fire services should be H₂-capable at destination
- Do NOT attempt to open H₂ valve after closure

### CAOS SUPPORT

- CAOS provides leak location identification
- Fuel endurance calculation on battery
- Suitable airport recommendations
- Emergency checklist automation

## FUEL CELL FIRE

**Condition:** FIRE warning - Fuel Cell Bay

---

### MEMORY ITEMS (Immediate Action - Bold)

1. **FUEL CELL AFFECTED** .................. **IDENTIFY**
2. **AFFECTED FUEL CELL** ................. **SHUTDOWN**
3. **H₂ SUPPLY TO AFFECTED** ............ **CLOSE**
4. **FIRE EXTINGUISHER** ................... **ACTIVATE**

---

### END OF MEMORY ITEMS

---

### SUBSEQUENT ACTIONS

5. Fire Status ........................................ MONITOR
   - Fire warning extinguished?
   - CAOS fire status display

6. If fire persists:
   - Fire Extinguisher ............................. ACTIVATE AGAIN
   - If still burning ................................. H₂ MAIN VALVE CLOSE
   - Emergency Descent .......................... INITIATE
   - Declare ............................................ MAYDAY

7. If fire out:
   - Remaining Fuel Cell ......................... CHECK STATUS
   - Battery Assist .................................. ENGAGE AS NEEDED
   - Ventilation ...................................... MAXIMUM (2 min)

8. ATC .................................................. NOTIFY
   - Declare emergency
   - Request priority

9. Landing ............................................. PLAN IMMEDIATE
   - Nearest suitable airport
   - Emergency services required
   - Brief situation to ATC

10. Cabin Crew ...................................... ALERT
    - Prepare for possible emergency landing
    - Situation brief

---

### LAND IMMEDIATELY

---

### NOTES

- Fuel cell fire can be invisible (H₂ flames)
- Do not delay fire suppression
- Both fire extinguisher bottles available
- Emergency services critical
- Post-landing: wait for fire service clearance

---

## DUAL FUEL CELL FAILURE

**Condition:** Both fuel cell stacks failed

---

### MEMORY ITEMS (Immediate Action - Bold)

1. **BOTH STACKS** .............................. **SHUTDOWN**
2. **BATTERY EMERGENCY MODE** ...... **ACTIVATE**
3. **EMERGENCY DESCENT** ................ **INITIATE**
4. **MAYDAY** ...................................... **DECLARE**

---

### END OF MEMORY ITEMS

---

### SUBSEQUENT ACTIONS

5. Non-Essential Systems ...................... ALL OFF
   - Only essential for flight
   - Maximum battery conservation

6. Battery Status ................................... CHECK
   - State of charge
   - Estimated endurance (15-45 min)
   - Time to critical (15%)

7. Nearest Airport ................................. IDENTIFY
   - CAOS recommendation
   - Emergency capability
   - Distance vs battery endurance

8. ATC .................................................. INFORM
   - Battery time remaining
   - Cannot hold or delay
   - Immediate approach required

9. Approach ........................................... PLAN
   - Straight-in if possible
   - Minimize maneuvering
   - Configure late to save power

10. Cabin Crew ...................................... BRIEF
    - Emergency landing
    - Brace position likely
    - Time estimate

11. Passengers ...................................... PREPARE
    - Brace briefing
    - Safety demonstration

---

### LAND IMMEDIATELY - DO NOT DELAY

---

### CAUTIONS

- Battery depletion = total power loss
- No second chance with depleted battery
- Accept first available airport
- Do not attempt go-around without H₂ power
