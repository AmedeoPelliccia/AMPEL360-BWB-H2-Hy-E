# BWB Operations Engineering
**Component Code:** 02-00-00

## Overview

This document defines the operational engineering considerations specific to the Blended Wing Body (BWB) configuration of the AMPEL360 H₂ Hy-E Q100 aircraft. The BWB design requires unique operational procedures and engineering analysis.

---

## BWB Configuration Characteristics

### Aerodynamic Features

**Advantages:**
- 30% improved aerodynamic efficiency vs. conventional
- Entire body generates lift
- Lower induced drag coefficient
- Better lift-to-drag ratio (L/D = 20 vs 17 conventional)
- Reduced wetted area per passenger

**Unique Characteristics:**
- Non-linear lift curve
- Different stall characteristics
- Pitch control considerations
- Ground effect enhancement
- Wake turbulence characteristics

---

## Ground Operations

### Taxiing Procedures

**Special Considerations:**
- Wide fuselage requires special taxi paths
- Winglet clearance on taxiways
- Turning radius: 45 m minimum
- Reduced pilot visibility of wing edges
- Camera systems for ground navigation

**Taxi Speeds:**
- Normal taxi: 15 kt maximum
- Restricted taxi: 10 kt maximum
- Sharp turns: 5 kt maximum

### Parking and Gate Operations

**Gate Compatibility:**
- Requires modified gate geometry
- Standard gate width insufficient
- Specialized jetway configuration
- Ground service access points
- Under-body cargo access

**Parking Stand Requirements:**
- Minimum width: 80 m
- Nose-in parking (preferred)
- Parallel parking (alternative)
- Remote stand operations (capable)
