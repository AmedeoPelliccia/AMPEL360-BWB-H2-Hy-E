## 1. REFUELING SAFETY ZONES

### 1.1 Primary Safety Zone (50-meter radius)

**Purpose:** Protect personnel and assets during H2 refueling operations.

**Boundaries:**
- **Radius:** 50 meters from aircraft H2 refueling connection point
- **Volume:** Ground level to 10 meters above aircraft
- **Marking:** Orange cones with "H2 REFUELING - NO ENTRY" signage

**Restrictions:**
- ❌ No unauthorized personnel
- ❌ No ignition sources (smoking, open flames, hot work)
- ❌ No electronic devices (except explosion-proof certified)
- ❌ No non-essential vehicles
- ✅ Only certified H2 refueling personnel
- ✅ Emergency response team on standby
- ✅ Fire suppression equipment positioned

**Entry Requirements:**
- H2 safety certification
- Personal protective equipment (PPE):
  - Cryogenic gloves
  - Face shield
  - Safety shoes
  - Fire-resistant clothing
- Communication device (explosion-proof)
- H2 leak detector (portable)

### 1.2 Secondary Monitoring Zone (50-100 meter radius)

**Purpose:** Early detection and response staging area.

**Boundaries:**
- **Inner:** 50-meter radius
- **Outer:** 100-meter radius
- **Marking:** Yellow cones with "H2 OPERATIONS AREA" signage

**Restrictions:**
- ⚠️ Controlled access
- ⚠️ No smoking
- ⚠️ Emergency equipment staging
- ⚠️ Fire service standby position

**Activities Permitted:**
- Fire truck positioning
- Emergency equipment staging
- Medical standby
- Safety monitoring
- Airport operations coordination

### 1.3 Personnel Exclusion Areas

**High-Risk Zones:**
1. **Vent Stack Area** (10m radius)
   - Potential H2 release during venting
   - Invisible flame hazard
   - No personnel during refueling

2. **Connector Area** (3m radius)
   - Direct exposure to cryogenic H2
   - Leak potential during connection/disconnection
   - Refueling operator only

3. **Tank Access Area** (5m radius)
   - High-pressure system components
   - Structural hazard if failure
   - Certified personnel only

### 1.4 Fire Equipment Positioning

**Required Equipment Locations:**

**Position 1 - Upwind (Primary):**
- Mobile foam unit
- Dry chemical extinguisher (50 kg)
- Distance: 40-50 meters
- Ready for immediate deployment

**Position 2 - Perpendicular (Secondary):**
- Water fog system
- CO2 extinguisher bank
- Distance: 40-50 meters
- Backup response capability

**Position 3 - Emergency Vehicle:**
- Fire truck with foam capability
- Distance: 60 meters (outside primary zone)
- Quick response position

**Additional Equipment:**
- IR camera for invisible flame detection
- H2 leak detection equipment
- Emergency eyewash/shower station
- First aid kit (cryogenic exposure)

### 1.5 Emergency Access Routes

**Primary Access:**
- Direction: From main airport road
- Width: 8 meters minimum
- Clear of obstacles
- Marked with reflective paint

**Secondary Access:**
- Direction: From taxiway service road
- Width: 6 meters minimum
- Emergency use only

**Evacuation Routes:**
- Three designated routes radiating from aircraft
- Clear signage
- Assembly point at 200 meters

## 2. AIRCRAFT PARKING ZONES

### 2.1 H2 Aircraft Designated Parking Areas

**Zone Identification:**
- **Code:** H2-1 through H2-6 (six positions)
- **Marking:** Blue boundary lines with "H2 AIRCRAFT ONLY"
- **Surface:** Concrete, sealed, sloped for drainage
- **Lighting:** Enhanced illumination (200 lux minimum)

**Spacing Requirements:**
- **Minimum distance between H2 aircraft:** 100 meters
- **Distance from conventional aircraft:** 75 meters
- **Distance from buildings:** 50 meters
- **Distance from fuel trucks (conventional):** 100 meters

### 2.2 Minimum Spacing from Other Aircraft

**Safety Separation Matrix:**

| Adjacent Aircraft Type | Minimum Distance | Rationale |
|----------------------|------------------|-----------|
| Another H2 aircraft | 100m | Prevent cascading H2 incidents |
| Conventional jet | 75m | Protect from H2 hazards |
| Turboprop | 75m | Protect from H2 hazards |
| Regional aircraft | 50m | Lower fuel quantity |
| General aviation | 40m | Smaller aircraft, lower risk |

**Factors Considered:**
- H2 dispersion modeling (worst-case leak)
- Fire/explosion blast radius
- Emergency response access
- Evacuation clearances
- Wind patterns at airport

### 2.3 Ventilation Considerations

**Natural Ventilation:**
- Open-air parking only (no enclosed hangars)
- Minimum 5 km/h wind for effective dispersion
- Wind direction monitored continuously
- Parking orientation considers prevailing winds

**Artificial Ventilation (if enclosed temporarily):**
- Forced ventilation: 50 air changes per hour minimum
- Exhaust at high points (H2 rises)
- Explosion-proof fans and motors
- Backup power for ventilation systems
- H2 detection interlocked with ventilation

**Enclosed Hangar Restrictions:**
- Generally not permitted with H2 onboard
- Special approval required
- Enhanced detection and ventilation
- 24/7 monitoring
- Emergency procedures established

### 2.4 Monitoring Equipment Placement

**Fixed H2 Sensors:**
- **Quantity:** 4 sensors per parking position
- **Locations:**
  - Under wing (near tanks): 2 sensors
  - Vent stack area: 1 sensor
  - Downwind position: 1 sensor
- **Type:** Electrochemical, 0.1% LEL sensitivity
- **Alarm levels:**
  - Advisory: 0.5% LEL
  - Caution: 1.0% LEL
  - Warning: 2.5% LEL
- **Communication:** Wireless to Operations Center

**Weather Monitoring:**
- Wind speed and direction
- Temperature and humidity
- Barometric pressure
- Lightning detection
- Data logged continuously

**Video Surveillance:**
- 360° coverage of each parking position
- Infrared cameras (night/invisible flame detection)
- Recording retained 90 days
- Real-time monitoring in Operations Center

## 3. MAINTENANCE ZONES

### 3.1 H2 System Maintenance Areas

**Outdoor Maintenance Pad:**
- **Location:** Dedicated area, 100m from buildings
- **Surface:** Non-sparking, grounded
- **Equipment:** Explosion-proof power, lighting, tools
- **Access:** Controlled, certified personnel only

**Permitted Activities:**
- H2 tank inspections (external)
- Leak detector testing and calibration
- Ventilation system maintenance
- Sensor replacement
- Line pressure testing (with H2 removed/purged)

**Prohibited Activities:**
- Hot work (welding, grinding)
- H2 tank internal work (requires special facility)
- Non-certified personnel access
- Smoking or open flames

### 3.2 Tool and Equipment Staging Areas

**Tool Staging Zone:**
- **Location:** Upwind, 30m from aircraft
- **Equipment Type:** Non-sparking tools only
  - Brass, bronze, or aluminum tools
  - Special coated steel tools
  - Explosion-proof power tools
- **Storage:** Organized shadow boards
- **Accountability:** Tool control system (prevent FOD)

**H2-Specific Equipment:**
- Cryogenic-rated pressure gauges
- H2 leak detection devices (portable)
- Special torque wrenches (H2 connections)
- PPE (cryogenic gloves, face shields)
- Emergency response equipment

**Calibration:**
- All measurement equipment calibrated
- Calibration records maintained
- Traceable to national standards

### 3.3 Emergency Equipment Locations

**Fire Suppression:**
- Dry chemical extinguisher: 20m intervals
- Foam cart: 50m from aircraft
- Fire blanket: Tool staging area
- Emergency shutdown station: Main maintenance area

**First Aid:**
- Emergency eyewash/shower: 15m maximum travel
- Cryogenic exposure kit: Tool staging area
- First aid cabinet: Main maintenance building
- AED: Main maintenance building

**Communication:**
- Emergency phone: Maintenance area entrance
- Two-way radios: All personnel
- Emergency alarm: Manual pull stations

**Spill Response:**
- Absorbent materials (for secondary fluids)
- Containment booms
- Neutralizing agents (for coolants, etc.)
- Disposal containers

### 3.4 Confined Space Entry Points

**Identified Confined Spaces:**
1. **H2 Tank Void Spaces**
   - Entry prohibited unless defueled and purged
   - Requires confined space permit
   - Continuous atmosphere monitoring
   - Retrieval equipment

2. **Fuel Cell Compartments**
   - Ventilation before entry
   - H2 detection verification (<10% LEL)
   - Buddy system required

**Entry Procedures:**
- Confined space permit system
- Pre-entry atmosphere testing:
  - Oxygen: 19.5-23.5%
  - H2: <10% LEL
  - Carbon monoxide: <35 ppm
- Continuous monitoring during work
- Emergency rescue plan
- Retrieval equipment set up before entry

## 4. EMERGENCY RESPONSE ZONES

### 4.1 Fire Service Approach Routes

**Primary Approach (Upwind):**
- **Route:** Main airport road to H2 parking area
- **Width:** 10 meters
- **Access Time:** Target <3 minutes
- **Positioning:** 60m from incident, upwind
- **Equipment:** Full foam capability, 2000 L/min

**Secondary Approach (Lateral):**
- **Route:** Taxiway service road
- **Width:** 8 meters
- **Use:** Perpendicular attack or primary blocked
- **Positioning:** 60m from incident, lateral

**Tertiary Approach (Downwind - Emergency Only):**
- **Route:** Emergency access road
- **Width:** 6 meters
- **Use:** Rescue operations only
- **Risk:** Downwind position (H2 exposure)
- **PPE:** Full protective equipment required

**Approach Considerations:**
- Wind direction critical (approach upwind)
- Invisible flame awareness
- IR camera deployment for flame detection
- Communication with aircraft crew
- Coordinate with airport operations

### 4.2 Evacuation Assembly Points

**Primary Assembly Point:**
- **Location:** 200m north of H2 parking area
- **Marking:** Large "ASSEMBLY POINT" sign, green flag
- **Capacity:** 200 persons
- **Facilities:** Emergency lighting, shelter
- **Upwind:** Of prevailing wind from H2 area

**Secondary Assembly Point:**
- **Location:** 200m west of H2 parking area
- **Use:** If primary downwind or inaccessible
- **Capacity:** 200 persons

**Assembly Point Features:**
- Clear line of sight to incident
- Protected from blast effects
- Accessible by emergency vehicles
- Communication capability
- Accountability system (check-in roster)

**Accountability Procedures:**
1. Report to assembly point immediately
2. Check in with designated personnel
3. Report missing persons
4. Provide information on incident
5. Await further instructions

### 4.3 Incident Command Post Location

**Primary ICP Location:**
- **Distance:** 150m from incident
- **Position:** Upwind, elevated if possible
- **Facilities:**
  - Mobile command trailer
  - Communication equipment (multi-channel radios)
  - Weather monitoring display
  - Airport diagrams and hazmat data
  - Media briefing area (separate)

**ICP Staffing:**
- Incident Commander (Airport Fire Chief or designee)
- Airport Operations representative
- H2 Safety Specialist (AMPEL360)
- Law enforcement
- Medical coordinator
- Public information officer

**Communication Systems:**
- Direct line to airport tower
- Emergency services radio net
- Company operations center
- Local emergency management
- Media liaison capability

### 4.4 Medical Triage Areas

**Triage Point Location:**
- **Distance:** 100m from incident (safe zone)
- **Position:** Upwind, accessible to ambulances
- **Marking:** Red cross or star of life flags

**Triage Categories:**
- **Immediate (Red):** Life-threatening, immediate treatment
- **Delayed (Yellow):** Serious but stable
- **Minor (Green):** Walking wounded
- **Deceased (Black):** No vital signs

**Medical Equipment:**
- Triage tags and marking system
- Advanced trauma kits
- Burn treatment supplies
- Cryogenic exposure treatment
- Spinal immobilization equipment
- Oxygen and airway equipment

**Medical Transport:**
- Ambulance staging: 100m from triage
- Helicopter landing zone: Designated area, 300m
- Hospital notifications: Pre-arranged protocols
- Specialized burns center: Identified and notified

## 5. LEAK RESPONSE ZONES

### 5.1 Detection Zone Coverage

**Fixed Detection System:**
- **Primary Coverage:** All H2 aircraft parking positions
- **Sensor Density:** 4 sensors per position
- **Detection Range:** 0.1% to 100% LEL
- **Response Time:** <2 seconds to alert

**Sensor Network Map:**

```
    [Sensor 3]           [Sensor 4]
```

**Mobile Detection:**
- Portable H2 detectors: 2 units per response team
- Vehicle-mounted detectors: Fire trucks
- Handheld detectors: All H2 personnel

### 5.2 Dispersion Modeling Results

**Leak Scenarios Modeled:**

**Scenario 1: Minor Leak (0.1 kg/s)**
- **H2 Concentration:**
  - 5m from source: 10% LEL
  - 10m from source: 2% LEL
  - 20m from source: <1% LEL (safe)
- **Dispersion Time:** 2-5 minutes to safe levels (wind dependent)
- **Recommended Action:** Monitor, isolate source if possible

**Scenario 2: Moderate Leak (1.0 kg/s)**
- **H2 Concentration:**
  - 10m from source: 50% LEL
  - 20m from source: 10% LEL
  - 50m from source: <1% LEL (safe)
- **Dispersion Time:** 5-15 minutes
- **Recommended Action:** Evacuate 50m radius, eliminate ignition sources

**Scenario 3: Major Leak (5.0 kg/s) - Vent System Failure**
- **H2 Concentration:**
  - 20m from source: 100% LEL (explosive)
  - 50m from source: 25% LEL
  - 100m from source: <5% LEL
- **Dispersion Time:** 15-30 minutes
- **Recommended Action:** Evacuate 100m radius, emergency response

**Factors Affecting Dispersion:**
- Wind speed: Higher = faster dispersion
- Atmospheric stability: Unstable = faster mixing
- Temperature: Warmer = faster rise and dispersion
- Obstacles: Buildings, aircraft slow dispersion

### 5.3 Ignition Source Control Areas

**Hot Work Restrictions:**
- **Zone 1 (0-100m from H2 aircraft):** No hot work permitted
- **Zone 2 (100-200m):** Permitted with H2 safety approval only
- **Zone 3 (>200m):** Normal hot work permit procedures

**Electrical Equipment Standards:**
- **Zone 1:** Explosion-proof, Class I, Division 1
- **Zone 2:** Explosion-proof or intrinsically safe
- **Zone 3:** Standard equipment acceptable

**Vehicle Requirements (Zone 1):**
- Engine shut off when parked
- No idling vehicles
- Electric vehicles: Approved intrinsically safe designs
- Special H2-rated ground support equipment

**Prohibited Items (Zone 1):**
- Cell phones (except explosion-proof certified)
- Cameras with batteries (except EX-rated)
- Radios (except explosion-proof certified)
- Lighters, matches
- Static-generating materials

### 5.4 Safe Approach Corridors

**Upwind Approach Corridor:**
- **Primary Route:** For all personnel and vehicles
- **Width:** 15 meters
- **Approach Speed:** Maximum 15 km/h
- **PPE Required:** H2 safety gear

**Lateral Approach Corridors (2):**
- **Use:** When upwind not available
- **Width:** 10 meters
- **Caution:** Moderate H2 exposure possible
- **Detection:** Portable H2 detectors required

**Downwind Approach:**
- **Status:** Emergency use only
- **Restriction:** Rescue operations priority
- **PPE:** Full protective gear, SCBA
- **Detection:** Continuous H2 monitoring

**Corridor Markings:**
- Painted lines: Yellow for safe, Red for caution
- Signage: "UPWIND SAFE APPROACH"
- Wind socks: Visible from all angles
- Lighting: Enhanced for night operations

## 6. ZONE COORDINATION AND MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Zone Activation Procedures

**Normal Operations:**
- H2 safety zones inactive
- Standard airport operations

**Refueling Operations:**
- Primary zone (50m) activated
- Personnel briefed and positioned
- Fire equipment staged
- Airport operations notified

**Leak Detected:**
- Zones established per leak severity
- Evacuation initiated
- Emergency services activated
- Incident command established

**Fire Event:**
- Maximum zones established (100m+)
- Full evacuation
- Emergency response implemented
- External mutual aid requested

### 6.2 Communication Systems

**Primary:**
- Airport emergency frequency
- H2 operations tactical channel
- Fire service command channel

**Secondary:**
- Cell phone (emergency contacts list)
- Airport operations landline
- Emergency notification system

**Backup:**
- Runner system (if communications fail)
- Visual signals (flags, lights)
- Aircraft ACARS/SATCOM

### 6.3 Training and Drills

**Required Training:**
- All airport personnel: H2 awareness (annual)
- H2 operations personnel: Comprehensive (initial + annual)
- Fire service: H2 firefighting (initial + semi-annual)
- Airport operations: Zone management (annual)

**Drill Schedule:**
- Tabletop exercise: Quarterly
- Functional drill: Semi-annual
- Full-scale exercise: Annual
