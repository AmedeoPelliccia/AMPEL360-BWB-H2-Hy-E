## 1. Executive Summary

This document outlines the comprehensive certification strategy for the AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E Q100 INTEGRA aircraft, addressing the unique challenges of:
- Blended Wing Body (BWB) configuration
- Hydrogen (H₂) fuel cell propulsion system
- CAOS (Cognitive Aviation Operations System) AI integration
- Carbon-negative operations with CO₂ capture

### 1.1 Certification Basis
- **Primary Authority:** EASA (European Union Aviation Safety Agency)
- **Secondary Authority:** FAA (Federal Aviation Administration)
- **Target:** CS-25 / FAR Part 25 Type Certificate
- **Special Conditions Required:** Yes (BWB, H₂, AI systems)

---

## 2. Certification Approach

### 2.1 Concurrent Certification Strategy
- **EASA:** Primary certification authority (lead)
- **FAA:** Concurrent certification for global market access
- **Timeline:** 2026-2028 (Initial consultation to TC approval)

### 2.2 Key Certification Challenges

#### 2.2.1 BWB Configuration
- Novel aircraft configuration requiring special conditions
- CS-25.841 pressurized cabin design for BWB geometry
- Emergency evacuation validation for wide-body layout
- Ground clearance and handling characteristics

#### 2.2.2 Hydrogen Propulsion System
- First commercial aircraft with hydrogen fuel cells
- ISO 19881 compliance for H₂ fuel containers
- SAE J2719 hydrogen fuel quality standards
- NFPA 2 Hydrogen Technologies Code compliance
- Cryogenic system safety certification
- Refueling procedures and ground safety

#### 2.2.3 CAOS AI System
- EU AI Act compliance (high-risk AI system)
- EASA AI Roadmap alignment
- DO-178C software certification level
- Human-machine interface validation
- Safety assessment and transparency requirements

## 3. Regulatory Framework

### 3.1 Applicable Regulations

#### EASA Certification Basis
- CS-25 (Large Aeroplanes)
- CS-25.1581 (General - Operations Manual)
- CS-25.1585 (Operating Procedures)
- CS-25.841 (Pressurized Cabins)
- CS-25.1309 (Equipment, Systems, and Installations)
- Special Conditions for BWB configuration
- Special Conditions for H₂ propulsion systems
- Special Conditions for AI/CAOS integration

#### FAA Certification Basis
- 14 CFR Part 25 (Airworthiness Standards)
- FAR 25.1581 (Operations Manual)
- FAR 25.1585 (Operating Procedures)
- Issue Papers for H₂ propulsion
- Issue Papers for BWB design
- Issue Papers for CAOS AI system

#### H₂-Specific Standards
- ISO 19881:2018 (Gaseous hydrogen - Land vehicle fuel containers)
- SAE J2719 (Hydrogen Fuel Quality)
- NFPA 2:2020 (Hydrogen Technologies Code)
- EN 17127 (Hydrogen refueling protocols)

#### AI-Specific Regulations
- EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689)
- EASA AI Roadmap 2.0
- DO-178C (Software Considerations in Airborne Systems)
- DO-254 (Design Assurance for Airborne Electronic Hardware)

## 6. Test and Validation Program

### 6.1 Ground Testing
- **H₂ System Tests:** Refueling, leak detection, emergency procedures
- **Weight & Balance:** BWB-specific CG envelope validation
- **Emergency Equipment:** Evacuation systems, fire suppression
- **Environmental:** Noise testing, emissions validation

### 6.2 Flight Testing
- **Performance:** Range, speed, altitude capabilities
- **H₂ System:** In-flight fuel cell operation, thermal management
- **CAOS:** AI system validation, human-machine interface
- **Handling:** BWB flight characteristics, stability and control

### 6.3 Analysis Evidence
- **Performance Analysis:** Aerodynamic calculations, fuel efficiency
- **Safety Analysis:** FMEA, FHA, system safety assessments
- **Human Factors:** Workload analysis, situational awareness
