# Maintenance Program
## AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E Q100

**Document:** MP-AMP-001  
**Revision:** 1  
**Date:** 2025-11-05  
**Regulatory Basis:** EASA Part-M, FAA Part 121 Appendix H

---

## 1. Overview

This Maintenance Program defines the scheduled maintenance requirements for the AMPEL360 aircraft.

## 2. Maintenance Philosophy

The maintenance program combines:
- Traditional time/cycle-based maintenance
- CAOS predictive maintenance
- Condition monitoring
- Reliability-centered maintenance (RCM)

## 3. Maintenance Checks

### Pre-Flight Check
- **Interval:** Before each flight
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Tasks:** Visual inspection, H₂ quantity check, CAOS status

### Daily Check
- **Interval:** Every 24 hours
- **Duration:** 2 hours
- **Tasks:** Detailed inspection, system tests, fluid levels

### A-Check (Weekly)
- **Interval:** 500 FH or 90 days
- **Duration:** 10 hours
- **Tasks:** Detailed inspections, lubrication, adjustments

### C-Check (Heavy)
- **Interval:** 3,000 FH or 18 months
- **Duration:** 1 week
- **Tasks:** Comprehensive inspection, component overhauls

### D-Check (Major)
- **Interval:** 25,000 FH or 5 years
- **Duration:** 4 weeks
- **Tasks:** Complete disassembly, structural inspection, major overhauls

## 4. H₂ System Maintenance

### Specialized Tasks
- Cryogenic tank inspection: Every C-Check
- Pressure relief device test: Annual
- Leak detection system test: Every A-Check
- Vacuum insulation test: Every D-Check

## 5. CAOS-Generated Tasks

Condition-based tasks generated by:
- Fuel cell degradation monitoring
- H₂ system health monitoring
- Structural health monitoring
- Fleet-wide anomaly detection

## 6. Maintenance Organization

- **Base Maintenance:** Heavy checks (C, D)
- **Line Maintenance:** Pre-flight, Daily, A-checks
- **Component Maintenance:** Off-wing repairs and overhauls
