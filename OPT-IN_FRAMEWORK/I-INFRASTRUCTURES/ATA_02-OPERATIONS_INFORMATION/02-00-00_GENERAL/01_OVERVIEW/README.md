## Key AMPEL360 Differentiators

### Unique Operational Aspects

1. **Blended Wing Body (BWB) Configuration**
   - Non-conventional weight and balance
   - Unique handling characteristics
   - Wide CG range (15% MAC typical)
   - Distributed passenger seating effects

2. **Hydrogen Fuel System**
   - Cryogenic fuel handling (-253°C)
   - Low density fuel (70.8 kg/m³)
   - Significant weight change during flight
   - Specialized refueling procedures

3. **Fuel Cell Propulsion**
   - Electric propulsion system
   - 4 × 2.5 MW fuel cell stacks
   - 55-60% efficiency
   - Different performance characteristics

4. **CO₂ Capture System**
   - Active carbon capture during flight
   - Weight and balance implications
   - Operational procedures

5. **CAOS Integration**
   - AI-assisted operations
   - Real-time optimization
   - Digital twin connectivity
   - Predictive analytics
   - Fleet learning

## Change Control

### Version Numbering
- **Major.Minor.Patch** (e.g., 1.0.0)
- Major: Significant operational changes
- Minor: Improvements, clarifications
- Patch: Corrections, typos

### Approval Process
1. **Draft**: Technical writer creates
2. **Review**: Subject matter expert review
3. **Approval**: Chief Pilot + Operations Manager
4. **Release**: Configuration control board
5. **Distribution**: All affected personnel notified

### Temporary Revisions
- Issued for urgent operational changes
- Valid for maximum 90 days
- Must be incorporated into permanent revision

## Training Requirements

### Initial Training
- **Flight Crew**: 40 hours (including BWB, H2, CAOS)
- **Cabin Crew**: 16 hours (including H2 safety)
- **Dispatch**: 24 hours (including H2 planning)
- **Ground Operations**: 16 hours (including H2 refueling)

### Recurrent Training
- **Annual**: All operational personnel
- **Quarterly**: H2 safety refresher
- **As Required**: After significant procedure changes
