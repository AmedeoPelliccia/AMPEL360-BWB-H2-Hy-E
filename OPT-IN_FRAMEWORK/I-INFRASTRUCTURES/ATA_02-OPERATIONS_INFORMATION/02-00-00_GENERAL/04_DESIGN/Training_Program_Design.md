# Training Program Design

**Document ID:** AMPEL360-02-00-00-DES-TRN  
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Purpose

Defines the training program design for AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E Q100 aircraft operations, ensuring all personnel are properly trained and qualified.

## Training Philosophy

**Competency-Based:**
- Focus on demonstrated competencies
- Performance-based assessment
- Scenario-based training
- Continuous evaluation

**Progressive:**
- Building block approach
- Prerequisites enforced
- Increasing complexity
- Regular reinforcement

**Practical:**
- Realistic scenarios
- Hands-on practice
- Real-world applications
- Operational relevance

## Flight Crew Training

### Initial Type Rating

**Ground School (80 hours):**

**Module 1: Aircraft General (12 hours)**
- BWB configuration overview
- Systems architecture
- Performance characteristics
- Limitations

**Module 2: Airframe & Systems (20 hours)**
- BWB-specific structure
- Flight controls
- Hydraulics, pneumatics
- Landing gear

**Module 3: H₂ & Power Systems (16 hours)**
- H₂ properties and hazards
- Fuel cell operation
- H₂ fuel system
- Electrical system
- Emergency procedures

**Module 4: CAOS System (12 hours)**
- System architecture
- Advisory interpretation
- Override procedures
- Trust calibration
- Failure modes

**Module 5: Flight Operations (12 hours)**
- Normal procedures
- Abnormal procedures
- Emergency procedures
- Performance planning
- Weight and balance

**Module 6: Human Factors (8 hours)**
- Crew resource management
- Workload management
- Decision making
- Error management
- Fatigue management

**Simulator Training (40 hours):**

**Phase 1: Normal Operations (16 hours)**
- Pre-flight and start
- Taxi, takeoff, climb
- Cruise operations
- Descent, approach, landing
- BWB handling characteristics
- CAOS interaction

**Phase 2: Abnormal Operations (12 hours)**
- System failures
- H₂ system issues
- Fuel cell degradation
- CAOS degraded mode
- Diversion procedures

**Phase 3: Emergency Operations (12 hours)**
- H₂ leak major/minor
- Fuel cell failure
- Multiple system failures
- Emergency landing
- Evacuation procedures
- Unusual attitudes

**Assessment:**
- Written examination (90% pass)
- Simulator check ride
- Line operational evaluation (25 hours)

### Recurrent Training (Annual)

**Ground School (16 hours):**
- Systems review (4 hours)
- H₂ operations review (4 hours)
- CAOS updates (2 hours)
- Emergency procedures (4 hours)
- Regulatory updates (2 hours)

**Simulator (8 hours):**
- Emergency procedures emphasis
- Rotating scenarios
- New procedures
- Performance assessment

### Transition Training

**For Pilots with BWB Experience:**
- Differences course: 40 hours
- Focus on H₂ and CAOS systems

**For Pilots with H₂ Experience:**
- Differences course: 40 hours
- Focus on BWB characteristics
