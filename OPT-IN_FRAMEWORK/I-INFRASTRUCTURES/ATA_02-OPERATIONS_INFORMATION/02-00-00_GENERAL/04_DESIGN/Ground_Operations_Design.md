# Ground Operations Design

**Document ID:** AMPEL360-02-00-00-DES-GND  
**Version:** 1.0.0

## Purpose

Defines ground operations procedures and requirements for the AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E Q100 aircraft, with emphasis on H₂ safety, efficient turnarounds, and CAOS integration.

## Ground Operations Philosophy

**Safety First:**
- H₂ safety procedures paramount
- Safety zones established and maintained
- Trained personnel only
- Continuous monitoring

**Efficiency:**
- Standard turnaround time: 45 minutes
- CAOS optimization of ground operations
- Parallel task execution where safe
- Digital coordination

**Environmental:**
- Zero emissions during ground operations
- Minimal ground power usage
- Noise reduction
- Sustainable practices

## Pre-Flight Operations

### Aircraft Positioning

**Parking Stand:**
- H₂-approved parking positions only
- Ventilation requirements verified
- Safety zone establishment
- Ground equipment positioning

**External Inspection:**
- Standard walk-around inspection
- H₂ system external checks
- Fuel cell system access verification
- CAOS ground mode activation

### Servicing

**H₂ Refueling:**
1. Safety zone establishment (30m radius)
2. Personnel certification verification
3. Equipment inspection and connection
4. Continuous monitoring during refueling
5. System verification after completion
6. Safety zone clearance

**Electrical Service:**
- Fuel cell ground power available
- External GPU connection optional
- Battery charging if required
- CAOS power management

**Potable Water / Waste:**
- Standard servicing procedures
- CAOS service tracking
- Capacity monitoring
- Service verification

## H₂ Refueling Procedures

### Safety Requirements

**Safety Zone:**
- 30-meter radius minimum
- Clear of ignition sources
- Posted and monitored
- Access controlled

**Personnel Requirements:**
- H₂ handling certification required
- PPE mandatory (specified equipment)
- Two-person minimum team
- Emergency response trained

**Equipment Requirements:**
- Certified H₂ refueling equipment
- Leak detection active
- Bonding/grounding verified
- Emergency shutdown accessible

### Refueling Process

**Pre-Refueling:**
1. Aircraft systems secured
2. Safety zone established
3. Equipment inspection complete
4. Weather check (wind, lightning)
5. Personnel briefing complete
6. Go/No-Go decision

**During Refueling:**
1. Continuous monitoring (pressure, temperature, leaks)
2. CAOS system oversight
3. Communication maintained
4. Emergency procedures ready
5. Flow rate control
6. Quantity monitoring

**Post-Refueling:**
1. System verification
2. Leak check complete
3. Equipment disconnection and securing
4. Safety zone clearance
5. Documentation complete
6. CAOS data logging

## Turnaround Operations

### Standard Turnaround (45 minutes)

**Timeline:**
```
T+0:  Aircraft arrival, parking
T+5:  Passenger deplaning begins
T+10: Refueling begins (if required)
T+15: Cabin cleaning/servicing
T+20: Passenger boarding begins
T+30: Refueling complete (if performed)
T+35: Loading complete, doors closed
T+40: Pre-departure checks
T+45: Pushback/taxi clearance
```

**CAOS Optimization:**
- Real-time task coordination
- Resource allocation optimization
- Delay prediction and mitigation
- Documentation automation

### Quick Turnaround (30 minutes)

**Conditions:**
- No refueling required
- Minimal passenger changes
- No cargo loading/unloading
- No maintenance actions

**CAOS Support:**
- Parallel task execution
- Real-time monitoring
- Crew coordination
- Time-critical alerts

## Emergency Ground Procedures

### H₂ Leak on Ground

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Activate emergency shutdown
2. Evacuate safety zone
3. Alert emergency services
4. Establish extended safety perimeter
5. Monitor situation
6. Coordinate response

**CAOS Response:**
- Automatic alert generation
- Emergency services notification
- Leak location identification
- Response guidance

### Ground Fire

**Response:**
1. Activate fire suppression
2. Evacuate personnel
3. Alert fire services
4. H₂ system isolation
5. Coordinate with emergency services

### Ground Collision/Damage

**Procedure:**
1. Assess damage
2. Check H₂ system integrity
3. Establish safety zone if needed
4. Document damage (CAOS photo capture)
5. Engineering assessment
