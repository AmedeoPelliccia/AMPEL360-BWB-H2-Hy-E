# 95-30-00-002 Circularity Strategy and Objectives

**Document ID:** 95-30-00-002  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2025-11-17  
**Status:** ACTIVE

## 1. Executive Summary

This document defines the strategic objectives and implementation roadmap for circularity across the AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E aircraft program, establishing measurable targets and governance framework.

## 2. Strategic Objectives

### 2.1 Primary Objectives

#### O1: Achieve Carbon-Negative Flight Operations
- **Target**: Net -5 kg CO₂ per flight by 2028
- **Method**: Solid-state CO₂ battery system integrated with propulsion and energy systems
- **Measurement**: Real-time monitoring via CAOS AI system

#### O2: Maximize Energy Circularity
- **Target**: 85% energy recovery rate across all systems
- **Method**: Thermal recovery loops, regenerative systems, optimized load sharing
- **Measurement**: Energy balance tracking per flight phase

#### O3: Implement Zero-Waste Fluid Systems
- **Target**: <1% fluid loss across hydraulic, pneumatic, and water systems
- **Method**: Closed-loop designs, predictive leak detection, advanced sealing
- **Measurement**: Fluid balance monitoring per flight and maintenance cycle

#### O4: Enable Component Second Life
- **Target**: 60% of components entering second-life applications by 2030
- **Method**: Design for disassembly, refurbishment protocols, market development
- **Measurement**: Component tracking through Digital Product Passport

### 2.2 Secondary Objectives

#### O5: Optimize Material Selection
- **Target**: 70% recyclable or renewable materials by weight
- **Method**: Material database, supplier qualification, design guidelines
- **Measurement**: Material composition analysis per aircraft

#### O6: Reduce Operational Waste
- **Target**: 90% waste diversion from landfill
- **Method**: Waste segregation protocols, recycling partnerships, composting
- **Measurement**: Waste audit per maintenance cycle

## 3. Implementation Strategy

### 3.1 Design Phase (2024-2025)

#### 3.1.1 System-Level Integration
- Establish circular design principles in all ATA chapters
- Define interface requirements for energy and material loops
- Create Digital Product Passport data model

#### 3.1.2 Component-Level Specification
- Develop circularity requirements for all major components
- Establish supplier qualification criteria for circular materials
- Define end-of-life disassembly procedures

### 3.2 Development Phase (2026-2027)

#### 3.2.1 Prototype Validation
- Test energy recovery systems in ground-based rigs
- Validate fluid loop performance under operational conditions
- Demonstrate component disassembly and refurbishment

#### 3.2.2 Digital System Integration
- Implement real-time monitoring for all circular loops
- Integrate DPP data collection with aircraft systems
- Deploy CAOS AI circularity optimization algorithms

### 3.3 Certification Phase (2028)

#### 3.3.1 Regulatory Compliance
- Demonstrate compliance with EU Digital Product Passport requirements
- Obtain EASA approval for circular system architectures
- Document lifecycle environmental impact (LCA)

#### 3.3.2 Performance Validation
- Validate all circularity KPIs against targets
- Complete flight test program with circularity monitoring
- Publish sustainability certification reports

### 3.4 Operations Phase (2029+)

#### 3.4.1 Continuous Improvement
- Analyze operational data to identify optimization opportunities
- Update circular design guidelines based on field experience
- Expand component second-life programs

#### 3.4.2 Fleet-Level Management
- Implement fleet-wide circularity monitoring
- Develop circular maintenance programs
- Create component exchange and refurbishment network

## 4. Cross-ATA Integration Points

### 4.1 Energy Systems (ATA 24, 28, 49, 70, 80)
- **Integration**: Unified energy management system with recovery loops
- **Key Loops**: Fuel cell waste heat → ECS, APU → main power grid, battery charge optimization
- **Governance**: Energy Systems Integration Board

### 4.2 Fluid Systems (ATA 29, 36, 38)
- **Integration**: Closed-loop fluid management with condition monitoring
- **Key Loops**: Hydraulic accumulator recovery, pneumatic heat recovery, water reclamation
- **Governance**: Fluid Systems Working Group

### 4.3 Environmental Systems (ATA 21, 26, 30)
- **Integration**: Thermal management and air quality loops
- **Key Loops**: Heat recovery, air recirculation, ice protection optimization
- **Governance**: Environmental Control Systems Team

## 6. Risk Management

### 6.1 Technical Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| Energy recovery efficiency below target | High | Multiple redundant recovery paths, advanced materials |
| Fluid system leak rates exceed target | Medium | Triple-redundant sealing, predictive monitoring |
| Component refurbishment economics unfavorable | Medium | Design for standardization, partnerships |
| Digital monitoring system integration delays | Low | Modular deployment, phased rollout |

### 6.2 Regulatory Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| DPP requirements change | Medium | Flexible data model, regular engagement with regulators |
| New circularity regulations | Low | Proactive compliance, exceed current standards |
| Certification delays | High | Early engagement with EASA, comprehensive documentation |

## 7. Success Metrics

### 7.1 Short-Term (2025-2026)
- Complete circular design for all major systems
- Establish Digital Product Passport data structure
- Validate energy recovery prototypes at >80% efficiency

### 7.2 Medium-Term (2027-2028)
- Achieve Type Certificate with circularity features
- Demonstrate carbon-negative flight in test program
- Establish component refurbishment partnerships

### 7.3 Long-Term (2029+)
- Maintain -5 kg CO₂ per flight in operations
- Achieve 60% component second-life rate
- Become industry reference for circular aircraft design
