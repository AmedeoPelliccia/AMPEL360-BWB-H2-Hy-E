## 1. Introduction

This document provides a comprehensive overview of circularity principles and implementations for the Fuel System (Hydrogen) in the AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E aircraft.

## 2. System Description

The Fuel System (Hydrogen) plays a critical role in the overall aircraft circularity strategy by focusing on boil-off management, return loops, minimal venting.

## 3. Circularity Objectives

### 3.1 Primary Objectives
- **Objective 1**: H₂ Boil-off Recovery: 92% recovery target
- **Objective 2**: Cryogenic Cooling Applications: 70% efficiency
- **Objective 3**: Minimal Venting Strategy: <1% loss

### 3.2 Cross-System Integration

The Fuel System (Hydrogen) circularity implementation interfaces with:
- ATA-70 (Propulsion)
- ATA-21 (ECS)
- ATA-38 (Water)

## 4. Circular Loops Overview

### 4.1 Material Flows
The system manages material flows to minimize waste and maximize reuse through closed-loop designs.

### 4.2 Energy Flows
Energy recovery and optimization are implemented throughout the system to minimize overall aircraft energy consumption.

### 4.3 Information Flows
All circularity data is captured in the Digital Product Passport (DPP) for lifecycle tracking and optimization.

## 5. Key Performance Indicators

- KPI-005: H₂ Boil-off Recovery Rate
