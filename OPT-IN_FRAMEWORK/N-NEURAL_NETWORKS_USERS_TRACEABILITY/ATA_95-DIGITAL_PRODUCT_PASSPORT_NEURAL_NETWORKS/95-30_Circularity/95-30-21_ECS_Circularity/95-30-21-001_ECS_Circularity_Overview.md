## 1. Introduction

This document provides a comprehensive overview of circularity principles and implementations for the Environmental Control System in the AMPEL360 BWB H₂ Hy-E aircraft.

## 2. System Description

The Environmental Control System plays a critical role in the overall aircraft circularity strategy by focusing on air quality loops, thermal recovery, filter management.

## 3. Circularity Objectives

### 3.1 Primary Objectives
- **Objective 1**: Cabin Air Recirculation: 90% recirculation efficiency target
- **Objective 2**: Fuel Cell Waste Heat → Cabin Heating: 75% thermal recovery target
- **Objective 3**: Filter Lifecycle Optimization: 95% design life utilization target

### 3.2 Cross-System Integration

The Environmental Control System circularity implementation interfaces with:
- ATA-70 (Propulsion)
- ATA-49 (APU)
- ATA-36 (Pneumatic)

## 4. Circular Loops Overview

### 4.1 Material Flows
The system manages material flows to minimize waste and maximize reuse through closed-loop designs.

### 4.2 Energy Flows
Energy recovery and optimization are implemented throughout the system to minimize overall aircraft energy consumption.

### 4.3 Information Flows
All circularity data is captured in the Digital Product Passport (DPP) for lifecycle tracking and optimization.

## 5. Key Performance Indicators

- KPI-002: Thermal Recovery Efficiency
- KPI-008: Air Recirculation Efficiency
- KPI-012: Filter Lifecycle Utilization
